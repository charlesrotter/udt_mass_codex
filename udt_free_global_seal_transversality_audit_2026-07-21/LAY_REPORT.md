# Lay report — varying the edge of the universe

Treating the edge as movable was a good idea. It adds a real equation—but not yet enough equations.

Imagine a guitar string whose far endpoint can slide. Varying the string tells you its shape.
Varying the endpoint also tells you that the total energy must be stationary when the endpoint
moves. But the answer depends on what is attached to the endpoint: nothing, a spring, a weight, or a
hinge. Declaring the endpoint movable does not tell you which attachment exists.

The UDT result is analogous. A movable global cell size gives one additional balance equation. A
fully shape-changing boundary gives one balance equation at every boundary point. But those
equations depend on the still-unknown boundary functional, and they do not tell all the metric's
angular and directional components what to do.

We also found an important correction. At a fixed seal, UDT says `phi=0` and permitted variations
preserve that value. If the seal itself moves, the field value at the old location need not remain
zero. Instead, the change in `phi` and the motion of the seal must cancel so that the new seal still
has `phi=0`. Imposing the old fixed-location rule as well would prevent a regular seal from moving.

The global-scale calculation supplied a useful clue:

- the pre-scale `C^2` bulk is completely indifferent to an overall cell scale;
- the post-scale EH bulk can have a scale-balance equation, but only after its shape, cosmological
  coefficient, and boundary contribution are known.

That resembles the proposed two-stage picture: the first stage preserves scale freedom and a later
stage may determine a physical scale. It does not yet explain how the universe passes between the
stages.

Finally, if the “mirror” is really a seam joining two copies, the two sides can cancel each other's
boundary flux. But that happens only after the complete matching rule is known. At present we know
the scalar `phi` reflection, not the full clock/angular/time-dependent stitching.

So the free-boundary idea survives as a promising part of the eventual bootstrap. It is not, by
itself, the missing complete action or boundary law.
