# Lay report

We found that UDT’s basic reciprocal structure is sturdier than the newer
`3+3` structure.

The `3+3` structure needs `phi` to be changing in a definite non-lightlike
direction. If `phi` flattens out, that local construction disappears.

But the original reciprocal rule only needs the value of `phi`. At the
mirror seal, the two sides reverse `phi` and swap the reciprocal pair. Those
two reversals cancel exactly. The reciprocal rule joins smoothly across the
seal even when `phi` is flat there.

An analogy is a road whose painted center arrow becomes ambiguous on a
perfectly flat patch. The road itself still continues through the patch.
Here the derivative-built `3+3` direction is the arrow; the reciprocal
value structure is the road.

This is encouraging because critical points no longer threaten the global
existence of reciprocal structure itself. They only interrupt one particular
way of displaying that structure.

The catch is equally clear. The reciprocal road crosses the seal without
telling the angular sector how to join it. We tested different angular
rotations and shears; the reciprocal identity did not care which one was
used. So this result does not yet assemble the complete orchestra or choose
one universe.

What is still missing is the native rule that ties this robust reciprocal
bundle to the physical angular coframe, full metric, and connection. Only
after that can a complete branch, source, mass, or density response be
calculated.
