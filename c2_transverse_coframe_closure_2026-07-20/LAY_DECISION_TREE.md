# Lay decision tree

## Did allowing the angular screen to stretch select one clock curve?

It produced an equation that looked like a selector, but that equation rejected an exact solution of
the complete metric equation. It was therefore a restriction artifact, not a new law.

## What restriction caused it?

The calculation changed the angular leg lengths while holding the angular profile, poles, periods,
and coordinate domain fixed. Those pieces cannot generally remain fixed during a smooth global
deformation.

## What survived the audit?

The full product Bach family remains locally valid. The twist is a genuine gauge connection with a
curvature, constant local twist is removable, and twist does not mix linearly with area or shear.

## Was the complete general twist equation obtained?

No. The exact arbitrary-area/shear contraction reached its registered CPU stop. It remains
inconclusive and was not replaced with a simplified ansatz.

## Where is the selector most likely hiding now?

In the missing angular completion: which domains, caps, periods, and smooth variations the finite
UDT cell permits. Those data must be derived before the angular equations can honestly be reduced to
radial ones.
