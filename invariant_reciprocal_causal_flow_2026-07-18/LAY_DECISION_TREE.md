# Lay decision tree

1. **What does the metric establish about causal propagation?**  
   The supplied Lorentzian metric mathematically determines null directions. Whether physical matter, clocks, and signals universally follow that cone remains open pending a native carrier/source/coupling theorem.

2. **Is `c0 exp(-2 phi)` a locally measured varying speed?**  
   No. It is an adapted coordinate slope. A metric-normalized orthonormal observer assigns `c0` to metric-null curves at finite `phi`; actual material rods and clocks are not yet derived.

3. **Is there an invariant version of the idea?**  
   Yes, conditionally. Once a static clock flow and spatial quotient are supplied, the optical stretch is `exp(2 phi)`, its reciprocal under `phi -> -phi`, and its integral gives light-travel time.

4. **Does the metric universally choose that clock flow and spatial line?**  
   No. Flat spacetime admits inertial and accelerated choices with different coordinate slopes and optical components.

5. **What happens at the WR-L wall?**  
   Formally integrating from the curvature-singular center gives finite proper distance (`2X`) and infinite static optical time. From any regular interior start, the remaining proper distance is still finite and the optical time still diverges. Only the radial block is Rindler-like; the full metric is curved but has a regular horizon-crossing coordinate form at the wall.

6. **Is the wall therefore an energy substrate, mirror seal, or the CMB?**  
   Not from the metric alone. The WR-L wall has `phi -> +infinity`, while the canonical static mirror seal has `phi=0`; the CMB is a time-live readout surface. Any identification needs new topology, matching, and additional physics.

7. **Is the substrate/recycling idea rejected?**  
   No. It remains a separate open hypothesis, but it must derive boundary degrees of freedom, black-hole transfer, dispersed mass emergence, conservation, and causal consistency.

8. **What should be tested next?**  
   A native selector for clock congruence + reciprocal spatial line + normalization + global matching, followed by a time-live null-arrival derivation. Do not retune an action or launch numerics before that conceptual selector is tested.
