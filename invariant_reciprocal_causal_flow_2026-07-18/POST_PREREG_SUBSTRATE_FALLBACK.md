# Post-preregistration substrate/recycling clarification

After the invariant causal-flow preregistration was being prepared, the owner added a fallback possibility: if the optical construction encounters a wall, that wall might be an energy substrate tying the universe together and participating in recycling from black-hole destruction to widely dispersed mass emergence.

This clarification is preserved as hypothesis class I5. It is not treated as a premise, evidence, or a rescue explanation for the metric calculation.

The hypothesis adds at least five structures not supplied by the reciprocal metric alone:

1. a physical boundary rather than a coordinate horizon or chosen domain edge;
2. boundary degrees of freedom or a substrate state space;
3. a bulk-to-boundary transfer law for black-hole destruction;
4. a boundary-to-bulk emergence law for dispersed matter;
5. conservation, causal, and no-signalling rules for the full cycle.

The current derivation can say whether the metric forces such a wall. It cannot derive the substrate or recycling dynamics. Those remain eligible for a separately preregistered future branch if the owner chooses it.

“Black hole,” “destruction,” “mass,” and “recycling” are preserved here as owner-hypothesis/comparison terminology. The present foundation has not derived native black-hole ontology, finite-cell mass or charge, transfer dynamics, conservation across such a surface, or intervention-level no-signalling.
