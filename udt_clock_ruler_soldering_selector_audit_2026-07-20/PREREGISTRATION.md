## Exact tests

### A. Tensor-type and freedom audit

For a Lorentz metric `H` and reciprocal generator `L`, classify all orthonormalizers `S` satisfying

```text
S^T H S = eta.
```

Identify their residual Lorentz freedom and determine which additional tensor/equation would be
needed to select one as the operational coframe. A scalar calibration may not be treated as a
coframe-valued selector.

### B. Full-coframe seal extension

Extend the mixed base pair to a complete real symmetric metric with an explicit angular/normal
block and seal involution. Derive the parity equations for base-angular cross terms. Determine
whether the complete involution equations constrain the base invariant `mu`.

### C. Countermodels with identical authorized structure

Attempt at least two exact Lorentzian full-coframe witnesses with different `mu` that share:

- the same finite `c_E` calibration convention;
- the same CSN representative convention;
- the same angular metric and angular involution where type-correct;
- the same spatial-seal algebra;
- the same signature, orientation class, and determinant normalization where jointly possible;
- both zero and nonzero parity-compatible cross-sector realizations when allowed.

Any proposed selector fails if both inequivalent witnesses satisfy it.

### D. CSN, determinant, and angular compensation

Test whether positive common rescaling, determinant/volume normalization, or a compensating angular
scale can remove or determine `mu`. Distinguish gauge normalization from a dimensionless invariant.

### E. Cartan/holonomy visibility

Audit whether the existing metric-led Cartan or holonomy results contain an equation that fixes the
relative reciprocal/orthonormal orientation. A connection computed after choosing a coframe does
not select that coframe unless an independent condition breaks the frame freedom.

### F. Bootstrap rank

Read the exact current bootstrap definition and equations. Count whether it supplies a load-bearing
equation involving the pair invariant, a coframe map, or an angular/base coupling. Do not promote
on-shell closure or admissibility to an off-shell selector.

## Falsification and certification contract

- A unique-soldering claim requires an exact current equation whose solutions form one allowed
  operational coframe class after legitimate gauge is removed.
- A unique-`mu` claim requires an exact equation with one allowed invariant value, not a chosen
  determinant or coordinate normalization.
- Angular/seal underdetermination requires two inequivalent `mu` witnesses satisfying every exact
  tested angular and seal condition.
- A block-diagonal countermodel is insufficient if current authority requires a nonzero coupling;
  in that case a nonzero parity-compatible witness must also be attempted.
- Bootstrap selection fails if the existing closure contains no variable or equation of the needed
  tensor type.
- A negative result is restricted to current authorized structures. It cannot exclude an unknown
  full angular dynamical equation from selecting the soldering later.
