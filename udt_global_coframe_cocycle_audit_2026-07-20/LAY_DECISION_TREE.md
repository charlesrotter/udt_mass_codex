# Lay explanation and decision tree

## What we learned

We found that the earlier choice was too narrow.

We had been comparing two options:

1. a diagonal metric where dilation is visible but the mirror does not close correctly;
2. a perfectly reciprocal crossed metric where the mirror closes but dilation disappears from the
   metric and becomes only a change of measuring frame.

There is a third option. The clock and ruler directions can be mixed rather than both being aligned
with the metric's principal axes. In that mixed description:

- dilation remains physically present;
- the metric remains Lorentzian;
- the finite-cell mirror closes exactly;
- at the ordinary reference point, a change of basis makes it look like a standard time direction
  plus a reflected spatial direction.

So reciprocity and an ordinary-looking Lorentzian mirror are not inherently incompatible. The
conflict came from trying to diagonalize two different structures at the same time.

## What is still free

There is a continuous, dimensionless amount of clock/ruler mixing. Call it `mu`. Two exact examples,
`mu=4` and `mu=9`, satisfy all the same local mirror tests. Nothing currently selects one.

The metric also supplies a precise grammar for joining neighboring reciprocal frames: every closed
loop must contain an even number of depth reversals. But grammar is not a map. UDT still has not
specified which patches, corners, and angular caps make up the whole universe.

## Decision tree

```text
Can reciprocal dilation and a Lorentzian finite-cell mirror coexist?
|
+-- Yes, in an exact mixed clock/ruler readout family.
|
Does reciprocity or CSN select one member of that family?
|
+-- No. A dimensionless mixing modulus remains.
|
Does cocycle consistency select it?
|
+-- No. Cocycle consistency constrains transitions after a chart/corner map is supplied.
|
Does smooth angular closure select the global topology?
|
+-- No. Several primitive smooth cap lattices remain.
|
Does the mirror then determine the legal action variations?
|
+-- No. Several boundary tangent and polarization classes remain.
|
What should be tested next?
|
+-- Whether the observed c clock/ruler anchor, the exact meaning of the physical
    time/radial slots, and the separate temporal-mirror sector select or reject
    the mixed readout and its modulus.
```

## Plain analogy

The reciprocal rule is like a set of gears. We had assumed every gear axle also had to line up with
the coordinate grid painted on the machine. That made the mirror mechanism appear impossible. Once
the gear assembly is allowed to sit at an angle to the grid, it works exactly.

But many tilt angles work. We still need the physical rule that tells us how the machine is mounted
relative to the clocks and rulers we actually observe.
