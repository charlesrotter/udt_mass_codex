# Lay Decision Tree

## What did we test?

We tested whether the relationship previously noticed between clock slowing and curvature was already forced by UDT's known rules, or whether it was simply a special fingerprint of the WR–L metric we had already chosen.

## What happened?

The relationship is much more than a coincidence: if we impose it on every possible static, round, reciprocal finite-cell metric, it leaves only the WR–L shape.

But that sentence contains an important “if.” UDT's presently registered rules do not yet tell the metric that it must obey this relationship.

We proved that by slightly reshaping the interior while leaving the central value, outer wall, reciprocal clock/ruler pairing, positivity, and wall behavior intact. The reshaped metric fails the clock–curvature relationship.

## So is the lead dead?

No. It has been graded correctly.

It is an excellent **lock**: once supplied, it locks the tested metric family onto WR–L. It is not yet the **key**: we still do not know which native UDT principle inserts that lock into the theory.

There is a second clue. Before the universe chooses a physical scale, UDT treats local common rescalings as equivalent. The clock–curvature equation changes under those rescalings. That means it naturally belongs after a physical member of the scale-equivalent family has been selected, unless a deeper scale-covariant form is derived.

## What remains missing?

In plain language, UDT still needs a native rule that says:

1. which member of the scale-equivalent metric family becomes the physical universe; and
2. why that physical metric's clock rate and curvature must obey this particular relationship.

The finite wall can help constrain the answer, and matter/bootstrap may select it globally, but the present wording does not yet calculate it.

## What did we not claim?

We did not derive a complete field equation, action, matter source, carrier, mass, or `X_max`. We also did not disprove that a deeper UDT principle could derive the relationship. We located the missing step precisely.
