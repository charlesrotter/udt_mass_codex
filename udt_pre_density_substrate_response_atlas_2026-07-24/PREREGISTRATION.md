# Pre-density substrate-response atlas preregistration

Date: 2026-07-24  
Base: `929106194476d7ef2dd062de8f303c7ea17ae903`  
Mode: CPU-only, metric-led, finite symbolic/algebraic atlas  
Order: `MAP -> OBSERVE -> PONDER -> DERIVE`

## Whole question

Before giving the global substrate a density label or solving a matter field,
what local and global structures does the complete UDT coframe already make
available to a conditional micro-sector, and which apparent structures remain
separate because they transform under different groups?

The exact bounded question is:

> Across the registered complete-coframe axes and all twelve finite-cell
> completion classes, do the normalized angular shape, its spin-two
> eigenaxis, and the relative torus shift connection descend separately or
> jointly?  If they do not join geometrically, how does the already-posited
> round-`S2` `L2+L4` diagnostic respond to each channel without being solved
> or promoted to native matter?

This is a **pre-density** atlas. No axis, parameter, branch, or sample may be
called density. The density-to-geometry map is not known.

## Why this is not a target search

The atlas does not ask for a particle, a Hopf charge, a smooth cap, a stable
branch, a preferred completion, or agreement with GR. It registers every
available axis before outcomes, retains degeneracies and obstructions, and
classifies all twelve inherited finite-cell completions without filtering.

The metric-native stage is evaluated first. The conditional carrier diagnostic
is evaluated second and cannot repair or redefine the geometric result.

## Exact bounded regime

- Complete `2+2` coframe in a positive triangular angular chart:
  `theta_ang = D(dxi + S dx)`.
- Determinant-normalized angular metric `H=D^T D/det(D)`.
- Angular depth `phi`, upper-triangular shear, all four base-angular shifts,
  angular-basis changes, torus-coordinate changes, and discrete torus gluing.
- The twelve already registered finite-cell completion classes.
- Local exact algebra plus finite global descent/obstruction tables.
- Conditional diagnostic only: the registered round-`S2` `L2+L4` functional,
  evaluated through exact conformal/anisotropic response identities and
  manufactured CPU anchors. No field relaxation and no time evolution.

Not covered:

- a density-to-geometry law or density window;
- a native action, native matter source, selected carrier, selected section,
  selected boundary, or physical representative;
- stability, spectrum, mass, charge, scale closure, or bootstrap fixed point;
- GPU exploration or time-live dynamics;
- arbitrary nonlinear global solutions outside the twelve registered
  completion types.

## Registered stages

### Stage A — metric-native local objects

Derive and type separately:

1. normalized angular scale, depth, shear, and isotropic locus;
2. the traceless angular spin-two pair and eigenaxis line;
3. the relative torus phase coordinate and shift connection;
4. curvature and holonomy of that connection;
5. their laws under every transformation in
   `TRANSFORMATION_GROUP_REGISTRY.tsv`;
6. whether a metric-supplied homomorphism, soldering form, common cocycle, or
   canonical section joins the spin-two line to the relative torus circle.

No equality such as `delta=2 alpha`, flatness, trivial holonomy, or chosen
framing is assumed.

### Stage B — global finite-cell descent

For every row of `COMPLETION_TEST_REGISTRY.tsv`, classify:

- existence of angular eigenaxis data away from isotropic zeros;
- behavior at caps, mirrors, nonorientation, rank changes, and anholonomy;
- preservation, reversal, or mixing of the relative phase covector;
- connection curvature/holonomy requirements;
- whether a joint object exists without additional structure.

An arbitrary section must not be confused with a covariantly constant section.
Flatness is required only for the latter.

### Stage C — conditional `L2+L4` response

Only after Stage A/B are frozen, evaluate the response channels listed in
`CONDITIONAL_PROBE_REGISTRY.tsv`.

The supplied carrier and action remain `POSIT`/`CONDITIONAL`. The probes may
show sensitivity to selected background geometry. They cannot derive the
carrier, action, source, background, or stable solution.

## Physical-choice ledger

Every physical or geometric choice is registered in
`PREMISE_LEDGER.tsv`. Every sampled substrate axis is registered in
`SUBSTRATE_AXIS_REGISTRY.tsv`, and every finite anchor value is frozen in
`SAMPLE_GRID.tsv`. No outcome-dependent axis or sample may be added to the
primary atlas. A later exploratory appendix would require a new
preregistration.

## Falsification and certification contract

The following outcomes are decided mechanically:

1. **Common descended bundle supported** only if the production and
   independent implementations derive the same transition cocycle for the
   two candidate objects under all applicable registered transformations,
   including torus `GL(2,Z)`, and no unregistered soldering map is used.
2. **Typed channels distinct; soldering open** if the eigenaxis and relative
   phase transform under different principal actions and the metric supplies
   no canonical identification.
3. **Conditional branchwise join** only if a join works on explicitly listed
   completion/holonomy/isotropy subcases and fails or is undefined elsewhere.
4. **No relative phase scalar** where the phase covector is mixed by gluing
   or where no global toric coordinates exist.
5. **No parallel phase section** where connection curvature is nonzero, or
   where flat holonomy is not integral. This does not deny ordinary sections
   of a separately supplied bundle.
6. **Conditional response only** for every `L2+L4` result, even if a response
   is exact or nonzero.

Certification requires:

- preregistration committed before outcome scripts;
- all registered axes and all twelve completions represented exactly once;
- exact symbolic residuals equal zero where exact identities are claimed;
- finite numeric residuals below `1e-11` for manufactured anchors;
- an independent implementation that does not import the production module;
- exercised catch-proofs for omitted axes/completions, group conflation,
  assumed soldering, density relabeling, flat-section conflation, carrier
  promotion, and outcome-dependent selection;
- source hashes and package hashes recorded.

## Maximum allowed conclusion

At most this audit may establish a typed, completion-scoped atlas of
metric-native substrate channels and exact conditional response identities.
It may identify a metric-derived join if the full transition-law contract
passes. It may not claim a selected carrier, action, source, boundary,
physical scale, density window, matter solution, stability theorem, mass, or
bootstrap closure.
