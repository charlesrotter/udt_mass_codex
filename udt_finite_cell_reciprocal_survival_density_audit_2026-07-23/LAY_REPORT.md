# Lay report

We found a real global constraint, not another guessed mechanism.

The new reciprocal structure works wherever `phi` is changing in a definite
non-lightlike direction. If that change stops or becomes lightlike, the
structure loses its clean three-versus-three split.

That matters for a complete finite universe. On a closed, static space, an
ordinary real-valued `phi` must reach a high or low point somewhere—just as
altitude on a globe must have a summit or a bottom. At such a point its
change is zero. So the new reciprocal structure cannot remain intact
everywhere in a closed **static** cell.

There are honest ways around that mathematical obstruction:

- a cell with actual boundaries can let `phi` keep changing from one side to
  the other;
- `phi` can change through time, even when space itself is closed; or
- a more complicated global field branch may exist.

But UDT has not yet selected any of those possibilities. The repository
currently contains 12 kinds of ways a cell could be completed, not 12 fully
solved universes. This audit classifies what each kind would require and
stops where the missing full field profile begins.

We also tested whether total density could now be used as the next dial. It
cannot—not yet. Density is not connected to these geometry branches by a
native UDT source equation or mass response law. Scanning tiny to enormous
density values would therefore produce identical rows because the program
would have nowhere to put the number.

So the useful outcome is:

- static closed branches have a newly proved reciprocal-degeneration point;
- boundary and time-live branches remain possible;
- no topology is selected;
- no density window has been found or ruled out; and
- the exact missing bridge is now visible: a native law connecting
  same-solution mass/density back to the complete metric branch.
