# Lay readout

Yes: WR-L turned out to be a narrow camera setting, not the whole UDT
camera.

The complete metric has ten independent places where geometry can live:
clock, distance, angular shape, and four ways those sectors can lean or
twist into one another. They may all change with time and with every
spatial direction.

WR-L reduced that orchestra to one instrument:

```text
one static radial function A(r).
```

It made the geometry spherical, diagonal, time-independent, centered,
round in the angles, and free of every mixed shift. Then it selected the
special profile `A=1-r/X`.

That makes WR-L useful but limited. It was never equipped to describe the
complete relation among differently oriented observers.

## What the full metric gives immediately

At any one event, the metric allows a whole family of equally valid
unaccelerated observer directions. It does not pick one as the local
throne. Any two observers agree on:

- the same light cone;
- the same magnitude of their relative motion;
- the same clock/space geometry, expressed in their respective frames.

That is genuine local frame reciprocity derived from the metric.

It does not mean every location in a curved universe is globally
identical. A particular solution can have curvature, matter, boundaries,
or other structures. Local equality of frames and global sameness of
places are different claims.

## The interesting new piece

When two observer changes point along the same line, their depth-like
parameters add cleanly. That is the simple reciprocal pattern we had
mostly been studying.

When observer changes point in different directions, they do not simply
add. Their combination rotates the observer's transverse angular screen.

So the full metric says:

```text
one-direction reciprocity is additive;
complete frame reciprocity is additive plus angular rotation.
```

That angular rotation was absent from WR-L because WR-L froze the angular
and mixed sectors before the question was asked.

This is not yet a particle, force, Hopfion, or action. But it is a clean
metric-born reason why the angular sector must participate in the complete
theory.

## What survives from WR-L

WR-L can still describe a bounded radial observational relation:

- its clock slowdown;
- its `X` asymptote;
- its optical depth;
- its successful supernova profile comparison.

What is withdrawn is the idea that this one centered radial chart is the
complete metric of every observer.

The next task is no longer to force WR-L to recenter. It is to follow the
full metric's non-collinear frame composition—including its induced
angular-screen rotation—and determine whether the already recorded
reciprocal, CSN, finite-cell, and `phi` structures join it intrinsically.
