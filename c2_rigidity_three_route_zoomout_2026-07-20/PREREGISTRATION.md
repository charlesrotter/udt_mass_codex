a positive diagonal lapse, and the first time/fiber Jacobi connection. What does that repeated
rigidity actually teach us about the open UDT action problem, and which of three routes can close an
upstream dependency rather than merely explore another conditional corner?

The routes are:

1. complete nonlinear stationary `C^2` closure;
2. physical finite-cell boundary closure;
3. variation-domain/two-stage-bridge closure.

This is an observing and dependency-ranking audit. It does not target a desired action, force a
two-stage bridge, or treat absence of modes as merit.
