# Lay Decision Tree

1. **Did the recent equations reveal that all of UDT is rigid?**
   No. They revealed that one smooth compact conditional `C^2` branch is locally rigid.
2. **Should we now solve the full nonlinear branch?**
   Eventually, yes. But first we should know what physical boundary problem that solution must obey.
3. **Will boundary variation automatically give the physical wall rule?**
   No. It gives the possible boundary quantities and natural equations. UDT must still identify which
   data are physically fixed.
4. **Is the deepest question still whether variation occurs before or after scale selection?**
   Yes. Current premises do not answer it.
5. **If we choose “after,” is the bridge solved?**
   No. We still need a rule that actually chooses the representative and a theorem connecting the
   pre-scale and post-scale dynamics.
6. **Do `c` and `G` pick the universe's mass and size?**
   Not alone. A continuous family can keep `c`, `G`, and dimensionless compactness unchanged while
   mass and size scale together.
7. **What is the next calculation?**
   Derive the exact finite-cell boundary variation of the conditional `C^2` metric action and list
   what it supplies versus what remains a physical choice.
