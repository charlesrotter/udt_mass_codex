# Reciprocal line-realization selector — lay decision tree

1. The reciprocal time/length **conversion pair** is still derived kinematics.
2. Ask whether the spacetime metric alone identifies which line is “time” and which spatial line is
   “parallel.”
   - Universally and locally: **no**. A flat Lorentz metric is unchanged by rotations and boosts,
     and no single line is unchanged by all of them.
   - On a specially structured geometry: **sometimes conditionally**. Curvature, a scalar gradient,
     or a static symmetry can identify directions only where their extra conditions hold.
3. Ask whether the finite mirror solves the problem.
   - At the seal it can provide a spatial normal line in the declared static sector.
   - It supplies neither a unique time line nor a unique way to extend that normal through the cell.
4. Therefore the current missing step is a native rule connecting the internal reciprocal pair to
   spacetime lines, including degenerate regions and the finite cell.
5. Until that rule and its variation status are derived, neither the pre-scale `C^2` route nor the
   post-scale EH route is promoted. Both remain conditional; their bridge remains open.

This does **not** say UDT must add a physical auxiliary field. A later derived projector,
endomorphism, boundary/global construction, or justified sector could supply the realization.
