# Motif Atlas — Lay Readout

The useful result is that the metric's local pieces do not behave like millions of unrelated
combinations. Across every tested local geometry and every combination of the five available
instruments, they repeatedly assemble from a small vocabulary:

1. **Silence:** nothing in that combination distinguishes one direction from another.
2. **A coupled pair plus two separate directions:** the pair contains one time-like and one
   space-like direction; the other two are space-like axes.
3. **Four separate directions:** one time-like and three space-like.
4. **One direction plus the other three:** this is what the tested nonzero local slopes of phi
   contribute. The registered slopes were time-like or space-like, not light-like.
5. **A four-way weave:** the instruments interact so that no smaller common direction or plane is
   left intact.

The most interesting interaction is between curvature represented by Ricci and the phi Hessian.
Each can independently make the coupled-pair motif, but their actual pairs normally cross rather
than coincide. In every one of the 2,090 clean cases where both pairs were available, they shared no
line, failed to commute, and together wove all four directions. The phi-gradient direction likewise
crossed both sides of every clean coupled-pair split in the registered ensemble. This is much closer
to an orchestra than to one privileged instrument playing the answer alone.

There is a tempting UDT picture here: the coupled time/space pair could be related to a reciprocal
clock-and-ruler plane, with two transverse directions. But the calculation has not earned that
physical name. It has found the local algebraic shape only. It has not shown how the shape is
transported, selected globally, made into matter, or governed by an action.

Nor have we shown that this small abstract vocabulary is exclusive to UDT; some of it may be the
generic grammar of four-dimensional Lorentzian geometry. What is specifically mapped here is how
the UDT metric/phi instruments populate and combine those shapes.

So the achievement is not “we found the final combination.” It is: **we found a small set of local
building blocks and a repeatable grammar for how they preserve, refine, or dissolve one another.**
The final structure can now be allowed to emerge from those blocks without searching for a lucky
combination that was chosen to resemble the desired universe.
