# Adversarial review

## Strongest overclaim

Because `S(log Q) direct_sum M` composes, one might call it the unique
native UDT observer representation.

That conclusion does not follow. A direct sum is always available after two
typed cocycles are placed on the same base path. The calculation gives no
off-diagonal mixing, invariant irreducibility, or proof that `log Q` is the
founding reciprocal depth.

## Strongest reduction error

The determinant of the vertex Jacobi map is the optical-area readout, but
the vertex map is not a propagator. It discards transverse position or
direction data needed at an intermediate event. Promoting it to a cocycle
fails the exact round counterexample.

## Strongest caustic error

At the round antipode the projected Jacobi block vanishes. Declaring
transport undefined there would confuse projection singularity with
fundamental-matrix singularity. The full propagator is `-I` and remains
invertible.

## Strongest branch splice

B19 supplies complete center-free transverse geometry with a trivial clock.
WR-L supplies nontrivial centered clock and transverse readouts locally.
Combining those virtues would mix different metrics and is forbidden. No
registered branch currently supplies both.

## Strongest observer error

The frequency ratio requires specified endpoint observers and events. It is
not an observer-independent scalar attached only to two bare spatial
points. Its composition law is exact only when the intermediate
observer/event and normalization are the same on both segments.

## Strongest ontology error

Neither the null path nor an observer-rest/co-present path is selected here.
The general first-order construction works for a supplied typed path, but
that mathematical generality does not choose the physical interpretation.

## Disposition

The bounded claim survives:

- full transverse metric-path cocycle: `DERIVED`;
- same-path reducible clock/transverse assembly: `DERIVED`;
- founding reciprocal solder: `CONDITIONAL`;
- irreducible and universal physical realization: `OPEN`.
