# What the four-bank interior map found

We had previously checked every line joining two of the four metric banks. Those edge checks were
orderly: lines among the first three banks stayed on one side of the metric's causal divide, while
each line to the fourth bank crossed that divide once.

This audit finally looked inside the whole tetrahedron rather than inferring its interior from its
edges.

The important finding is simple:

- When already-evaluated metric fields are blended (`J2`), the interior keeps the one-divider
  pattern suggested by the edges.
- When the underlying metric generators and their evaluation point are blended first (`J1`), an
  additional enclosed pocket appears. A path starting inside that pocket crosses out of it and then
  crosses the outer divider on the way to the fourth bank.

All 384 registered field/mask combinations showed the same qualitative split at both frozen
resolutions. No angular field or shift was removed.

This is the first clean “orchestra” effect in this particular configuration map: every two-bank
edge among the first three banks stays on the same side, yet their simultaneous three-way
generator mixture develops structure that no one edge contains.

It is not yet physics. `J1` and `J2` are two chosen ways of joining configurations, and UDT has not
derived which, if either, is the physical composition law. The chart disagreement is therefore the
main scientific information: nonlinear metric assembly matters, and pairwise adjacency cannot
determine the complete interior.

The cautious next question is not “what particle or cosmic regime is the pocket?” It is whether the
registered metric principles supply a native, coordinate-independent rule for composing complete
coframes. Until that rule exists, the extra pocket is a robust bounded clue, not a selected feature
of the universe.
