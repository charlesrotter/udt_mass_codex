## Ruling in one paragraph

UDT does not currently inherit the GR action package. It retains a
four-dimensional comparison arena, but leaves the dynamical fields, covariance
class, locality, off-shell variation domain, and derivative order open. It
modifies the boundary problem from spatial infinity to a finite mirrored cell,
while the differentiable boundary action and normalized charge remain open.
Reciprocity and Common-Scale Neutrality constrain the pre-material kinematics;
finite-cell structure constrains the global boundary ontology; bootstrap closure
demands a realized global solution. None of those existing selectors presently
chooses a complete action. The immediate missing discriminator is whether the
fundamental variation acts on the pre-scale CSN equivalence class or only after
global closure selects a physical representative. That discriminator separates
the C2 and EH routes, but does not by itself derive either complete action.

No carrier is assumed in this audit. The reopened round-S2 carrier and every
carrier-dependent source or mass statement retain the conditional status in the
final adjudication.

## Classification rule

- `INHERITED`: retained from the GR comparison frame without claiming a C0/C1
  derivation.
- `MODIFIED`: retained only after a UDT selector changes its domain or meaning.
- `REJECTED`: inadmissible as native/default input, while an explicitly named
  conditional comparison may still exist.
- `OPEN`: not selected by C0/C1 or the accepted A/B/C adjudication.

The primary classification does not erase a separately stated open residue.

## Conditional branch comparison

### 1. Pre-scale C2/Bach

The strongest pre-scale route requires all of the following class premises:

1. four dimensions;
2. exact local CSN/Weyl compatibility;
3. metric-only independent fields;
4. local diffeomorphism covariance;
5. a local polynomial parity-even bulk;
6. the lowest nontrivial curvature-square inventory; and
7. unrestricted metric variation.

Within that class, the parity-even bulk reduces to `p C^2 + q Euler`; modulo
Euler/topological and boundary terms and normalization, the bulk direction is
`C^2`, and unrestricted variation gives the fourth-order Bach equation. This is
`UNIQUE-CONDITIONAL`, not a unique complete UDT action. Reciprocal-constraint
implementation, fourth-order boundary/corner data, source compatibility,
normalization, a nontrivial finite-cell solution, stability, and bootstrap
closure remain open. Restricting to the reciprocal ansatz before variation can
discard normal metric equations.

### 2. Post-scale Einstein-Hilbert/Lovelock

The EH route begins only after a physical representative and scale are
available. It additionally assumes metric-only unrestricted variation, local
four-dimensional diffeomorphism covariance, at-most-second-order metric
equations, and Lovelock minimality. Within that class the bulk is EH plus the
usual conditional cosmological, topological, and boundary freedoms.

This route is `CONDITIONAL`. EH is not exactly pre-scale CSN invariant: it needs
a selected representative and a dimensionful coefficient. C0/C1 does not fix
the representative-selection rule, `G`, any `Lambda`, GHY/corners, finite-cell
reference and normalization, source, solution, or stability. An asymptotic GR
normalization remains a scoped comparison, not native cell input.

### 3. Possible two-stage bridge

A logically clean bridge would have to expose every transition:

```text
pre-scale CSN class
    -> pre-scale law, if any
    -> finite-cell/global bootstrap selects a physical representative and scale
    -> demonstrated regime/matching map
    -> post-scale effective law and its finite-cell boundary completion
```

Two variants remain possible but unproved:

- A pre-scale C2/Bach law could be fundamental, with EH appearing only as a
  post-scale effective regime.
- The pre-scale layer could remain kinematic, with an action introduced only
  after bootstrap selects a representative.

Neither variant is derived. Selecting a representative does not transform a
fourth-order Bach theory into second-order EH. A bridge must also prove the
dynamical matching or term-selection rule, reconcile degrees of freedom and
variation domains, and match the finite-cell boundary data and charges. Exact
pre-scale CSN plus metric-only fields plus an at-most-second-order non-topological
local bulk has no nontrivial EH-like member in the audited inventory; a genuine
bridge therefore changes regime or assumptions and must say where.

## Smallest genuinely missing selector

At the immediate C2/EH fork, the smallest missing selector is the placement of
the variational law relative to scale selection:

> Is fundamental variation defined on the pre-scale CSN equivalence class
> `[g]_CSN`, or on a physical representative `g_*` only after finite-cell/global
> bootstrap closure?

This question already exists in the accepted ledger; naming it invents no new
principle. A pre-scale answer requires exact CSN compatibility and makes EH
inadmissible in that role. A post-scale answer permits EH as a comparison class
but does not select it.

There is no single presently known selector that closes the complete action.
After the immediate fork, independent choices remain for the field census,
covariance, locality, variation domain, derivative order/invariant inventory,
normalization, source, boundary completion, solution, stability, and global
closure. For a two-stage bridge, even a derived representative-selection rule
would still need a separate dynamical matching theorem. Reporting more would
invent the missing physics.
