## 0. Why this MAP exists

We can already get **local** continuum solutions that:

- have a workable **middle** (especially a **finite core**),
- carry **nontrivial redshift**,

**This MAP’s job:** name the ways a solution can be **whole**, what each assumes, what would kill it, and what we should compute *only after* you lean.

## 3. What “finished whole” could mean (four options)

These are **outer architectures**. They can combine later; first see them separately.

---

### Option E1 — **Closure (critical whole)**

**Picture.**  
The universe (or universe-cell) only exists as a smooth global solution for **special matter content** — one amount or a narrow window. Outside that window: singular, incomplete, or nonexistent. That critical amount is the theory’s **prediction**. Redshift–distance and “how much stuff we see” are checked **afterward**.

**What it assumes**

- “Existence of a regular global solution” is a sharp enough filter to pick amount (or a discrete set).  
- Some outer condition is part of what “global” means (finite size, matching, or both) — must be named, not vibes.  
- Continuum matter is allowed to be the scale-giver.

**What success looks like**

- Scan matter budget → solutions exist only near a critical value (or discrete set).  
- At that value: regular core + controlled outer end.  
- Amount is **output**; not dialed to data.

**What kills it**

- Smooth solutions exist for a **wide continuum** of amounts with no selection (then “closure” is not predictive — only a fit knob in disguise).  
- The only way to close is to re-import **cell seals / winding** (contamination).  
- Closure only works if we tune α or shape to force it (imposition).

**Traps**

- Calling any finite box “closed.”  
- Putting mirror-seal BCs in by habit and declaring victory.  
- Tuning μ until D turns over and calling that critical-universe proof.

**Fit to canon / hunches**

- Close to **critical universe** and “matter at one critical amount.”  
- Finite-cell canon *may* live here if “finite” **emerges**, not if imposed as particle seal.

**Tag if pursued:** outer definition FREE until specified; amount FREE to scan, pin by existence.

---

### Option E2 — **Edge as limit (vacuum / max-reach matching)**

**Picture.**  
Deep outside, the solution approaches a **limiting structure** (your “vacuum as limit” / unreachable horizon idea — like *c* in relativity: you never sit on it, but it organizes the interior). Matter **regularizes the middle**; the outer is matched onto that limit at large redshift or large distance. The whole = **interior + matching + limit**.

**What it assumes**

- A well-defined limit solution exists (matter off or matter dilute).  
- Matching conditions are native (not GR junction copied blindly — GR corpus is a **mine**, but forms must be re-derived under dilation).  
- The limit can sit at finite coordinate radius with infinite proper distance / infinite z, or another clear geometric meaning.

**What success looks like**

- Interior finite-core matter solution.  
- Outer region approaches the limit profile (e.g. characteristic shape of areal radius vs redshift).  
- Matching is continuous in the derived sense; no hand patch.

**What kills it**

- Limit solution is the same structural failure as “vacuum cosmology” (no consistent matching surface).  
- Limit only matches if we force the interior toward a data shape.  
- Owed check fails: limit shape does **not** connect to high-z required geometry (you already flagged this check — still owed, not assumed).

**Traps**

- Rebranding open growth as “approaching a limit” without a real limit solution.  
- Smuggling FLRW infinity or Schwarzschild exterior by name.  
- Using x_max as an **input length** instead of an emergent edge notion.

**Fit to hunches**

- Closest to **vacuum-as-limit** and star-interior ↔ exterior matching intuition.  
- Compatible with observer frame-relation (edge is where z → large for that observer’s chart).

**Tag if pursued:** limit type FREE/THEORY; matching FREE until derived.

---

### Option E3 — **Honest open whole (no finite edge)**

**Picture.**  
The macro is **not** a closed ball. It is an open geometry: areal size can grow without bound; redshift may approach a ceiling or keep growing slowly. “Whole” means: **complete as an infinite/open manifold solution** with regular core and controlled asymptotics — not “has a lid.”

**What it assumes**

- Finite-cell canon is **re-read** or scoped (universe-as-finite-cell might mean something else, or apply only to matter cells, or emerge only at another level).  
- Observations can still make sense via frame-relation + asymptotics (harder sell; must be honest).  
- Open + saturating Δφ (what we roughly saw) might be **physical**, not a failure.

**What success looks like**

- Clear asymptotic class (e.g. φ → φ_∞, D_A ∼ c r + …) derived or stably observed across premises.  
- No need for a critical amount *from closure* (amount might be free or fixed another way).  
- Explicit reconciliation note with finite-cell language in CANON (cannot silent-ignore).

**What kills it**

- Canon + Charles: “universe must be a finite mirrored cell” as non-negotiable **and** open asymptotics cannot be rephrased into that.  
- Asymptotics are chaotic / depend wildly on every FREE knob (no structural open class).  
- Open solutions cannot support later particle embedding in any controlled way.

**Traps**

- Using “open” as an excuse to never finish the outer story.  
- Quietly dropping finite-cell canon without a Charles ruling.  
- Claiming saturating Δφ~2 is “the CMB depth” — it is not.

**Fit**

- Matches **current numerics’ appearance** better than E1/E2 so far.  
- Highest tension with **finite-cell universe** wording unless re-interpreted.

**Tag if pursued:** requires **Charles ruling** on canon scope before it becomes the program.

---

### Option E4 — **Ensemble whole (average of many cells)**

**Picture.**  
There is no single continuum “dust universe” as fundamental. The macro background is what you get by **averaging** many localized cells’ exteriors. Smooth φ is emergent statistics.

**What it assumes**

- Cells/particles exist with exterior fields.  
- A native averaging law exists (not imported FLRW dust).  
- Macro work is **effective**, tagged as such.

**What success looks like**

- Controlled average produces smooth redshift law + outer behavior.  
- Visible/dim split might fall out of which cells light up (maybe).

**What kills it**

- Average cancels (no net macro source).  
- Requires finished micro **before** macro — fights “stage first” pivot; risk of circular wait.  
- “Ensemble” used only as a word to license any smooth ρ.

**Traps**

- Parking forever (“we’ll average later”).  
- Building macro on unsolved particle exteriors.

**Fit**

- Philosophically coherent long-term; **bad as the next compute**.  
- Keep **parked** until stage-or-cell story is clearer.

**Tag:** PARKED for near-term macro.
