# Lay decision tree

## What did the curved angular sector do?

It changed both the allowed clock-dilation curves and the way a transverse twist responds. The
angular and reciprocal sectors are therefore genuinely coupled in the metric.

## Did that coupling choose one physical curve?

No. It narrowed the algebraic relation among the curve's constants, but a continuous family remains.

## Did it choose one favorable twist sign?

No. One fully allowed positive local curve produces positive, zero, and negative values in different
places.

## Why not?

A single curvature number describes how the screen bends on average at a point. A twist also points
along a direction on that screen. The metric calculation retains information about how that chosen
direction turns; scalar curvature alone cannot define it.

## Does that derive the old `S2` carrier?

No. It exposes a missing directional section or framing. Choosing one and calling it the carrier
would replace the open derivation with an assumption.

## What is the next honest test?

Restore the screen's area and shear and write its twist through the complete geometric connection.
Then ask whether the full coupled equations and finite-cell boundary select the remaining branch.
This is a symbolic/geometric task first, not a GPU scan.
