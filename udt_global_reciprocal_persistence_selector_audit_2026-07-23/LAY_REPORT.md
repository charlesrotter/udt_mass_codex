# Lay report

We learned that two ideas which looked almost identical are actually
different layers of UDT.

The first is the basic reciprocal rule: at any place, the clock side and the
ruler side receive opposite exponential weights. That rule depends on the
value of `phi`, and the founding UDT premises do establish it.

The second is the new `3+3` geometric split. That split uses the *direction in
which phi is changing*. If `phi` briefly flattens out, that direction
disappears even though the reciprocal clock/ruler weights themselves remain
perfectly meaningful.

A simple exact example exposes the difference. Both `phi=x` and `phi=x^3`
are smooth, nontrivial, and mirror correctly through the cell seal. But
`x^3` is flat exactly at the seal. At that point the reciprocal values still
exist, while the derivative-built `3+3` split does not.

So the founding reciprocal principle does not secretly demand that the new
split exist everywhere. Nor do CSN, the finite cell, the seal, or the current
bootstrap statement add that demand.

This is useful progress because it removes a false shortcut. The `3+3` split
is a real structure carried by many parts of the metric, but it is not yet
the law that chooses the universe. If it becomes globally mandatory, that
must come from a still-missing native metric equation or from an additional
premise stated openly.

In plain terms: we have found an important piece of the metric's machinery,
and we have now correctly established that the owner's manual for that piece
has not yet been derived.
