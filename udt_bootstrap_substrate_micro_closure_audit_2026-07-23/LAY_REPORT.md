# Lay report

Your concern was justified.

The earlier micro work mostly tested a soliton inside a fixed mathematical
room: fixed flat geometry, fixed box, fixed boundary, and supplied matter
recipe. It established that the soliton is stable in that room. It did not
test whether changing the universe surrounding the room changes whether the
soliton can exist.

The complete UDT metric shows that the room is not generally isolated:

- the global metric determines the local measuring geometry;
- the angular sector determines local stretching and directional structure;
- the shifts determine how angular phase comparisons twist across the cell;
- caps, mirrors, topology, and holonomy constrain whether those local
  directions fit together globally.

When the existing conditional soliton energy is placed on a varying spatial
metric, the background changes its two energy terms differently and changes
the local equation. So there is a mathematically real route by which the
whole universe could affect microscopic stability.

What has not been found is the complete feedback law:

```text
universe geometry
  -> allowed matter
  -> native matter mass
  -> total density
  -> the same universe geometry.
```

We know the first geometric arrow exists. The present soliton supplies only a
conditional example of the second. Native matter, mass, and the return arrow
remain missing.

One useful negative result is that simply enlarging or shrinking everything
uniformly does not solve the particle scale. The important global effects
would have to involve the substrate's shape, variation, angular structure,
holonomy, boundaries, or physical representative—not density pasted into a
local equation.

So this audit does not close bootstrap, but it removes an artificial
restriction that may have prevented closure: future micro work must allow the
complete global metric to participate.
