# Questions 2–4 in ordinary language

## 2. What does “observer preservation” mean?

It asks a very simple consistency question:

> If two observers describe the universe from different frames, do both
> still encounter the same `Xmax` limit?

They need not assign the same coordinate number to every intermediate
event, just as two drivers need not report the same speed for a car. They
must agree on the same finish line and the same law for approaching it.

Your frame-reciprocity postulate already says yes in the ordinary regime.
So this is no longer a search for a new law. It is only a check that a
future complete moving or accelerating UDT frame does not accidentally make
`Xmax` observer-dependent. Existing conditional frame constructions are
consistent with a shared limit; the complete native frame mechanics remain
unfinished.

## 3. What does the angular question mean?

The metric describes both:

- the clock/radial direction toward `Xmax`; and
- the two-dimensional surroundings—the angular part, like the surface
  around you.

We must check that the surroundings do not tear, explode, or become
inconsistent as the clock slows toward the limit.

That check has already done useful work. Requiring finite angular curvature
at the wall helped select the linear WR-L profile instead of a continuum of
other powers. But it did not determine the complete shape, twist, topology,
or normalization of the angular sector. In short: the orchestra's angular
instruments helped choose the tempo, but they have not yet supplied the
whole score.

## 4. What does “calculate `Xmax`” mean?

The profile tells us the shape of the ruler, not the number printed at the
end.

`c_E` and `G_obs` are known observational anchors. Once UDT supplies its own
definition of the universe's total mass, a relation of the general form

```text
Xmax = dimensionless_number * G_obs * M_total / c_E^2
```

could turn that mass into a length. But UDT must still derive both the
native mass and the dimensionless number or an equivalent bootstrap
condition. With only `c_E` and `G_obs`, there is no unique length to
calculate.

So the open task is not “guess `Xmax`.” It is “finish the global
matter/bootstrap loop that selects it.”
