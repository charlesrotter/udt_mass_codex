# Lay Decision Tree — Is This Even a Constraint?

Start with something called a “constraint.”

Is it actually a finite penalty, symmetry premise, choice of spacetime domain, list of fundamental
fields, scaling rule, clock calibration, selection map, existence example, or missing parent law?

- **Yes:** it is not a constraint merely because it restricts a discussion. Classify that role first.
- **No:** continue.

Does it describe which complete universes are allowed?

- **Yes:** it is a whole-solution admissibility rule. It does not yet say how anything propagates.
- **No:** continue.

Was it added to an action with a multiplier that is varied?

- **Yes:** the multiplier equation, reaction on the other fields, and boundary terms are all real
  parts of that variational problem.
- **No:** continue.

Was the variation restricted to configurations already satisfying the constraint?

- **Yes:** that is a smaller variation domain. It is not automatically equivalent to unrestricted
  variation.
- **No:** continue.

Is it a Noether identity?

- **Yes:** it relates equations because of a symmetry. It is not another independent physical law.
- **No:** continue.

Is it a Hamiltonian or momentum constraint obtained after splitting spacetime into space plus time?

- **Yes:** it is a projection of a parent covariant equation relative to a selected slicing. UDT may
  use an analogous equation as a later diagnostic only after deriving the parent law and the status
  of the slicing. Calling it an initial-data constraint additionally requires principal-part,
  admissible-data, and propagation analysis. This audit derived a lapse-like toy but no shift anchor.
- **No:** continue.

Is it supposed to remain true from one time slice to the next?

- **Yes:** that requires a propagation theorem from the local equations. Co-presence does not move the
  constraint through time.
- **No:** continue.

Does it make the finite-cell action differentiable or specify allowed boundary data?

- **Yes:** it belongs to boundary completion, which is still open.
- **No:** its role has not yet been identified and it must not be adopted.

## Bottom line

The earlier mathematics was not wasted. It correctly showed that multipliers can be redundant or
reactive and that restricted variation is different from unrestricted variation.

What changes is the order of judgment:

> Derive the complete whole-solution law first. Only then ask what its Hamiltonian, momentum, gauge,
> initial-data, and boundary constraints look like in a chosen representation.

Co-presence makes global consistency conceptually primary. It does not turn familiar GR constraints
into native UDT equations and does not remove the need for local causal-response dynamics.
