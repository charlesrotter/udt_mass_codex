## Exact question

Under the working whole-solution semantics

\[
S=(M_S,g,\Phi,\ldots),
\qquad p\,C_S\,q\Longleftrightarrow p,q\in M_S,
\]

and the conditional metric reachability relation `J_g^+`, what role can each GR-origin “constraint”
honestly play in UDT?

The audit must distinguish:

1. a predicate on completed whole solutions;
2. an equation obtained by varying a declared multiplier;
3. a restriction of the off-shell variation domain;
4. a Noether/gauge identity among equations;
5. a slice-dependent initial-data constraint obtained by projecting complete covariant equations;
6. a finite-cell boundary or differentiability condition; and
7. a material causal-response/no-signalling condition.

It must determine whether co-presence selects any of those roles, whether Hamiltonian/momentum
constraints may be imported independently of complete dynamics, and which earlier conclusions require
only interpretive regrading rather than algebraic repetition.

## Registered candidate classes

### R0 — whole-solution admissibility

`B[S]=0` is imposed only on completed candidate solutions. It does not enter an off-shell action or
define a physical intervention by itself.

### R1 — varied global or local constraint

`S_aug=S_0+eta B` is varied with respect to every declared field and `eta`. The normal reaction and
boundary terms must be retained. This class is not equivalent to R0 without a theorem.

### R2 — constrained variation domain

Variation is restricted to a submanifold `B=0`. This differs from unrestricted variation unless the
normal equation is redundant or separately recovered.

### R3 — covariant equation and Noether identity

A complete covariant action yields Euler equations and gauge identities. A Noether identity relates
those equations; it is not automatically an extra physical constraint.

### R4 — foliation-projected constraints

Given a complete tensor equation `E_mu_nu=0`, a selected foliation and normal `n`, define normal-normal
and normal-tangent projections analogous to Hamiltonian and momentum constraints. Test whether those
projections are invariant standalone objects or conditional representations of the complete tensor
equation.

### R5 — causal propagation of constraints

Test the distinction between a constraint holding on one slice, being propagated by specified local
equations, and holding on every event of a completed history. Co-presence must not be used as a
substitute for a propagation theorem.

### R6 — finite-cell boundary completion

Test whether a multiplier or projected constraint changes endpoint/boundary variation. No GHY, ADM,
spatial-infinity, or asymptotic charge may be inherited.
