# Adversarial review

## Was the prior branch census merely repeated?

No. Its 12 completion rows, 28 equation/evidence families, and three
calculated controls were replayed unchanged. This package adds only the
profile-compatibility overlay enabled by the later projective derivation.

## Does the common endpoint exponent make `B19` equal `tanh`?

No. At `kappa=1`, both endpoint gaps fall like `exp(-2phi)`, but their
constant-depth-rescaling invariant third-origin shapes are

```text
-2             for tanh,
-pi^2/4        for B19.
```

They differ exactly. Matching the origin slope requires `kappa=pi/4`,
whereas matching the endpoint exponent requires `kappa=1`.

## Could a different `kappa` hide the equivalence?

No finite positive `kappa` changes the round shape invariant
`-pi^2/4`. It cannot equal either the projective pair `(0,-2)` or the
exponential pair `(-1,1)`.

## Does `FC12` select one profile?

No. Its free `A(phi)` can be chosen as the derivative of any desired
distance profile. The source supplies no equation choosing `A`. Counting
that flexibility as a solution would be circular.

## Is `B19` a clock-dilation solution?

No. It is on shell only in the conditional metric-only `C^2` branch and its
`phi` is angular reciprocal depth with constant lapse. Its exact profile
and diameter are geometric controls, not a physical UDT clock law.

## Does WR-L calculate global `X_max`?

No. It supplies the founding clock depth and an exact local radial
proper-distance endpoint, but lacks a complete global angular observer-pair
geometry and global diameter proof.

## Exercised corruption catches

The independent verifier rejects:

- physical promotion of projective `tanh`;
- global promotion of WR-L;
- clock-depth promotion of round `B19`;
- identity inferred from a shared endpoint exponent;
- first-order Taylor selection;
- free-profile selection in `FC12`;
- missing finite-cell or equation-family rows;
- cross-family witness construction;
- physical `X_max` promotion;
- missing or altered source identities;
- false equality of the round/projective or round/exponential shape
  invariants.
