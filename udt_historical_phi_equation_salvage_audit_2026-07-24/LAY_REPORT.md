# Lay report

The old document had one good tool wrapped inside a claim that was too strong.

The good tool tells us how any ordinary scalar quantity bends across the
simple static UDT metric. That mathematics is exact. It also gives us a useful
test equation and a familiar `sinh`-shaped solution when the geometry is held
fixed.

But the document then treated `phi` as both:

1. the thing being solved for; and
2. the thing already determining the metric in which it was being solved.

Those are not the same calculation. If the metric is allowed to change with
`phi`, an additional term appears. The historical screened equation therefore
describes a test field placed on a fixed UDT geometry; it is not automatically
the law that creates that geometry.

So we keep the ruler and discard the unsupported claim:

- keep the exact geometric operator as a future check;
- keep the `sinh` solution as a limited test case;
- do not adopt its action, screening scale, source, or universal profile.

It does not solve today's observer-pair problem because it only describes one
centered, static radial chart. It says nothing about how two arbitrary
observers recenter the geometry and carry the clock and angular information
together.
