## Why the lanes cannot be merged

The pre-scale and post-scale lanes vary different objects at different stages.

- `L01` acts on a pre-scale CSN class. Its conditional classification additionally assumes four
  dimensions, metric-only fields, locality, local diffeomorphism covariance, the named parity-even
  curvature-square inventory, and unrestricted variation before scale selection. Only inside that
  class is the bulk direction `C^2` and its metric equation Bach.
- `L02` starts after a physical metric representative and scale have somehow been selected. It then
  adds metric-only fields, locality, four-dimensional covariance, unrestricted metric variation,
  at-most-second-order equations, and Lovelock minimality. Those premises condition the EH bulk.
- `L03` would have to define the missing map between those stages. Selecting a representative does
  not by itself change a fourth-order Bach system into a second-order EH system. The bridge needs a
  selection functional, stagewise fields, variation rules, degree-of-freedom matching, boundary
  matching, and a regime theorem. None currently exists.

Consequently the three lanes are not three already-complete equations competing on equal footing.
They are three separately stamped research domains.
