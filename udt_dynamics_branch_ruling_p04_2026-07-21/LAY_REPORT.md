# P04 in lay language

We have now agreed on three workbenches. We have not declared that any one of them contains the laws
of UDT.

- The first asks what happens if the universe has a rule **before** physical size and scale are
  chosen. Under a strict list of added assumptions, that workbench contains the `C^2`/Bach bulk.
- The second asks what happens **after** the universe has selected an actual measurable metric and
  scale. Under another strict list of assumptions, that workbench contains the Einstein-Hilbert
  bulk used by GR.
- The third asks whether UDT itself supplies a bridge from the first stage to the second. At present
  that is an empty workbench with a label: we do not yet have the bridge rule.

The important safeguard is that we did not let a metric equation quietly throw away the other
possible ingredients. If `phi`, a frame, a projector, a constraint, or an independent connection is
a genuine field, it also needs an equation or a clearly justified role. We checked all three lanes
against all seven field possibilities, and none was deleted for convenience.

We also carried forward every unresolved global question—the universe's patching, angular mirror,
boundary behavior, topology, degeneracies, scale, and finite-cell closure. Nothing was filled in
with the usual GR answer.

So the accomplishment is organizational but scientifically important: the next derivations now
have honest walls. We can explore conditional laws without accidentally calling them UDT or hiding
the fields and boundary choices they fail to govern.
