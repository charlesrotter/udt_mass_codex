clock. Once the new observer moves, the slices of “same time” tilt through the depth direction. That
tilt is the off-diagonal metric term.

If the observer accelerates, the tilt changes with time. The changing tilt appears in the connection,
which is the metric's bookkeeping for how neighboring rulers and clocks change from event to event.

Nothing had to be imported from SR or GR. The terms follow by differentiating the UDT metric and the
observer's depth trajectory. More importantly, they cancel out of invariant scalar curvature. An

and accelerating frame coordinates; the timelike subset supports the conditional observer reading.
