# Lay readout

We separated two questions that had been getting tangled.

First: if somebody hands us a complete Hopf-shaped field, can its
topological winding be counted without first choosing UDT's physical
rule for transporting directions from place to place? **Yes**, within
the already declared carrier, domain, orientation, normalization, and
boundary assumptions. The winding count is built from the map itself
and ordinary exterior differentiation. Changing the still-open affine
connection does not change that count.

Second: does the metric thereby create that field, choose its physical
carrier, give it an energy, keep it stable in real time, or close the
finite-cell boundary? **No.** Those are different jobs. The choice of
metric changes the balance of the two- and four-derivative energy
terms. A sphere of possible directions is not yet one chosen field.
Static finite-box stability remains exactly as strong as it was, but
does not automatically transfer to another metric or to real time.

Bootstrap is indeed “hanging around” this work, but at the outside. The
registered bootstrap can eventually compare **finished candidate
universes** and reject those whose total proper density or global
closure is inconsistent. It cannot currently reach inside the
calculation and manufacture the missing carrier, connection, action,
or boundary condition.

The useful gain is that the open problem is smaller. The integer
winding itself is not waiting on the affine-connection selector. What
is still waiting is the metric-native route from the reciprocal
geometry to a particular physical Hopf field and its dynamics.
