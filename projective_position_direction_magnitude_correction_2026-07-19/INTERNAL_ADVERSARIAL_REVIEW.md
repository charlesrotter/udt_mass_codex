# Internal adversarial review

Date: 2026-07-19

Scope: overcorrection, category, and premise audit. This is an internal adversarial pass, not a newly
transmitted cold arm.

## Strongest objections

### 1. Does rejecting negative distance destroy the reciprocal group?

No. The group acts on an ordered comparison or oriented chart. Such a quantity may change sign when
the ordered pair or chosen-axis orientation reverses. Its magnitude is a separate object. The algebra
survives; only the physical-distance interpretation is withdrawn.

### 2. Are angular reversal and observer exchange the same operation?

No. Looking in the opposite angular direction at the same nonnegative depth keeps the depth fixed.
Exchanging which observer is first in a relational comparison negates the ordered comparison and
inverts its transformation. Conflating these operations caused the apparent direction-dependent
clock speed-up.

### 3. Did the correction incorrectly discard the existing clock branch?

The first draft risked doing so. The audited local Lorentzian temporal slot was already explicitly
`CHOSE`. With nonnegative radial depth it conditionally supplies `exp(-rho)`, which is invariant under
angular reversal and does not speed up as `rho` grows. This branch is retained as `CONDITIONAL`.

The `sech(delta)` and `sech(delta)^2` constructions establish only that Reciprocity, CSN, and
direction-evenness do not select an unconditional scalar readout without the temporal-slot premise.
They are not alternative UDT laws and do not refute the chosen-slot branch.

### 4. Is `abs(delta)` now the physical distance?

Not globally. It is the minimal magnitude of the ordered radial comparison. Two events can have the
same radial depth and nonzero transverse separation. A complete distance requires the weighted
radial term, angular metric, path, representative, and any necessary slicing. The independent
verifier includes an exact transverse-displacement witness with zero radial difference and nonzero
metric length.

### 5. Is `L dphi` itself proper radial length?

Not by itself. It is the additive-depth seed selected in the conditional full-frame family. The
displayed spatial metric contains the reciprocal weight `exp(2phi)`, so a chosen representative's
radial coframe carries `exp(phi)L dphi`. The correction report was narrowed accordingly.

### 6. Does taking an absolute value create a smooth field?

Not automatically. `abs(delta)` has a cusp when treated as a scalar on a signed line. It can instead
serve as a nonnegative radial coordinate whose origin is a coordinate boundary, but smooth Cartesian
or global completion then depends on the angular sector and regularity conditions. This is another
reason it remains a candidate rather than a theorem.

### 7. What does the odd static seal field mean now?

The sign may remain necessary on the doubled cover to encode the mirror involution and flux parity.
Physical separation on the quotient need not inherit that sign. The correction does not alter the
binding `Phi(S)=0` seal or its free normal derivative.

## Disposition

The correction survives after narrowing:

- signed reciprocal imbalance: oriented ordered-comparison chart;
- nonnegative `abs(delta)`: minimal radial-magnitude candidate, not global distance;
- direction: angular datum, not negative radius;
- chosen-slot clock factor `exp(-rho)`: `CONDITIONAL`;
- unconditional native clock extraction: `OPEN`;
- bounded projective radius: coordinate candidate, not proper-distance theorem.

The maximum grade is `VERIFIED-WITH-CAVEATS`. No action, angular completion, global distance,
unconditional clock observable, `X_max`, carrier, mass, or canon follows.
