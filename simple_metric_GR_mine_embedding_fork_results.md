## 1. Areal mass language → soft L

In GR, the serious quasi-local mass (Misner–Sharp, etc.) is built on **areal** radius: sphere area \(4\pi r^2\), compactness \(2m/r\).

On our reciprocal metric that compactness is exactly the residual flip:
\[
C=\frac{2m}{r}=1-A.
\]

**L says:** \(r/X = C\) — the mass-native radius is proportional to compactness used.  
**H says:** \(r/X = C/(2-C)\) — a Möbius warp of the same compactness.

If one privileges GR’s habit that **areal radius is the mass coordinate**, L is the direct paint:  
*sphere size tracks how close to critical you are* (scaled by the cosmic bound \(X\)).

Not a theorem. A **corpus alignment**.

## 3. Optical (Fermat) geometry → soft L (strong elegance)

For static metrics, light’s spatial Fermat metric scales as \(\mathrm{d}\ell_{\mathrm{opt}}\sim \mathrm{d}\ell_{\mathrm{proper}}/\sqrt{-g_{tt}}\).  
On the reciprocal metric that is
\[
\mathrm{d}\ell_{\mathrm{opt}}=\frac{\mathrm{d}r}{A}.
\]

**Under L** (\(A=1-r/X\)):
\[
\int\frac{\mathrm{d}r}{A}=-X\ln A=2X\,\phi.
\]
Optical radial length is **exactly proportional to dilation depth** \(\phi\).

**Under H**, \(\int\mathrm{d}r/A\) is not simply \(\propto\phi\) (CAS: messier closed form).

For a theory of **place + light**, “light’s optical depth *is* dilation depth” is a sharp elegance hit for L.
