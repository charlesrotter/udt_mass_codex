## Whole question and bounded regime

Across every retained complete metric-amplitude path and every registered native-instrument
projector motif, does the metric intrinsically distinguish a negative one-dimensional line, a
higher-dimensional Lorentzian subspace, or no temporal subspace at all? Where a local negative line
exists, does it persist through the full sampled path, registered chart/coframe transformations,
motif transitions, and current global gluing data? What lapse, shift, slicing, or optical connector
data genuinely follow?

This is a bounded kinematic atlas over the already retained finite registry and sampled paths. It is
not an action/EOM solve, not time evolution, and not a search for the line that gives the desired
two-ended accessibility behavior.

## Method and premise ledger

The audit is metric-led.

- `pinned-by-THEORY`: the retained full metric amplitudes and jets; the native instrument/projector
  algebra actually recorded in the source packages; exact metric-self-adjoint projector identities;
  the registered chart/coframe transformation laws; the existing finite-cell/cocycle taxonomy.
- `pinned-by-OBSERVATION`: terrestrial/solar `c_E`, used only if a physical normalization is already
  available. It cannot select a negative line.
- `POSIT/CHOSE`: the conditional Lorentzian quadratic readout and representative used by the parent
  atlases. This premise must remain visible.
- `free-and-explored`: every retained amplitude identity, instrument family, motif, path node,
  transition class, and registered coframe transformation.
- `OPEN`: time orientation, observer congruence, physical slicing, lapse, shift, global line-bundle
  orientation, physical scale, connector curve, action, carrier, source, boundary polarization, and
  information-transfer interpretation.
- `pinned-by-HABIT`: none may support a verdict. In particular, the coordinate called `t`, a
  spherical chart, zero shift, GR observers, the toric control, or the desired microscopic/cosmic
  result may not be privileged.

1. Reproduce the complete retained path count and every motif count without treating family
   presentations as independent mechanisms.
2. Determine the metric signature of every invariant block at every available path node, not only
   at the midpoint.
3. Match projectors continuously along stable paths and classify every appearance, loss, exchange,
   or unresolved transition of a negative line.
4. Test the line/subspace classification under every registered chart/coframe transformation. Raw
   component eigenvectors are not invariants; projector images and metric restrictions are.

   transport is not global holonomy.
6. Test whether current cocycle, seal-lift, orientation, and finite-cell data define a global line
   bundle, its time orientation, or neither.

   Only then may a hypersurface-orthogonal zero-shift threading be recorded.
8. Determine what can be reconstructed invariantly: a unit line up to sign, its spatial projector,
   or a complete lapse/shift. Lapse and shift must remain slicing-dependent unless the metric
   supplies a slicing.
9. Apply Reciprocity, CSN, finite cell, static seal, and current bootstrap separately; classify each
   as `SELECTS`, `CONSTRAINS_NOT_SELECTS`, `SILENT`, or `OPEN`.

## Fail-closed certification

The audit fails if it:

- omits or duplicates a frozen path/record;
- counts presentations as physical universes or mechanisms;
- uses a coordinate label or component sign to select time;
- promotes a Lorentzian two-plane or three-plane to a unique time line;
- ignores a path node, motif transition, rank/signature margin, or unresolved projector match;
- calls an unoriented line future-directed;
- calls an open-path line a global line bundle;
- calls Kato transport physical time evolution or global holonomy;
- infers zero shift without orthogonal-complement integrability and a selected slicing;
- infers lapse/shift from a line without the required chart/slicing data;
- treats the toric control, GR, SNe, a particle result, or the desired two-ended behavior as a
  selection filter;
- imports an action, carrier, source, boundary polarization, detector, or information law.

Required evidence includes base blobs/SHA-256 values, deterministic full-coverage outputs, raw
stdout/stderr, exact algebraic controls, independent implementation of the load-bearing signature
and line logic, exercised corruption catches, and a fresh adversarial review.
