## Lay interpretation

Imagine every location carrying a relational “depth coordinate” that can approach a universal
maximum reach but never exceed it. Ordinary distances cannot simply add near that limit. The chosen
fractional-linear addition law tells them how to combine without crossing the limit.

The remarkable part is that the familiar UDT dilation factor `A` automatically multiplies when these
depths combine. Turn the comparison around, and `A` becomes its reciprocal. Thus the maximum reach
and the inverse clock/ruler relationship are two faces of the same one-dimensional comparison
structure.

But a comparison rule is not yet the geometry of the whole universe. When the audit actually
re-centers the current metric, its time and radial pieces do not transform by the same common factor.
The comparison law and the current metric profile therefore fit algebraically without yet forming a
full positional-relativity symmetry.

This is useful contrast, not a dead end. It localizes the possible missing premises: why positional
composition should be fractional-linear/projective at all, and how that chosen comparison law acts
on the entire coframe/metric, including angular directions and the finite cell.

## Exact countermodel to uniqueness of the hyperbolic law

Let an additive coordinate `u in R` be compactified by

\[
 \xi=f(u)=\frac{u}{\sqrt{1+u^2}},
\]

and define composition by transporting ordinary addition:

\[
 \xi\boxplus\eta
 =f\!\left(f^{-1}(\xi)+f^{-1}(\eta)\right).
\]

This is a smooth, ordered, associative, commutative bounded group with identity `0`, inverse `-xi`,
and limiting endpoints `+-1`. But it is not the fractional-linear XR1 law. At the exact witness

\[

\[

which are unequal. Thus generic bounded-composition axioms do not force either `tanh` or the
multiplicative `A` character. The historical `P2` fractional-linear/projective premise is doing real
work.

## Which “reciprocal” maps fail

The literal arithmetic proposal

\[
 x\longmapsto\frac{X_{\max}^2}{x}
\]

is an involution on a larger nonzero domain, but it is not an internal maximum-reach reciprocity:
`x=X_max/2` maps to `2X_max`, outside the allowed interval. It also has no neutral `x=0` image.

The complement `x -> X_max-x` is an involution on the nonnegative interval, but moves the comparison
identity `0` to the boundary and does not preserve bounded composition. It is not positional
reversal.

A separate scale involution

\[
 \sigma\longmapsto\frac{X_{\max}^2}{\sigma}
\]

is mathematically valid on all positive scales and has fixed point `sigma=X_max`. But it sends a
submaximal scale to a super-`X_max` scale. It would require an operational scale domain extending on
both sides of the pivot, so it is a distinct scale-duality candidate rather than maximum-reach
reciprocity.

The internal reciprocity supported by the current bounded domain is therefore reversal
`xi -> -xi`, together with multiplicative inversion `A -> A^-1`.

## The full-metric symmetry test

Re-centering by `alpha=tanh(beta)` naturally acts as

\[
 \xi'=\frac{\xi-\alpha}{1-\alpha\xi},
 \qquad \phi'=\phi-\beta.
\]

For the pinned static reciprocal radial metric

\[
 ds^2=-A(\xi)c_0^2dt^2
 +X_{\max}^2A(\xi)^{-1}d\xi^2,
\]

the temporal pullback ratio with unchanged time is constant:

\[
 R_t=\frac{A(\xi')}{A(\xi)}
 =\frac{1+\alpha}{1-\alpha}.
\]

The radial ratio is

\[
 R_x=\frac{A(\xi)}{A(\xi')}
 \left(\frac{d\xi'}{d\xi}\right)^2
 =\frac{(1-\alpha)^3(1+\alpha)}{(1-\alpha\xi)^4}.
\]

It depends on position. For `alpha=1/3`,

\[
 R_x(0)=\frac{32}{81},
 \qquad
 R_x(1/2)=\frac{512}{625}.
\]

No constant rescaling of time can make both equal to the same conformal factor. Hence this
re-centering is neither an isometry nor a CSN equivalence of the pinned metric within this
transformation class.

This result does not rule out every possible field/frame realization. A position-dependent time
map, independent coframe gauge action, non-diagonal metric transformation, or transformation of
additional fields would define a different problem and may introduce cross terms. Current premises
do not select one.

## Angular sector: the promising conditional clue

The one-dimensional result alone does not determine the angular metric. Retain

\[
 d\ell^2=d\phi^2+f(\phi)^2d\Omega^2.
\]

The exact sectional curvatures are

\[
 K_{\rm radial}=-\frac{f''}{f},
 \qquad
 K_{\rm angular}=\frac{1-(f')^2}{f^2}.
\]

Three choices sharing the same radial `phi` coordinate give:

| `f(phi)` | radial curvature | angular curvature |
|---|---:|---:|
| `phi` | 0 | 0 |
| `sinh(phi)` | -1 | -1 |
| `sin(phi)` on a local positive interval | +1 | +1 |

Thus radial Xmax reciprocity does not select flat, hyperbolic, or spherical angular completion.

There is nevertheless a potentially important conditional clue. If one **chooses** the standard
Lorentz/negative-curvature embedding for three-dimensional positional re-centering, its

\[

\[

\[

Therefore Xmax may participate in global normalization, but it does not yet select the physical
metric representative or decide whether variation occurs before or after scale selection.
