## What the corrected map changes

The seal question cannot be reduced, before dynamics, to three named possibilities. Even the set
`phi=0` can locally or globally be:

- absent or all of a region;
- a regular hypersurface;
- a critical crossing or touching surface of arbitrary finite order;
- an isolated, branching, or positive-dimensional critical set;
- multiple disjoint or intersecting components;
- flat to every finite order;
- nonsmooth or distributional; or
- part of the required arbitrary closed/stratified remainder.

Those zero-set properties still do not determine the metric regularity there, the causal type of
the set, the angular behavior, the field/coframe transition, the global incidence, or the
variational role. Each is a separate axis.

Likewise, a label such as `boundary`, `cap`, `gluing`, or `quotient` describes only some global
incidence data. It does not specify the cover, transition map, isotropy representation, matching
order, angular collapse, field soldering, corners, or action-domain completion. Free actions and
fixed-set actions already have different local quotient types; more general actions and groupoids
form an unbounded remainder.
