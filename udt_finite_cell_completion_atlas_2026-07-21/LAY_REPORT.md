# Lay readout

We corrected the question before doing more physics.

The previous framing asked which of three pictures the edge of a UDT cell resembles. That was like
asking whether an unknown animal is a horse, a dog, or a bird before first checking whether those
are the only possibilities. They are not.

We have now made a neutral map of the kinds of mathematical information a complete finite UDT cell
would need. The map keeps ordinary boundaries, smooth continuations, glued regions, caps, mirrors,
quotients, moving surfaces, and much more—but treats them as examples, not finalists.

The strongest insight is that the missing space is not a short menu. Until UDT supplies its full
law, the surface where `phi` is zero can have many different shapes and degrees of smoothness. The
angular part of the metric can twist, shear, mix with time or depth, collapse in different ways, or
change topology. The global cell can be assembled through many kinds of covers and joins. These are
whole families of functions and topologies, not a few discrete choices.

We preserved all 89 local geometric categories found earlier and all known global degrees of
freedom. We did not throw away ugly, trivial, singular, time-dependent, or disconnected cases to
make the answer look physical.

What we did **not** accomplish is choosing the universe. We still do not have the native law that
says which member of this large map is realized. No action, boundary rule, scale, `X_max`, or matter
source was derived here. The next legitimate derivation must operate on this whole map or state a
clearly bounded slice; it cannot start by naming a desired seal and trying to prove it.
