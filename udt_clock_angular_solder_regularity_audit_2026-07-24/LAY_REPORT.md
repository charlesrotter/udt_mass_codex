# Lay report

We tried the most direct way of making the clock part and angular part of the
UDT metric use the very same “depth dial.”

It does not work on a smooth closed two-ended cell.

At one or both ends, the clock factor is driven to zero or infinity too
quickly. This is not just an awkward coordinate. A curvature measurement
blows up, so the completed spacetime itself is singular there. Changing the
relative strength of the clock and angular dials does not fix both ends.

This is useful because it removes a misleading shortcut.

`X_max` should be the greatest metric distance allowed between observers. A
greatest distance is naturally a relationship between two places, like the
diameter of a globe. It does not have to be the final number reached by one
smooth field drawn over every point of the globe.

That leaves several honest possibilities:

- the infinite-dilation end is approached but is not an ordinary included
  cap;
- different coordinate patches describe different parts of the cell;
- the clock and angular depth are related but not literally the same field;
- motion/shift terms complete the geometry; or
- the relevant depth belongs to an observer pair rather than to either
  observer alone.

The angular geometry could also make the limiting distance vary slightly by
direction. In that language there is a narrow set of directional limits and
one absolute largest value. The current round example has no such variation,
and we have not used CMB observations to manufacture one.

So we are closer to the proper definition of `X_max`, but we have not yet
calculated its value.
