# Internal adversarial review

This is not a fresh external-model review.

## Is the result just the earlier compact critical-point theorem?

No. The angular depth already diverges at the caps and evades the hypothesis
that `phi` is a smooth real scalar on the completed sphere. This audit
directly inserts the clock factor and calculates four-dimensional curvature.
The obstruction is the resulting lapse behavior, not merely the existence of
a critical point.

## Was only the convenient Hopf normalization tested?

No. The relative angular unit `kappa` remains arbitrary over all finite
positive values. One cap can be regular at `kappa=1`; the other has lapse
power `1+1/kappa`, which never vanishes.

## Could a common factor repair the result?

Not while retaining the same regular physical caps. A smooth finite positive
factor adds finite conformal-derivative terms and cannot cancel the
`1/rho^2` scalar-curvature pole. A factor with a zero or pole changes the
physical completion and remains open as a different family.

This distinction is mandatory because strong local CSN is challenged and no
common physical factor was discarded as gauge.

## Could a crossed coframe pairing evade the result?

For generic `kappa`, fixed-pairing invariance permits only the two opposite
weight blocks. At `kappa=1`, crossed pairings exist between the two
equal-weight planes, but they do not change the diagonal metric coefficients.
They are coframe-lift alternatives, not new metric regularity witnesses.

## Does the result exclude open or time-live branches?

No. Arbitrary positive open profiles remain infinite in number. Shifted,
time-live, nonintegrable, patchwise, and pair-space realizations were
explicitly outside the bounded family and remain open.

## Does it disprove `X_max`?

No. It rejects one one-field realization. It strengthens the need to derive
`X_max` as a global observer-pair diameter from the complete metric.

## Ruling

The two-cap obstruction is exact in its bounded class. The global ontology
and value of `X_max` remain open.
