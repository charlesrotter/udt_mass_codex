## Whole question

Did registered UDT foundations derive physical Common-Scale Neutrality (CSN),
or did Reciprocity only determine opposed clock/ruler dilation while leaving a
common metric factor unselected?

The audit must distinguish:

1. a physical clock-distance calibration by observed Einsteinian `c`;
2. ordinary global freedom to change measurement units;
3. an undetermined common factor in a derivation;
4. global-constant common rescaling as physical equivalence;
5. position-dependent conformal rescaling as physical equivalence;
6. later selection of a calibrated physical metric.

It must determine whether the repository silently moved from item 3 to item 4
or 5 without an additional premise.

## Metric-led frame

This is a premise/provenance audit of the registered UDT metric construction.

two-stage bridge, or any desired matter result.

physical scale, or new field equation may be supplied.

## Falsification and certification contract

The challenge to strong CSN is falsified only if the frozen foundation derives
physical equivalence under the stated rescaling class without assuming that
equivalence or importing a later scale selector.

Strong CSN is premise-dependent rather than derived if:

1. it is stated as a founding postulate;
2. Reciprocity fixes only a ratio/product or normalized pair;
3. the common factor is discarded, normalized, or quotiented without a
   separately derived physical-equivalence argument; or
4. recovery of a calibrated physical metric requires an unprovided selector.

The audit is inconclusive if the rescaling class or the operational meaning of
the metric is not stated precisely enough to distinguish these cases.

Certification additionally requires:

1. a frozen source manifest;
2. exact quotations kept within repository evidence and mapped to claims;
3. production and independent algebra implementations;
4. a downstream premise-impact ledger;
5. catch-proofs rejecting conflation of undetermined, normalized, and gauge;
6. unchanged frozen packages and repository baseline.
