# Lay readout

The earlier calculation accidentally pictured one giant spherical map
with a fixed center and then imagined observers moving inward or outward
on that map. That is not your UDT premise.

In the corrected picture, every unaccelerated observer has an equivalent
map centered on themselves. Distance is the separation shown on that
observer's map; it is not distance from an absolute center of the
universe.

Three results now separate cleanly.

## 1. `Xmax` really is the limit of one observer's clock-and-ruler map

As separation approaches `Xmax`, the remote clock relation slows toward
zero and optical depth grows without limit. At the limit, the metric's
clock and distance channels break down. Continuing the formula beyond it
would exchange their roles: the clock direction would behave like space
and the distance direction like time.

So you cannot cross `Xmax` while keeping the same observer-centered
meaning of clock and distance.

## 2. A mathematical continuation is not a travelling observer

Mathematicians can rewrite the tensor so that it continues beyond the
surface. That only says the abstract geometry has a continuation. It
does not produce a person travelling through the edge, because UDT has
not supplied the relation between two independently centered observer
maps.

The previous “crossing observer” conclusion is therefore withdrawn.

## 3. One fixed WR-L sphere cannot be everybody's recentered map

The curvature of the fixed WR-L metric changes with distance from its
center. Curvature is something a coordinate relabelling cannot change.
Therefore moving the center is not a mere coordinate trick: one fixed
WR-L geometry has a mathematically identifiable center.

Since UDT forbids a preferred inertial observer, the WR-L map cannot be
one universal sphere centered on somebody special. It must be a
relational map indexed by the observer, or one part of a more complete
metric whose observer-composition rule is still missing.

## Acceleration

Uniform relative motion preserves the local cone. A changing or
accelerating coordinate frame can tilt how that cone is drawn and adds
connection terms, but changing the frame alone does not change the
underlying curvature.

For acceleration to physically warp the metric, UDT still needs to
derive the response law relating acceleration to a changed metric. We
have not imported the GR equivalence principle.

## Bottom line

Your intuition was substantially right: the earlier crossing conclusion
came from treating an observer-centered distance as though it were an
absolute radial track.

The repaired result is:

```text
Xmax is the asymptotic limit of each admissible observer-centered
clock/ruler chart.
```

What remains is not another horizon calculation. It is the exact rule
that relates one observer-centered map to another while preserving the
full radial and angular geometry.
