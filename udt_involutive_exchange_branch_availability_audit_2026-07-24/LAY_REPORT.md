# Lay report

We checked whether the answer was already sitting somewhere in the work we
have completed.

The answer is: **not quite—but now we know exactly why.**

One existing branch comes very close. It has a real equation and a real
mathematical solution with the right kind of smoothly closing angular
geometry. But that equation only sees the metric. It cannot tell which way
the underlying angular frame is exchanged. Several different frame
arrangements look identical to it.

The newer geometry solves the opposite half of the problem. It tells us
exactly what happens if the correct exchange and the correct two caps are
present: the Hopf-like bundle follows cleanly. But it does not tell us why
the universe must choose that exchange and those caps, and it has no native
equation saying that this is the realized branch.

So the two halves are:

- an equation with a solution that is blind to the needed frame choice; and
- a beautiful frame/topology structure with no native equation selecting it.

We also checked the time-dependent, spherical, macro/SNe, particle, boundary,
bootstrap, and older branches. None already joins those halves.

This is progress because it gives us permission to stop drilling into the
same seam. We are not one more routine calculation away from closure under
the present premises. The next useful step is to step back and ask whether
one foundational UDT idea has been stated too weakly—or interpreted at the
wrong level—so that the metric's equation side and frame/topology side have
been artificially separated.
