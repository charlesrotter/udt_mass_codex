## Why it still fails

Five decisive gates remain non-`YES`.

1. `G02 native_action_variation = CONDITIONAL`

   The `C^2` bulk is unique only inside the already named four-dimensional,
   metric-only, local, parity-even, lowest-curvature-square,
   unrestricted-pre-scale-variation class. That is not a complete native UDT
   action.

2. `G03 full_on_shell_branch = CONDITIONAL`

   The round branch is on shell for that conditional bulk, not for a complete
   native action with all fields, boundary terms, source, and global
   bootstrap closure.

3. `G05 global_angular_exchange_lift = OPEN`

   The metric-only action is exactly blind to coframe sign. The stationary
   package reports metrically indistinguishable seal lifts. Hence its on-shell
   status cannot select the needed angular exchange.

4. `G07 cocycle_monodromy_compatibility = OPEN`

   The bounded ansatz does not derive the complete coframe cocycle or prove
   that one involutive exchange descends through the physical cell.

5. `G09 exchange_cap_compatibility = OPEN`

   Smooth primitive caps exist in the mathematical ansatz, but no native rule
   joins those caps to a selected exchange lift. The later FC04/Hopf theorem
   proves what follows if the join is supplied; it does not retroactively
   supply it.

The physical finite-cell boundary/polarization is also only conditional:
the stationary solve used mathematical smooth caps, while the repository
explicitly leaves the physical wall/seal completion open.

## Why cross-family assembly does not close it

One might try to take:

- B19's conditional on-shell Bach solution;
- B24's conditional involutive exchange;
- B18's conditional FC04 cap theorem; and
- B23's bootstrap principle.

That is not one branch. The pieces use separately supplied variation,
coframe, lift, cap, section, and boundary premises. No evidence proves they
coexist in one complete solution, and several source packages explicitly
warn that the metric-only action cannot distinguish the coframe lifts.

The combination is therefore a research diagram, not a derived universe.

## Stop condition

The existing repository has reached a real selector boundary:

- equation-side evidence does not choose the coframe/global lift;
- coframe/topology evidence does not supply native equations;
- bootstrap currently filters complete solutions after the fact and cannot
  join the two sides before such solutions exist.

Another local selector test against the same premises would likely restate
this separation. Moving forward requires either a genuinely new
metric-derived global law or a corrected understanding of an existing
foundational premise—not another conditional splice.
