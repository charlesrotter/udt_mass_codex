# Lay report

The complete metric contains more intrinsic structure than a preferred
direction, but less than a finished Hopf carrier.

The clearest new result uses the way `phi` changes, not a named coordinate
axis. Wherever `phi` has a definite nonzero gradient that is not lightlike,
the metric itself can separate all possible oriented area elements into two
equal three-dimensional families:

- area elements with one leg along the `phi` direction; and
- area elements lying entirely across it.

This split does not depend on how we label or rotate the local measuring
frame. It also survives a common rescaling of the metric. The metric's Hodge
duality swaps the two families exactly. Therefore one family can carry
`e^phi` while the other carries `e^-phi`, and Hodge exchange turns that
reciprocal operation into its inverse.

The geometry does not yet decide which family gets which sign. Swapping the
assignments simply replaces the operation by its inverse, and both choices
pass the same geometric tests. That physical ownership remains open.

That is a real geometric echo of UDT Reciprocity hiding in the full metric
plus `phi`. It is not yet the final answer:

- it exists only where the gradient of `phi` is nonzero and not lightlike;
- it degenerates rather than continuing smoothly when that gradient becomes
  lightlike—where the surviving two-form operation is rank-two and
  nilpotent rather than a `3+3` split—and disappears when the gradient is
  zero;
- the present UDT premises do not prove that one allowed finite-cell solution
  stays in the good regime everywhere;
- it divides possible area elements but does not select one actual area
  direction or one field;
- even on a timelike branch, where a round sphere of transverse directions
  exists naturally, the metric still gives the whole sphere rather than a
  chosen point on it; and
- no Hopf map, global circle bundle, boundary framing, or Hopf number follows.

So the answer is no longer simply “the metric has no intrinsic reciprocal
reduction.” A precise local reduction does exist. The honest remaining
question is whether the complete finite-cell geometry keeps it globally
well-defined and then selects a section or quotient. The existing conditional
toric Hopf construction still supplies an exact global model if its circles,
caps, periods, orientation, and quotient are given, but this audit did not
derive those inputs or connect them uniquely to the new two-form split.

No action, matter carrier, source, mass, scale, or dynamics was obtained.
