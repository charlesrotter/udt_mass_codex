## Dimensional lead, not a derivation

If \(c\), \(G\), and a natively defined total mass are the only dimensional inputs, dimensional
analysis permits

\[
X_{\max}=\alpha\,\frac{G M_{\rm total}}{c^2},
\]

where \(\alpha\) is dimensionless. Dimensional analysis does not determine \(\alpha\), prove that
these are the correct inputs, or establish that \(G\) and \(M_{\rm total}\) are already independent
native quantities. No Schwarzschild factor, including \(\alpha=2\), may be imported.

A possible whole-system closure would instead be implicit:

\[
X_{\max}
=\alpha\,\frac{G\,M_{\rm total}[X_{\max}]}{c^2}.
\]

This becomes meaningful only after UDT supplies a native action, mass definition, and total-mass
functional. It is not evidence merely because it is dimensionally possible.

## Global constants need not be primitive inputs

Owner PONDER, 2026-07-15: \(X_{\max}\) may be as permanent and universal within the realized
universe as \(c\), while both numerical values may be fixed by the universe's total mass and size.
Record this as a **LEAD**, not a derived relation and not a decision that \(c\) varies locally.

\[
\boxed{
\text{operationally universal constant}
\;\not\Rightarrow\;
\text{ontologically independent primitive}.
}
\]

If \(c\) is also a whole-universe output, the dimensional relation should be written as the single
dimensionless compactness

\[
\chi_U=\frac{G M_{\rm total}}{c^2X_{\max}}.
\]

Equations such as

\[
c^2=\beta\,\frac{G M_{\rm total}}{X_{\max}}
\qquad\Longleftrightarrow\qquad
X_{\max}=\beta\,\frac{G M_{\rm total}}{c^2}
\]

are the same one relation, not two independent derivations. They determine only
\(\chi_U=1/\beta\). A second independent native closure is required to determine both \(c\) and
\(X_{\max}\), and the dimensionless \(\beta\) must emerge rather than be fitted or imported.

The admissible target is therefore a coupled global-eigenvalue problem in which \(c\) and
\(X_{\max}\) remain constant across the realized solution, while the full UDT action, carrier (if
any), and whole-system regularity determine their values. No local \(c(x)\) or \(X_{\max}(x)\) is
implied.

## Falsification gates

The mass-closure lead fails if:

1. \(M_{\rm total}\) cannot be defined without already assuming \(X_{\max}\) in a circular,
   non-variational way;
2. the closure has no positive solution, an arbitrary continuum, or requires a fitted \(\alpha\);
3. different subsystems demand different values of the allegedly universal \(X_{\max}\);
4. the relation requires an imported gravitational mass or boundary normalization;
5. the resulting solution does not make \(X_{\max}\) unattainable under the full time-live
   dynamics.
6. treating \(c\) and \(X_{\max}\) as joint outputs supplies only the one dimensional relation
   above and no independent equation fixing the pair;
7. either “constant” must be varied locally or fitted separately across systems to make the
   closure work.
