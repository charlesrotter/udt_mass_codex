# What the metric did across ordinary and extreme regimes

## Result first

The static spherical UDT metric does **not** show a hidden change of observer-frame law at any
ordinary, finite value of `phi`. Wherever the metric remains finite and nondegenerate, it still has
one time direction and three space directions. Different observers can continue to describe the
same geometry without one observer becoming fundamental.

But the full metric did reveal something important that the clock–radial pair alone conceals:

> The angular sector forces the clock–radial imbalance to return to balance at a smooth spherical
> center.

Within this static, spherical, smooth class, a regular center requires

```text
A(0)=1,   A'(0)=0,
```

where `A=exp(-2 phi)`. In terms of `phi`, this is

```text
phi(0)=0,   phi'(0)=0,
```

and the first allowed departure is quadratic in radius.

## Why the angular sector changes the answer

If we look only at time and radial distance, a constant nonzero imbalance can be hidden by changing
the units of those two coordinates. That two-dimensional piece is flat.

The complete metric also contains spheres whose circumference and area are set by the areal radius.
Those spheres compare the radial ruler with angular circumference. If the reciprocal imbalance is
constant but nonzero, that comparison no longer fits smoothly: the four-dimensional geometry is
curved, and the curvature diverges at an areal center.

This is an exact example of the “orchestra” effect. The angular instruments make a fact visible that
the clock–radial instrument cannot reveal by itself.

## What happens in each direction

- **Every finite `phi` at a regular nonzero radius:** no signature or local frame-type flip. The
  coordinate light slope changes, while locally normalized metric-null speed remains the observed
  baseline `c_E`.
- **Finite negative `phi` away from a center:** allowed kinematically. It changes curvature according
  to the profile but does not itself flip time and space.
- **`phi -> -infinity` at a fixed finite areal radius:** `A -> infinity`, and the angular curvature
  necessarily diverges. In this static spherical class it is a singular endpoint, not a new regular
  frame regime.
- **A regular areal center:** `phi` must return to zero with zero first radial slope. Thus this branch
  approaches the ordinary balanced tangent geometry at its exact center.
- **`phi -> +infinity` at a finite radius:** `A -> 0`. The endpoint can be regular or singular
  depending on derivatives and extension. The recorded WR-L profile is the regular-horizon control:
  its static observer frame ends, but an ingoing Lorentzian frame crosses the surface.

## Reciprocity is not the same as flipping the whole metric

The reciprocal measurement law remains founded. But replacing `phi` by `-phi` is not generally a
symmetry of the complete static spherical metric. It swaps the clock and radial weights while
leaving the areal angular sector fixed; the resulting scalar curvature is usually different.

That is not a failure of Reciprocity. It says Reciprocity relates the paired measures, not that an
entire universe with `phi` is automatically identical to one with `-phi`.

## What this says about micro UDT

It does not yet tell us the microscopic frame law, because UDT has not derived the scale or complete
time-live/nonspherical geometry that identifies a physical micro regime.

It does tell us that simply extrapolating the static spherical macro profile inward is not enough.
The WR-L linear profile is singular at its areal center, as previously recorded. A smooth static
spherical completion would have to turn over to the quadratic balanced behavior above, or the true
micro geometry must leave this static spherical class.

So we followed the metric rather than assuming the answer. In this branch the metric says:

```text
no regular finite-phi frame flip;
ordinary reciprocal balance at a smooth areal center;
static-frame termination at a regular positive-phi horizon;
curvature singularity at a negative-phi infinite endpoint of finite areal radius.
```

Time-live and nonspherical sectors remain free to reveal a different invariant structure later.
