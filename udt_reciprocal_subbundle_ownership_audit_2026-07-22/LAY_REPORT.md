# Lay report — where the reciprocal pair lives

## What we learned

UDT already has the reciprocal rule itself: one member of the clock/ruler pair changes by the
inverse of the other, and `phi` measures that imbalance.

What UDT does **not yet specify** is where that two-part instrument is mounted inside the complete
four-dimensional geometry.

The finite-cell seal helps, but not enough. It can identify the outward direction. Even after we
also supply the metric, the direction of time, the mirror, `phi=0`, and a possible `phi` slope
pointing outward, there are still many equally valid local clock directions that can be paired with
outward distance. Exact algebra shows that none of those clock/ruler planes is left uniquely fixed
by all the supplied information.

That is the central result. It is not a failure of Reciprocity. It is a missing **wiring rule**
between Reciprocity and the complete metric.

## What the angular sector is doing

The angular sector is not merely decoration. Its remaining freedom can mix with the possible clock
direction while leaving the metric, seal, and outward direction unchanged. That is why naming a
“time/radial” pair in a convenient chart does not make it an intrinsic object.

There is one positive clue. If the angular part of the seal is specifically a mirror reflection,
the screen metric supplies the complementary reciprocal angular direction, apart from an overall
sign. But other currently allowed seal actions do not supply it. UDT has not yet selected the
reflection-type seal or told us how to carry its direction through the whole cell.

## Why the other structures do not finish the job

- CSN removes common calibration; it does not choose directions.
- Curvature can mark directions at some uneven points, but several pairings can survive, and the
  markings disappear at symmetric points.
- The complete registered curvature ensemble is either fully mixing or completely silent, neither
  of which marks one lasting two-plane.
- Global cocycle rules tell an already chosen pair how it may glue across charts; they do not choose
  the pair.
- Projector transport carries an already chosen plane; it does not create one.
- Bootstrap currently judges complete solutions after they exist; it supplies no calculation that
  chooses the wiring.

## The possible conceptual point hiding in plain sight

We may have been assuming too quickly that the “length side” of reciprocal `c` must be one tangent
line in the full geometry. Current UDT does not derive that assumption.

Two possibilities remain genuinely open:

1. UDT has a preferred clock-and-depth structure—possibly related to co-presence and the finite
   cell—that has not yet been stated precisely enough to supply the wiring.
2. The length side is realized by a broader spatial/coframe object rather than one selected line,
   so a rank-two subbundle is the wrong final language.

This audit does not choose between them.

## Honest status

Reciprocal kinematics remains derived with its recorded premises. The physical reciprocal
subbundle, its soldering into the metric, and its cell-wide transport remain `OPEN / NOT DERIVED`.
Therefore `phi` cannot yet be declared a universally metric-derived eigenvalue ratio, and a
time-live solve is still premature.

The next useful step is an owner-level meaning decision about the physical codomain of reciprocal
`c`, not another search through local curvature formulas.
