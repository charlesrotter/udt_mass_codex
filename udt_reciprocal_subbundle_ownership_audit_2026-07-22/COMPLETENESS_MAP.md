# Completeness map

This audit is one configuration-ownership tile. It does not solve a variational or dynamical system.

| Criterion | Covered here | Dropped or still open |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Fields | The abstract reciprocal pair, candidate rank-two subbundle, soldering, and transport are separately typed. | Metric-only, coframe, constrained, or metric-plus-`phi` off-shell census remains unselected. |
| 2. Action terms | None adopted. | Complete native bulk and boundary action. |
| 3. Full equations | No equation is inferred from kinematics. | Realization relation, metric/`phi` equations, constraints, source, and boundary response. |
| 4. Domain/charts | Exact pointwise and admitted symmetric-seal controls; prior chart/coframe atlas reused. | Arbitrary four-metrics, nonlinear global charts, singular distributions, and a realized cell. |
| 5. Boundary/regularity | Static `phi=0`, free normal derivative, and every registered complete seal-lift class are retained. | Selected lift, full jet matching, polarization, corners, and differentiable boundary completion. |
| 6. Topology | Existing completion/cocycle families are audited as nonselectors. | One selected cover, seam, cap, quotient, orientation, or topology. |
| 7. Dynamical character | None. | Native physical time, characteristics, constraint propagation, and evolution. |
| 8. Branches | Metric/orientation/gradient/curvature/coframe/seal/transport/bootstrap selector classes are classified. | Higher-jet, nonlocal spectral, torsionful, action-derived, and time-live selectors. |
| 9. Stability | Not used as a merit filter. | Stability of any future realized metric/matter branch. |
| 10. Regime/scale | Dimensionless reciprocal structure and CSN behavior only. | Physical representative, absolute scale, `X_max`, density closure, mass, and extreme-regime readout. |

The exact boundary stabilizer counterexample is enough to show that the **current premises do not
universally entail** a rank-two reciprocal subbundle. It is not a classification of all possible
future UDT realization laws. Every dropped criterion could add structure and therefore remains an
explicit blind spot rather than a negative theorem.

All checks characterize admissible structure. None rejects a branch for failing to resemble a
particle, Hopfion, preferred topology, smooth cap, GR solution, or observed universe.
