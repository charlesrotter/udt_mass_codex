## Owner clarification being tested

The proposed microscopic effect is not a new channel, coupling, carrier, or local scalar speed law.
It is the owner's claim that the existing UDT reciprocal metric already has a two-ended limit:

- ordinary terrestrial/solar frames occupy the finite measured `c_E` anchor regime;
- at the outer dilation extreme, causal accessibility relative to ordinary clocks can tend to zero;
- when two adjacent frames and the connecting metric segment jointly occupy a shrinking microscopic
  regime, causal accessibility relative to ordinary clocks can tend to infinity.

The microscopic limit is a limit of characteristic regime scale across metric configurations. It is
not identified with spherical areal radius, a regular center, signed distance, or mere coincidence of
the two observers.

family parameter `sigma > 0` and keep the dimensionless adjacency ratio

```text

```

finite and nonzero while `sigma -> 0`. Here `L_AB` is a metric-defined slice length only where the
required frame/slicing exists. If no invariant or registered construction supplies `sigma`, the
physical micro-regime map remains `OPEN`; formal family limits will be labeled conditional.

At minimum classify:

- both endpoints and the full connector in the microscopic regime;
- microscopic endpoints with a nonmicroscopic connector;
- one microscopic and one ordinary endpoint;
- both ordinary;
- the reversed comparison `A <-> B`;
- both reciprocal wings `phi -> +/- infinity`;
- finite, zero, divergent, oscillatory/path-dependent, degenerate, and curvature-singular classes.

No class is rejected merely for being singular, nonparticle-like, or unlike the expected universe.
