## The strongest mirror-fold challenge

A tempting shortcut is:

> A fixed surface of an ordinary metric reflection is totally geodesic, so the mirror seal must have
> `K_ij=0`.

That theorem is valid when its premise—a full metric reflection isometry preserving the tangential
slots—is supplied. Current UDT does not supply that premise.

For an allowed odd profile `phi=a n`, the reciprocal clock coefficient is

`h_tt=-exp(-2 a n)`.

At the seal its normal derivative is `2a`, which is generically nonzero because the canon explicitly
leaves `phi'` free. Replacing the actual sector-split fold by an ordinary even tangential metric would
silently remove that allowed flux branch. The full action on the angular and time-on coframe is also
open.

Therefore `K_ij=0` is **not derived** from the word “mirror.”

## Reciprocity supplies an exact clue, but not the missing involution

For the two-channel reciprocal character

`D(phi)=diag(exp(-phi),exp(phi))`

and the dual-pairing swap

`J=[[0,1],[1,0]]`,

the exact identity is

`J D(phi) J = D(-phi)`.

This is an elegant algebraic inversion witness. But with the Lorentzian two-channel form
`eta=diag(-1,1)`,

`J^T eta J = -eta`.

The raw swap is therefore not a Lorentzian isometry, and the minus sign cannot be absorbed by the
positive rescalings allowed by CSN. It also says nothing about the angular or time-on sectors.

This pinpoints the next missing conceptual object: a complete metric/coframe seal involution, if UDT
has one, must do more than reuse the algebraic dual-pairing matrix.
