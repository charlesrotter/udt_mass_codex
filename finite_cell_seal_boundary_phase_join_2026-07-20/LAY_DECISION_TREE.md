# Lay Decision Tree

## Did the finite-cell seal connect to the boundary variation?

Yes, but only partly.

The static mirror says the dilation imbalance `phi` is zero at the seal. Variations that preserve
the mirror therefore keep that one imbalance at zero. Its outward slope remains free.

## Does “mirror” force the whole boundary to have zero slope?

No. That would require an ordinary reflection symmetry of the complete metric. UDT currently gives
a sector-specific `phi -> -phi` rule and explicitly permits nonzero `phi'`.

## Does reciprocity provide the missing full reflection?

It provides a clue: swapping the two reciprocal channels changes `phi` to `-phi` exactly. But that
raw swap also reverses the sign of the Lorentzian two-channel metric, so it is not by itself a
physical metric reflection. The angular and time-on sectors are also absent.

## What remains open?

Almost the entire boundary control panel: overall scale, angular shape, off-diagonal terms, most
normal slopes, conjugate momenta, corners, and charge normalization.

## What comes next?

Test whether the complete UDT coframe derives a more complete seal involution. If it does not, future
nonlinear solves must compare multiple boundary prescriptions instead of assuming one.
