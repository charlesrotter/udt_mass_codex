# Lay decision tree

## What did the metric itself do?

- A perfectly symmetric angular metric did **not** choose two reciprocal directions.
- Uneven Hessian or curvature can mark directions locally, but the marks vanish at symmetric or
  degenerate points and different tensors can disagree.
- Therefore ordinary local curvature is not the missing universal selector.

## What did the finite-cell seal do?

- If its angular action is identity, it supplies no second reciprocal pair.
- If its angular action is a reflection, the metric supplies the perpendicular reflection and
  leaves only a sign/exchange choice.
- This is an exact conditional boundary result, including nonzero metric cross-coupling.

## Why is that not closure?

- UDT has not selected which complete angular seal action is physical.
- Boundary directions do not automatically extend consistently through a curved interior.
- Consistent extension requires a reduced holonomy/transport law that current UDT has not derived.

## Honest decision

Do not adopt the second pair yet. The next conceptual gate is whether complete Reciprocity itself
requires a reflection-type seal and global integrability of the metric-derived boundary pair.

If yes, the second pair is downstream geometry. If no, the founding transverse-zero realization
remains primary and the Hopf correspondence remains conditional.
