# The map in plain language

The main correction is simple: we should stop asking one narrow version of the metric to reveal the whole theory.

Think of the metric as a machine with ten geometric controls at every point. Three describe the two-dimensional clock/depth block, three describe the two-dimensional transverse or “angular” shape, and four describe how those two blocks slide, twist, and tilt through each other. The familiar diagonal, static, round form turns most of those controls off. It is a useful test case, but it is not the whole machine.

There is an even earlier issue. Calling those controls “clock/depth” and “angular” already assumes that spacetime has been split into a reciprocal two-plane and its transverse screen. UDT has promising local ways to describe such a split, but has not uniquely selected one everywhere. Therefore the honest parent map starts with the complete four-dimensional coframe/metric and treats the `2+2` picture as a very useful conditional coordinate system beneath it.

The exploration then has three different floors:

1. **What geometry can be written?** This is configuration space. It keeps every metric component, dependence, twist, shear, chart, topology, boundary class, and possible relation between `phi` and the metric.
2. **What geometry satisfies the actual UDT premises?** This is constrained space. Reciprocity, Common-Scale Neutrality, finite-cell structure, the limited static seal statement, and owner corrections may remove possibilities—but only when a current source actually says so.
3. **What geometry solves a law?** This is dynamical solution space. It cannot be entered honestly until the fields, action or other admissibility law, variation domain, source, and boundary terms are specified. UDT's complete native law is still open, so this floor remains a set of explicitly conditional lanes.

Algebra, ODEs, and time-dependent calculations have different jobs:

- Algebra maps local possibilities, degeneracies, and exact branches without assuming motion.
- ODEs can explore static, stationary, or spatially homogeneous slices after those reductions are declared. They can be deep and useful, but they cannot cover the complete metric.
- Time-dependent physics ultimately requires a selected full nonlinear law, its constraints, and PDE/global work. Merely letting a formula depend on time does not create physical evolution.

The angular sector is not an appendix. Its area, anisotropic shape, moving axes, twist, and four cross-sector shifts are mapped alongside `phi`. Mixed `phi`–angular curvature is a native geometric observable, but geometry alone does not say that any such component must vanish or take a preferred value.

The map deliberately keeps ugly outcomes. Flat, trivial, singular, divergent, oscillatory, disconnected, type-changing, unresolved, and solver-failed branches all remain evidence. A branch is never discarded because it fails to look like a particle, GR, a stable universe, or an expected answer.

GR, empirical data, Hopfion/carrier diagnostics, and mass/stability questions appear only after a bounded atlas is frozen. They can tell us what a UDT branch resembles or fails to resemble. They cannot reach backward and choose what the metric was allowed to do.

What has been accomplished here is the research blueprint and its safety audit—not the exploration itself. If Charles accepts the frame, the smallest next step is to build a law-neutral geometry evaluator that can represent and independently check all ten conditional `2+2` slots while still retaining the abstract whole-frame parent. No action, ODE, PDE, or GPU run is authorized by this package.
