# Lay decision tree

## What did the wall flux turn out to be?

Imagine measuring flow through a series of nested spheres.

```text
Is the measured flow the same through every sphere?
|
+-- No. It grows steadily from zero at the center to -2*pi*X at the wall.
    |
    +-- Therefore it is not an empty-space conserved charge by itself.
    |
    +-- What accounts for the growth?
        The WR-L metric's curvature between the spheres. The clock field obeys
        an exact Poisson-like clock-curvature balance on this branch.
```

The wall value is therefore the total accumulated geometric budget of the static WR-L region. That
is more informative than an arbitrary boundary number, but UDT still needs a native law saying
whether this budget is mass and how to normalize it.

## Can `c` and `G` turn it into mass?

```text
First choose a native action and charge rule.
|
+-- Not chosen -> c and G cannot tell us which geometric quantity is mass.
|
+-- Chosen and matched to ordinary gravity -> G can calibrate its normalization.
    The wall mass would then have the general form gamma*c^2*X/G.
```

The coefficient `gamma` must come from UDT, not from a familiar GR formula.

## Does that determine the size of the universe?

```text
Combine M=gamma*c^2*X/G with X=alpha*G*M/c^2.
|
+-- alpha*gamma is not 1 -> no positive homogeneous solution.
|
+-- alpha*gamma is 1 -> the two statements are the same equation.
    Every rescaled M and X remains possible.
```

So the wall flux can determine compactness, but not absolute size.

## What would finish the job?

One genuinely independent ingredient must be derived from the complete universe:

- a matter mass that does not simply grow in direct proportion to `X`;
- a boundary eigenvalue equation selecting one scale; or
- an actual physical density center selected by stable matter and global closure.

This is exactly where the owner bootstrap idea can enter—but the theory must calculate that datum,
not insert it.
