## D6.3 Carrier-covariance route

### Step 1 — identify the conditional continuum object

The relevant conditional four-dimensional minimal physical-metric completion is

\[
S_m^{\rm min}
=-\int d^4x\,\sqrt{-g}\left[
\frac{\xi}{2}g^{\mu\nu}\partial_\mu\mathbf n\cdot\partial_\nu\mathbf n
+\frac{\kappa_4}{4}F_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu}
\right].
\]

This is not supplied by the three-dimensional static energy alone. It chooses a four-dimensional measure, time sector, metric contraction, unit-vector representative, minimal coupling, and unrestricted metric variation.

### Step 2 — what four-dimensional covariance does after the action is chosen

Once that action is chosen, covariance fixes how its indices contract and how its metric stress tensor is computed. In the static sector, unrestricted metric variation gives

\[
\rho+S=2\rho_4.
\]

This is a valid conditional theorem. It is a classification of the chosen completion, not a derivation of that completion from positional dilation.

### Step 3 — choices covariance does not make

Covariance alone does not choose:

- the field's conformal/dilation weight or whether a compensator is present;
- whether \(\mathbf n\) is fundamental S2 data, an RP2 axis, an oriented lift, or only a metric parametrization;
- minimal versus nonminimal curvature terms;
- the lapse, shift, and time-derivative sector;
- the measure or preferred foliation in a non-four-dimensionally-covariant completion;
- the physical representative within a conformal/common-scale class;
- whether \(\phi\) and \(g_{\mu\nu}\) are independent off shell;
- whether reciprocity is imposed before variation, after unrestricted variation, or with a constraint.

Calling one of these choices “natural” is **HABIT**, not a derivation.

### Step 4 — position-dependent dilation does not remove the freedom

In a static reciprocal representative,

\[
N=e^{-\phi},\qquad \gamma_{\parallel\parallel}=e^{2\phi}.
\]

The lapse and longitudinal determinant factors cancel in the corresponding product measure, while contractions carrying a longitudinal inverse metric acquire \(e^{-2\phi}\). A generic chosen completion may therefore contain

\[
\rho_{\rm comp}(\phi)
=e^{-2\phi}\!\left[w_{2\parallel}(\phi)X_{2\parallel}
+w_{4\parallel}(\phi)X_{4\parallel}\right]
+w_{2\perp}(\phi)X_{2\perp}
+w_{4\perp}(\phi)X_{4\perp}.
\]

Its \(\phi\) derivative contains the independently chosen \(w'_a\) and \(w_a\) coefficients. Constant weights are only one member and select the longitudinal channels under constrained reciprocal variation. Position-dependent weights or compensators change all source coefficients while preserving a declared covariance class. The finite chain rule is checked by `cas_stage2_carrier_weight_source.py`.

### Step 5 — metric variation and phi variation are different questions

1. If \(g_{\mu\nu}\) is varied independently, the carrier produces \(T_{\mu\nu}\). The EH lapse equation then uses a particular stress combination.
2. If \(\phi\) is independent and the matter action has no direct \(\phi\) dependence, varying \(\phi\) produces no carrier source.
3. If \(g=g(\phi)\) is imposed before variation, the chain rule produces \(T^{\mu\nu}\partial g_{\mu\nu}/\partial\phi\), a directional combination determined by the constrained tangent.
4. If direct weights \(w_a(\phi)\), a compensator, or a different representative are present, their derivatives add source terms.
5. Imposing reciprocity only after unrestricted metric variation retains normal metric equations that restrict-then-vary discards.

These operations are not interchangeable. In particular, the conditional unrestricted-metric combination \(\rho+S=2\rho_4\) is not generically the constrained reciprocal-\(\phi\) source, which can contain directional L2 and L4 contributions.

It does not force the carrier-to-dilation source weight because it does not choose the carrier ontology, weights, time sector, representative, independent fields, variation order, or geometric action.
