# Ponder gate — organize structural ensembles next

The registered eleven-amplitude family has now been sampled on axes, sign corners, 64 scattered
interior points, and sixteen five-radius rays. That preregistered tile is complete. This does not
prove that another same-chart amplitude design would find nothing new; it means no identified gap
in the present tile blocks the next descriptive organization step.

The next metric-led mapping tile should be a **structural ensemble atlas**, not a physics hunt. A
preregistration should define ensembles only from their positions and transformations in the full
coframe, then vary every nonempty ensemble combination without filtering results. The natural
first bookkeeping partition to audit is:

1. the reciprocal/base block;
2. the angular screen block;
3. the base-angular shift/connection block; and
4. the independent signed `phi` field.

This partition is a candidate coordinate description, not a claim that nature contains four
separate substances or mechanisms. Before computation, the audit must prove that each of the ten
metric amplitudes and `phi` appears exactly once, record all cross-ensemble combinations, and keep
the full metric intact during evaluation.

The atlas should ask only:

- what each ensemble activates alone;
- which pair, triple, and full combinations add genuinely new tensor directions or invariants;
- whether interactions are additive, redundant, or irreducibly cooperative in the sampled jets;
- whether conclusions survive coefficient banks, sample points, signs, and amplitudes; and
- where the candidate partition fails to be invariant or chart-independent.

It must not ask which combination resembles a particle, GR, cosmology, or a desired action. Other
function bases, charts, global completion, boundary structure, and dynamics stay open around this
bounded ensemble map.
