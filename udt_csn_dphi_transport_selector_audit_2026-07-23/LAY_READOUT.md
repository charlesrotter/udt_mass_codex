# Lay readout

We found an actual built-in piece of geometry.

Wherever `phi` is changing in a non-lightlike way, its gradient changes
size exactly opposite to the arbitrary common ruler/clock scaling. That
lets the metric cancel the arbitrary scale and construct a local,
scale-free “graph paper.” This graph paper has its own perfectly valid
connection—a rule for comparing directions from place to place.

That means UDT does **not** have to wait for a physical meter stick or a
bootstrap-selected metric merely to possess local pre-scale transport.

The catch is selection. Several different graph papers can be built from
the same metric and `phi` while respecting all current rules. If we also
demand that transport never mix the `phi` direction with the other three
directions, the clean torsion-free version works only when those other
directions swell or shrink together without shearing. The full UDT
metric has not yet been shown to force that.

We can always build a geometric connection that follows the split, but
it usually has torsion and retains internal rotation freedom. That makes
it a useful construction, not yet a law of nature.

Finally, the construction breaks when the `phi` gradient becomes
lightlike or vanishes. A whole universe or finite cell needs a rule for
those interfaces.

So this audit produced real forward movement:

```text
local scale-free transport exists;
the unique physical transport does not yet follow;
global continuation still needs a native selector/interface law.
```

The missing step is now smaller and cleaner than “find a connection.”
It is “derive why one of the available connections is physical and how
it closes across the whole cell.”
