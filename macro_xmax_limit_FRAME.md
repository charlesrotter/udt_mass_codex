## 2. What “only `c` and `G` as inputs” can honestly mean

### Dimensional fact (non-negotiable arithmetic)

- `c` alone: speed.  
- `G` alone: not a length.  
- **`c` and `G` together still do not make a unique length.**  
  The combination that *does* make a length is  
  **`G M / c²`**  
  — which needs a **mass** `M` (or equivalent integrated matter / charge of the solution).

So if “no other inputs” means *literally only the two constants `c` and `G`, and no mass/matter scale at all*, then **`x_max` cannot be a pure number times a Planck-free length built from `c,G` alone.** Something in the solution has to supply a mass scale.

### Reading that *does* work (recommended)

**Fundamental constants of the theory:** `c`, `G` (and later maybe ħ if quantum — not for this classical macro stage).  
**Not allowed as extra fundamental inputs:** H₀, 1101, Pantheon shape, hand-set cosmological constant, fit polynomial coefficients, imported “size of the universe.”

**Allowed as part of the solution (not extra constants of nature):**

- the matter content / mass of **this** whole (possibly **selected by closure** — E1),  
- then  
  **`x_max ∼ G M / c²`**  
  (or a pure number of order one times that — Misner–Sharp / marginal-edge style, already in the corpus as a relation to re-derive natively, not to use circularly as a depth fit).

That matches the project’s own earlier line: *a distance scale cannot come from `c,G` alone — you need a mass* — and the critical-universe idea: **matter amount is not an input constant; it is selected, then `x_max` follows from `c,G` and that amount.**

| 1101 / 7.004 as inputs to fix `x_max` | Data anchors, not constants of nature |
