# Lay Decision Tree — Projective Transport and Physical Section

Start with the conditional sphere of possible light directions at every spacetime point.

1. **Does the metric know how a chosen light direction travels?**
   - **Yes.** The unparametrized null path is preserved by Common-Scale changes.

2. **Does that travel rule choose which direction starts out physical?**
   - **No.** It carries every possible starting direction.

3. **Can `phi` choose?**
   - If `d phi` is null throughout a region, it gives one local geodesic light direction.
   - In the conditional ordinary static branch, spacelike `d phi` plus a time direction gives the
     inward/outward radial pair.
   - At zeros, timelike regions, or type changes, it does not give one universal null direction.

4. **Can the angular sector or curvature choose?**
   - A nondegenerate angular Hessian can give two local axes, but can vanish or depend on conformal
     representative.
   - Weyl curvature is stronger: Petrov II, III, and N have one locally distinguished repeated null
     direction. UDT has not yet shown that physical solutions must remain in those classes.

5. **Can the Hopf or tractor connection choose?**
   - **No.** They can transport representation data after a path/seed is supplied. They do not
     produce the spacetime section they would need to pull back.

6. **Can global holonomy choose?**
   - Not generally. It may preserve every direction, a pair, a special single ray, or none. It also
     still needs an initial seed.
