## Whole question

Does the registered UDT metric, including the interaction of `phi` with the angular sector, derive a
frame-independent physical section and transport law in the conditional projective null-direction
bundle without adding a carrier, observer, spin frame, conformal representative, topology, period,
or boundary action?

The audit must keep three logically distinct structures separate:

1. **ray propagation:** once a null ray is chosen, does the conformal metric carry it along an
   unparametrized null geodesic?
2. **neighboring-fiber comparison:** does the registered conformal geometry canonically compare
   arbitrary projective null directions over neighboring spacetime points?
3. **physical section selection:** does `phi` plus metric/angular data choose one global physical ray
   or carrier-like section rather than merely describe the bundle of possibilities?

A positive answer to the first does not certify the second or third. A connection that transports
already-chosen data does not itself choose that data. A section of the celestial `S2` bundle is not
automatically the existing fixed-round internal `S2` carrier.
