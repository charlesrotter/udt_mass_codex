topological, or other sectors while agreeing on a common static subtheory. A static observable could
then be unique on that equivalence class even though the full dynamics is not.

`E2+E4` restriction is insufficient: terms that vanish on static fields can still change lapse or
metric variation, constraints, canonical momenta, and boundary charges. The route needs all of the

4. the same differentiable boundary generator, topology, reference, orientation, and normalization;
5. compatible nontrivial finite-cell solutions and global `Xmax`/proper-density closure; and
6. a controlled weak/static limit with a uniform remainder.

If those conditions are proved, a scoped static mass result may move ahead of full-action
uniqueness. Until then, the route is a legitimate open fork, not a positive mass result and not a
no-go. `LAY_DECISION_TREE.md` gives the nontechnical gate sequence.
