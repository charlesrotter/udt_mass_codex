# K1 — Kaleidoscope on residual L (first tile)

**Binding:** physics does not change with distance; **observations** transform between frames.

## What we observe on L (\(A=1-r/X\))

1. **Shared law, every seat** — with \(\phi=0\) at the observer, the same formulas for \(z\), \(D_A=r\), \(d_L=(1+z)^2 r\) apply. Relational, no throne.

2. **Same shell, different seats → different \(z\)**  
   Source at \(r_s/X=0.8\): observer at center sees \(1+z\approx 2.24\); observer at \(0.6X\) sees \(1+z\approx 1.41\).  
   Shell unchanged; **residual between seat and shell** changed.

3. **Fixed stick → pure visual distortion**  
   Same proper transverse size: deeper placement → smaller angle (\(\propto 1/r\)), much fainter (flux \(\propto 1/d_L^2\) with \(d_L=(1+z)^2 r\)).  
   At \(r=X/2\): \(d_L = 2r\) already — twice flat-space luminosity distance; flux \(1/4\) of naive flat.

## One-line

**Kaleidoscope on L is residual maps between seats — redshift, size, brightness — not local physics changing with \(r\).**
