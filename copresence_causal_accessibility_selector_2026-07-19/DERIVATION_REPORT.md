# Co-Presence and Causal Accessibility Selector

## Result

`COPRESENCE_CAUSAL_PARTITION_COHERENT_CONFORMAL_LAYER_DERIVED_ACTION_BRIDGE_OPEN`

The new framing is coherent and genuinely clarifying, but co-presence is not a newly derived force,
speed, or uniquely UDT principle. Its clean mathematical role is whole-solution semantics already
available to ordinary block-universe descriptions. The distinctive conditional UDT content remains
reciprocal dilation; in the inherited static radial representative that metric structure supplies the
adapted optical warping.

The concise result is:

> One whole solution can have all events in its domain without making all events mutually accessible.
> Conditional reciprocal dilation shapes adapted radial causal accessibility; positive Common-Scale
> Neutrality preserves the causal cones while physical calibration remains open to matter rules.

This yields a natural separation between global closure, causal geometry, physical scale, and local
response. It does not choose the complete action or derive a unique two-stage bridge.

## 1. Co-presence is not zero-time separation

Correcting the committed preregistration's type-imprecise notation, write a whole candidate solution as

\[
S=(M_S,g,\Phi,\ldots).
\]

The working definition is

\[
p\;C_S\;q \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad p,q\in M_S=\operatorname{dom}(S).
\]

Inside one world this is deliberately broad. It states ordinary whole-solution event-domain
co-membership, not a UDT selector or causal order. It has no numeric duration, distance, propagation
speed, simultaneity convention, or intervention content.

An explicit optical-coordinate counterexample makes that unavoidable. Three event pairs belonging to
the same global solution can have intervals

\[
-3L^2<0,\qquad 0,\qquad +L^2>0.
\]

They are respectively timelike, null, and spacelike. Common membership therefore cannot imply mutual
accessibility or zero temporal separation.

“Atemporal global solution” should be read carefully. It means the complete solution is specified as
a whole rather than manufactured by a foundational clock. It does not erase the temporal relations
represented inside the solution, and it does not make every internal temporal separation zero.

## 2. Event-relative causal reachability is conformal metric content

For a time-oriented Lorentzian metric `g`, define geometric causal accessibility by

\[
q\in J_g^+(p)
\]

when a future-directed causal curve joins `p` to `q`. The phrase “causal partition” is retained only as
lay shorthand for this event-relative reachability structure. `J^+` is not an equivalence partition;
future and past sets can overlap in causally pathological spacetimes, and the relation is a causal
order only when additional global conditions exclude causal loops.

Under a positive common rescaling

\[
\widetilde g_{\mu\nu}=\Omega^2g_{\mu\nu},\qquad \Omega>0,
\]

every tangent vector satisfies

\[
\widetilde g(v,v)=\Omega^2g(v,v).
\]

Its timelike, null, or spacelike sign is unchanged. The inverse-metric characteristic polynomial
similarly scales by `Omega^-2`, so its zero set is unchanged. With topology and time orientation held
fixed,

\[
J_{\widetilde g}^{\pm}(p)=J_g^{\pm}(p).
\]

This is the exact place where Common-Scale Neutrality and causal accessibility fit together. A
positive common-scale change does not alter who can causally reach whom. Metric proper line elements
rescale pointwise:

\[
d\widetilde\tau=\Omega d\tau,
\qquad
d\widetilde L=\Omega dL.
\]

Thus the conformal class can carry event-relative reachability before a physical scale representative
has been selected. A representative is required to assign geometric proper intervals. Whether
physical rods, clocks, units, normalized energies, and mass readouts transform in that way additionally
requires material laws and coupling; no physical calibration theorem has been derived here.

This does not cover a vanishing or sign-changing scale, signature change, closed causal curves, or an
unselected time orientation. Those remain outside the bounded derivation.

## 3. Reciprocity supplies the optical warping

In the already conditional static radial representative,

\[
ds^2=-N^2c_0^2dt^2+N^{-2}dr^2,
\]

a radial metric-null curve obeys

\[
\left|\frac{dr}{dt}\right|=c_0N^2.
\]

Define optical position

\[
d\chi=\frac{dr}{N^2}.
\]

Then the radial metric becomes

\[
ds^2=N^2\left(-c_0^2dt^2+d\chi^2\right),
\]

and the null characteristics are simply

\[
\frac{d\chi}{dt}=\pm c_0.
\]

The coordinate warping is therefore a metric mapping between `r` and optical depth `chi`; it is not a
locally measured variable scalar speed. For a separated radial null curve, the static-chart coordinate
interval is

\[
\Delta t=\frac{1}{c_0}\int\frac{dr}{N^2}=\frac{\Delta\chi}{c_0},
\]

which is generally nonzero and can diverge at an optical horizon. This is coordinate time in the
registered static chart. The null curve's proper time is zero. An elapsed detector-clock reading
requires a specified detector worldline, lapse, and endpoint protocol.

Component slopes also depend on time-coordinate normalization. The executable convention is
`t_prime=t/lambda`, so `dr/dt_prime=lambda dr/dt`. This changes no geometric causal curve.

As a comparison readout only, the two-dimensional metric scalar d'Alembert principal equation is

\[
-\frac{\partial_t^2\psi}{N^2c_0^2}
+\partial_r\!\left(N^2\partial_r\psi\right)=0.
\]

In `chi` it becomes

\[
\frac{1}{N^2}
\left(-\frac{\partial_t^2\psi}{c_0^2}+\partial_\chi^2\psi\right)=0.
\]

Its characteristics reproduce the metric cone. This is not a derivation that native UDT matter or
photons obey that scalar equation.

## 4. What the distant star statement can and cannot mean

In a static optical chart, let the conditionally labeled detector event be

\[
O=(t_o,\chi_o)
\]

and let `Delta chi` be the optical separation from the star. **If** a physical signal follows this
metric-null characteristic, its associated emission event is

\[
E=\left(t_o-\frac{\Delta\chi}{c_0},\chi_e\right),
\]

which is null-related to `O`. The remote source event at the same chosen static-chart time,

\[
R_{\rm src}=(t_o,\chi_e),
\]

is spacelike from `O` when `Delta chi` is nonzero. All three events may be co-present in the complete
solution. The algebra identifies only the null-related candidate emission event, conditional on a
physical signal following that metric characteristic.

Any physical detector interaction would occur locally at the detector. This static radial comparison
does not prove that a UDT signal follows the characteristic, select a unique path, or derive a source,
detector, photon, or time-live cosmology. It is not correct to say that the detector receives the
star's state at the same chosen static-chart time. The null proper time is zero, but the static-chart
coordinate interval is not; a detector-clock interval needs its worldline and lapse.

Conditionally labeled source and detector events may be components of one whole solution. A physical
signal history between them requires the still-open matter equation, coupling, data domain, and
response prescription. Electron identity exchange and a photon-as-source–absorber ontology remain
unproved.

## 5. Global dependence is not operational signalling

An all-at-once boundary problem can have global sensitivity. The executable countermodel solves

\[
\begin{pmatrix}1&1\\1&-1\end{pmatrix}
\begin{pmatrix}x_1\\x_2\end{pmatrix}
=
\begin{pmatrix}b_1\\b_2\end{pmatrix},
\]

giving

\[
x_1=\frac{b_1+b_2}{2},\qquad x_2=\frac{b_1-b_2}{2}.
\]

Changing one boundary datum changes both components. Yet this model has no time variable, agent,
intervention, or signal. It maps one complete solution to another.

The distinction is essential, but both sides are counterfactual comparisons between solutions:

- **global retuning:** replace defining boundary or constraint data and compare the resulting global
  solutions;
- **localized intervention comparison:** apply a localized admissible perturbation while specifying
  which initial, boundary, and external data are held fixed, then use a well-posed retarded response
  map to compare solutions.

The metric supplies the candidate geometric boundary for the second comparison. It cannot alone prove
that every material equation has a hyperbolic principal symbol on that cone, that admissible data are
retarded, that every carrier couples universally, or that global causal pathologies are absent.

Accordingly,

> controllable influence is restricted to the metric causal future

is retained as a `POSIT/CONSISTENCY OBLIGATION`. It becomes a UDT theorem only after the complete
action, material source/coupling, variation/data domain, and well-posed response have been derived.

## 6. Why this does not select C-squared, EH, or the bridge

The strongest unique-action counter-derivation fails exactly. Let

\[
Q(k)=g^{\mu\nu}k_\mu k_\nu.
\]

A second-order principal polynomial `Q` and a fourth-order polynomial `Q^2` have the identical real
characteristic set

\[
Q(k)=0.
\]

They have different derivative order. Likewise `Q` and `Q+m^2` have the same principal cone but
different lower-order dynamics. Hence causal accessibility cannot by itself select derivative order,
lower-order terms, an action, a source, or boundary completion.

This countermodel directly blocks all three promotions:

- causal accessibility does not uniquely select conditional pre-scale `C^2`/Bach;
- it does not uniquely select conditional post-scale EH;
- it does not derive a unique action connecting them.

Nor does the all-at-once wording select the variation domain. The same hyperbolic Euler–Lagrange
equation can be represented as a stationary global action problem or as a Cauchy evolution. Causal
response depends on the operator, coupling, and admissible solution/data prescription—not on a story
that the variational calculation itself happens sequentially.

## 7. A useful compatible two-stage architecture

Although it is not uniquely derived, the audit exposes a clean compatible hierarchy:

1. **Global admissibility:** one complete solution supplies the referent for co-presence.
2. **Reciprocal conformal geometry:** the time-oriented conformal class supplies causal accessibility.
3. **Physical scale selection:** a bootstrap map could select a positive representative for geometric
   proper intervals without changing the cone; physical rods and clocks would still require material
   transformation and coupling rules.
4. **Response dynamics:** a complete action, source, boundary prescription, and material coupling
   would determine interventions, observables, and mass.

The smallest genuinely missing bridge remains an actual bootstrap rule mapping the pre-scale class to
a physical representative together with a compatible response/coupling prescription. Co-presence
does not invent that rule. It tells us how not to misinterpret it: a global bootstrap selection is not
automatically an instantaneous physical transmission.

There is an important nuance. Reciprocal dilation changes the causal geometry through the clock/ruler
ratio; a subsequent positive common-scale representative change does not. Therefore the UDT-specific
candidate is not co-presence alone. It is the combination:

> in the inherited conditional static radial representative, reciprocal positional dilation determines
> adapted conformal causal accessibility; Common-Scale Neutrality leaves that accessibility invariant,
> while bootstrap selection and material rules would have to supply physical calibration.

That is a sharper organization of existing principles, not a new forced action principle.

## 8. Final status

Derived in the registered class:

- positive common scaling preserves event-relative causal reachability;
- geometric proper intervals require a representative, while physical calibration additionally
  requires material transformation and coupling rules;
- reciprocal static dilation gives the optical-depth warping and nonzero static-chart coordinate
  interval for a separated null curve;
- common global membership does not imply causal accessibility;
- a causal cone cannot select action order or lower-order dynamics.

Working or conditional:

- co-presence as global-solution membership;
- the conditional source/detector endpoint interpretation;
- the four-layer pre-scale/post-scale architecture;
- the static star illustration and reciprocal optical representative.

Still open:

- the bootstrap representative-selection map;
- the pre-scale/post-scale variation-domain bridge;
- a complete native action, source, boundary charge, and matter coupling;
- operational no-signalling as a theorem;
- photon ontology, native mass, CMB/time-live realization, substrate, and recycling.

So the framing is promising because it simplifies the conceptual architecture without pretending to
solve the missing dynamics. The global unity is a working ontology; the local restraint is metric
geometry; the physical influence law remains a derivation obligation.
