# Co-Presence — Lay Decision Tree

## What “one with everything” can mean here

Are two events in the event-domain of the same whole candidate universe?

- **No:** this audit says nothing about their relation.
- **Yes:** they are co-present in the working sense. This is ordinary whole-solution co-membership,
  not yet a UDT selector. Continue.

Does co-presence make them simultaneous, identical, or mutually visible?

- **No.** Co-presence contains no clock reading, distance, or transmission rule.

Does the metric place one event inside the other's light cone?

- **No:** they may still belong to the same universe, but there is no registered controllable
  influence between them in that direction.
- **Yes:** the later event is geometrically accessible. Continue.

Do the actual matter and signal equations follow that metric cone with a well-posed retarded response?

- **Not yet derived:** geometric accessibility is known, operational influence remains conditional.
- **If later derived:** a source can affect a detector through the causal history joining them.

Does this select the complete action?

- **No.** Different equations and derivative orders can have the same light cone.

## The star example

When you look at a distant star, any detector interaction occurs locally at the detector. In the
conditional static radial comparison, **if** the physical signal follows the metric-null
characteristic, the associated emission event lies earlier by the optical static-chart coordinate
interval. The star's state at the same chosen static-chart time is also part of the whole solution,
but this calculation does not make it observable at the detector.

That is the clean meaning of the new framing:

> The whole universe is co-present as one solution; observations expose only its causally accessible
> relations.

The apparent lookback is not a fake delay. In the registered comparison it is optical coordinate
depth. The null curve has zero proper time, while elapsed detector-clock time requires the detector
worldline and lapse. No foundational clock must tick while the complete solution “comes into being,”
but events inside that solution retain metric relations and event-relative reachability.

## The old electron-swap picture

The safe surviving intuition is that conditionally labeled source and detector events may be described
inside one globally consistent solution. A physical signal, its path, or any propagation history
requires the still-missing matter equation and coupling. A literal instantaneous exchange of electron
identity or state is unsupported.

## The concise formulation

**Globally one, causally partitioned.**

Here “partitioned” is lay shorthand for event-relative causal reachability. The relation is not an
equivalence-class partition and is not necessarily a global partial order unless causal loops are
excluded.

- “Globally one” is presently a working way to read a complete solution.
- “Causally partitioned” is metric content in the registered conditional class: positive common
  scaling preserves the light cones, while the inherited static reciprocal representative determines
  their adapted radial optical warping.
- “No signalling outside the cone” is a required dynamical consistency condition, not yet a theorem
  from the incomplete action/source sector.
