## Scope and authority

This isolated, CPU-only derivation formalizes the owner's long-standing picture of a globally
co-present universe whose observations and controllable influences remain causally partitioned by
dilation geometry. It asks whether that picture supplies a genuinely new UDT selector, clarifies the
pre-scale/post-scale bridge, or merely restates structure shared by ordinary Lorentzian theories.

This is a principle and kinematics audit. It does not authorize a photon ontology, instantaneous
electron exchange, a preferred simultaneity, a new action, a native source or carrier, an imported GR
field equation, data fitting, GPU work, canonization, repository reorganization, or `grok` integration.
The July-1 provenance firewall remains binding. The original dirty checkout is metadata-only and its
dirty file contents must not be read.

## Whole question

Can the phrase

> globally one, causally partitioned

be made mathematically precise so that:

1. co-presence does not mean zero-time separation, instantaneous transmission, mutual observability,
   or local identity;
2. causal accessibility is supplied by the UDT metric rather than an additional signal substrate;
3. positive Common-Scale Neutrality rescalings preserve the causal partition;
4. reciprocal dilation supplies the previously derived static optical warping;
5. a pre-scale global bootstrap can coexist with post-scale local causal propagation without being
   misreported as superluminal dynamics; and
6. any actual action-selection or two-stage-bridge claim is separated from what the metric alone
   forces?

The maximum allowed conclusion is a scoped selector/architecture ruling. A complete action, material
coupling, source–absorber ontology, or intervention theorem cannot be derived in this slice.

## Registered definitions to test

### K0 — global solution membership

Let a candidate world be one complete admissible field configuration or solution `S` on an event set
`M`. Register the working definition:

`p` and `q` are **co-present in S** iff both are events/states represented in that same complete
solution.

This is a proposed semantic definition (`CHOSE/WORKING`), not yet affirmative UDT physics. It has no
duration, order, propagation speed, or intervention content by itself.

### K1 — metric causal accessibility

For a time-oriented Lorentzian representative `g`, register:

`q` is causally accessible from `p` iff a future-directed `g`-causal curve joins `p` to `q`.

The null boundary is the light cone. Test which parts depend only on the conformal class `[g]` and
which require a selected metric representative.

### K2 — operational influence

Register as a consistency obligation, not a derived theorem:

A controllable local intervention may influence only the metric causal future of its support.

The audit must distinguish a change of the complete global boundary-value solution from a physical
intervention performed inside one solution. It must not use global consistency as a disguised
instantaneous signalling channel.

### K3 — source, path, and detector

Test the limited statement that emission event, causal field history, and detector event can be parts
of one global solution while remaining distinct local events. Do not identify electrons, assert a
literal state swap, or derive a photon ontology.

### K4 — pre-scale/post-scale layering

Test a possible hierarchy:

1. global admissibility/co-presence;
2. conformal causal structure and reciprocal cone geometry;
3. bootstrap selection of a positive physical scale representative;
4. proper clocks/rulers and any post-scale action/matter readout.

Determine whether this hierarchy is forced, merely compatible, or contradicted. In particular, test
whether causal accessibility alone selects pre-scale `C^2`, post-scale EH, or a unique bridge.

## Registered model and countermodel classes

1. **Lorentzian global-solution class:** one globally specified solution with a metric causal order.
2. **Positive conformal class:** `g_tilde = Omega^2 g`, `Omega>0`, testing invariance of timelike,
   null, and spacelike signs and therefore of the causal partition.
3. **Static reciprocal radial class:** `ds^2=-N^2 c0^2 dt^2+N^-2 dr^2`, testing optical coordinate
   `chi'=N^-2`, characteristics, and nonzero metric travel time.
4. **Comparison hyperbolic scalar principal part:** the metric d'Alembert principal operator is used
   only as a comparison/readout of the cone, not as a native UDT matter equation.
5. **All-at-once boundary countermodel:** a global constraint or boundary-value solution can change
   everywhere when its boundary data are changed; this does not by itself define an in-world
   controllable signal.
6. **Ordinary block-universe countermodel:** an otherwise non-UDT Lorentzian theory can satisfy global
   co-presence plus local causal order. This tests uniqueness.
7. **Action countermodels:** conformal/pre-scale and representative/post-scale actions may share the
   same causal cone. This tests whether the proposed principle selects a variation domain or action.
