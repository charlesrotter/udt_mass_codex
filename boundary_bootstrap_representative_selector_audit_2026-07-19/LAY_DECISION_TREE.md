# Lay Decision Tree

## What did we learn?

UDT already has a rule that selects the WR–L **shape** of the reciprocal clock/ruler relationship near and, under the adopted global re-centering ruling, across the simple metric. What it does not have is a rule that turns that scale-free shape into one fully calibrated physical universe.

Think of it as having a perfectly specified map printed without a scale. The roads and boundary are fixed relative to one another, but nothing on the map says how many physical miles one printed inch represents.

## Didn't the wall fix the scale?

No. We explicitly changed the interior common scale while leaving the center, outer wall, wall area, wall strength, causal cones, and optical travel geometry unchanged. The interior proper lengths, angular areas, volume, curvature, and clock–curvature relationship changed.

So the wall is real and restrictive, but the present wall information does not calibrate the whole interior metric.

## What does Xmax reciprocity contribute?

It may provide a very natural rule for **where a position lies as a fraction of the maximum reach** and how different positional comparisons combine.

But `x/Xmax` is unchanged if both `x` and `Xmax` are enlarged by the same factor. Therefore the reciprocity can organize the map without telling us the map's physical scale.

Even if a future global calculation determines the physical value of `Xmax`, that fixes only one global ruler unless it also tells every interior clock, radial ruler, and angular ruler how to scale.

## What about bootstrap?

Current bootstrap says that only globally self-consistent universes with matter in a narrow density window are physically realized. That is a powerful acceptance test, but the theory still lacks the action, total mass, complete volume, and boundary charge needed to calculate the candidates being tested.

## What is actually missing?

The missing piece is a simultaneous whole-universe closure that produces:

- the native total mass/charge;
- the physical `Xmax`;
- the complete interior metric calibration; and
- stable matter, if matter is what makes the scale physical.

This is why recombining more scale-free geometric identities cannot finish the job. The next successful step must introduce no invented mechanism, but it must derive the scale-bearing boundary/matter object that the present theory openly lacks.
