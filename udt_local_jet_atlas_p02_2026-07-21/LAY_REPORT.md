# P02 in plain language

We used the P01 geometric ruler to map everything the metric can do at one location before imposing
laws of motion.

The clearest result is that local curvature has twenty independent pieces. Ten are the shape-distorting
or tidal part called Weyl curvature. The other ten are the Ricci/Schouten part. UDT's Common-Scale
freedom can change the entire Ricci/Schouten part locally by changing the scale calibration, but it
does not erase the Weyl part.

That makes Weyl geometry the surviving local pre-scale information. It helps explain why
four-derivative conformal mathematics keeps appearing, but it does **not** prove that Weyl-squared is
the UDT action. Geometry tells us what survives the scale freedom; it does not tell us what equation
that geometry must obey.

We also kept all the ways `phi` can point relative to the angular sector. Nothing in unrestricted
local geometry forces it to be lightlike or aligned with one direction. Angular expansion can be
changed by the common-scale choice, while angular shear and twist cannot. Twist can genuinely make
the supplied two-sector split nonintegrable.

So P02 did not find “the favored solution.” It established the complete local stage on which a real
UDT constraint or dynamics law would have to act, without hiding angular mixing, twist, degenerate
limits, or continuous alternatives.
