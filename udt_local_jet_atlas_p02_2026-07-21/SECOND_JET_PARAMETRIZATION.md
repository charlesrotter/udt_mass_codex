# Complete local second-jet parametrization

The second-jet atlas is continuous, not a catalogue of 18 special tensors.

At a regular Lorentzian point, arbitrary metric second jets modulo the normal-coordinate redundancy
are exactly arbitrary algebraic Riemann tensors. The package represents every such tensor uniquely
as:

`arbitrary Weyl tensor + metric wedge arbitrary symmetric Schouten tensor`.

Both summands have ten real components. The machine-readable direct-sum basis has exact rank 20,
so no curvature component, mixed angular component, Ricci component, or relative alignment is
missing. Arbitrary coefficients and relative orientations remain live.

The separate curvature-rank, Ricci-rank, and Petrov tables describe exact discriminant surfaces in
that continuous space. They are not asserted to form a 210-cell Cartesian product. Any proposed
joint label must be evaluated on the full `C + g wedge P` tensor; P02 neither declares an
intersection empty from failed sampling nor demands that every marginal combination exist.

This distinction prevents two opposite errors:

- reducing the atlas to a handful of attractive exact witnesses; and
- claiming a combinatorial branch count that ignores algebraic compatibility and continuous moduli.

Common-Scale Neutrality acts transitively on the ten local Schouten components through the scale
Hessian at a point. The pre-scale local second-jet content is therefore the arbitrary ten-component
Weyl tensor. The representative-specific Schouten tensor is preserved in the atlas because a future
physical representative, boundary, or matter law may make it meaningful.

Nothing here chooses a curvature action or field equation.
