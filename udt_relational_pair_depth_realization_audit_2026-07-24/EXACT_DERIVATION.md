# Exact relational observer-pair depth derivation

## 0. Owner frame meaning

Dilation is relational observer-frame data. For every observer `p`,

```text
rho_p(p)=0,
S_p(p)=I.
```

The observer's own local frame is neutral and local physics is unchanged.
Nontrivial dilation belongs only to comparing distinct frames.

The three-observer controls below test how pair transformations can be
represented and composed. They do not test whether dilation exists and do
not turn it into a local physical effect.

## 1. Starting point

The parent audit established the ordered reciprocal operator

```text
S(delta)=diag(exp(-delta),exp(delta)).
```

The operator is not missing. This audit asks what complete geometric object
provides `delta`, or its nonnegative physical magnitude `rho`, for actual
observer pairs.

The exact metric supplies a bilocal distance

```text
d_h(p,q)=infimum of metric lengths from p to q.
```

This is symmetric and nonnegative. The ordered reciprocal parameter is
signed and reverses under comparison-order reversal. These are distinct
types. A sign/orientation lift is required to feed a symmetric distance
magnitude into an ordered inverse operator.

## 2. No one absolute global scalar difference can encode every isotropic pair depth

Suppose one global scalar `phi` and one strictly positive distance response
`F` represented every pair on round `S3_b`:

```text
abs(phi(q)-phi(p))=F(d_h(p,q)),
F(d)>0 for d>0.
```

Fix

```text
p=b(1,0,0,0)
```

and choose three observers in orthogonal tangent directions:

```text
q1=b(1/2,sqrt(3)/2,0,0),
q2=b(1/2,0,sqrt(3)/2,0),
q3=b(1/2,0,0,sqrt(3)/2).
```

Each obeys

```text
p dot qi/b^2=1/2,
d(p,qi)=pi b/3.
```

Every pair obeys

```text
qi dot qj/b^2=1/4<1,
```

so the three `qi` are mutually separated.

But every scalar value `phi(qi)` must be either

```text
phi(p)+F(pi b/3)
```

or

```text
phi(p)-F(pi b/3).
```

Three observers and only two possible values force two observers to have
the same scalar value. Their scalar difference is zero even though their
metric separation is nonzero, contradicting the assumed relation.

Therefore:

```text
ONE GLOBAL LOCAL-SCALAR DIFFERENCE IS REFUTED AS A UNIVERSAL
CENTERLESS ISOTROPIC PAIR-DEPTH REPRESENTATION.
```

This does not refute a signed local `phi` solution field, a local ordered
chain, an anisotropic profile, or the exact identity
`S(phi(p))^-1 S(phi(q))`. It refutes assigning that identity the job of
representing every nonnegative pair separation on the complete round branch.
It also does not refute the three observers' pairwise dilations. Those are
separate relational comparisons rather than differences of one absolute
location label.

## 3. Observer-indexed bilocal depth is naturally centerless

For any supplied complete metric and scalar response `F`, define

```text
rho_p(q)=F(d_h(p,q)).
```

Every observer has such a map. On a homogeneous branch, isometries carry

```text
rho_p(q) -> rho_{G p}(G q)
```

without changing its value. Each observer is the origin of their own
comparison chart, but none is a globally preferred center.

This is the smallest surviving metric-native type in the bounded census:

```text
OBSERVER_INDEXED_BILOCAL_METRIC_FAMILY GIVEN F.
```

It does not yet select `F`, the signed ordered lift, or transition laws
between different observer charts.

Every observer retains `rho_p(p)=0`. The value `rho_p(q)` is not a physical
field imposed locally at `q`; it is comparison data between the `p` and `q`
frames.

The same type remains available in the homogeneous squashed control.
Transitivity retains point equivalence, while its angular metric can make
the cut depth depend on direction. The full squashed cut locus remains open,
so no band is calculated here.

## 4. Exact composition domain

Metric distance is not additive for arbitrary triples. It obeys the triangle
inequality. Equality holds when the intermediate observer lies between the
other two on the same minimizing geodesic before its cut point:

```text
d(p,r)=d(p,q)+d(q,r).
```

Suppose a universal continuous response `F` is required to turn those
ordinary proper subsegment lengths into additive depth:

```text
F(x+y)=F(x)+F(y).
```

The continuous Cauchy theorem on the nonnegative interval gives

```text
F(d)=kappa d.
```

One direct proof first obtains `F(n x)=nF(x)` and
`F((m/n)x)=(m/n)F(x)` for nonnegative rationals, then extends to real
arguments by continuity. The production algebra independently confirms
that every polynomial coefficient except the linear one is eliminated.

Consequently, if proper diameter `D` is finite and `kappa` is finite,

```text
lim_(d->D-) F(d)=kappa D
```

is finite. Infinite additive depth at a finite proper diameter is
incompatible with identifying all three objects:

1. universal `F(d)`;
2. ordinary proper-length subsegment additivity; and
3. infinite depth at finite `D`.

At least one identification must change.

## 5. What a nonlinear asymptotic display can do

The exact control

```text
rho=atanh(d/D),
d=D tanh(rho)
```

has

```text
rho -> infinity as d -> D-.
```

But it does not obey ordinary length additivity. Two display values
`d/D=1/2` combine under additive `rho` to

```text
(1/2+1/2)/(1+1/4)=4/5,
```

not to the ordinary length sum `1`.

Thus a projective law can reconcile finite display and infinite additive
coordinate, but only by making the bounded quantity a nonlinear display
rather than ordinary additive proper length. That is exactly the type
distinction retained by the earlier full-frame audit.

Another possibility is a location-dependent accumulation rate in one
observer chart:

```text
rho_p(r)=rho_p(q)+[rho_p(r)-rho_p(q)].
```

This always telescopes, but for nonlinear `F` the bracket is generally not
the rebased pair value `rho_q(r)`. A transition law between observer charts
is then necessary. Current UDT does not yet supply it globally.

## 6. Angular data are required for composition

On round `S3_b`, write two observers relative to `p` as

```text
q/b=(cos alpha, sin alpha n),
r/b=(cos beta,  sin beta  m),
```

where `n` and `m` are unit directions in the three-dimensional tangent
space at `p`. Then exactly

```text
cos[d(q,r)/b]
 =cos(alpha)cos(beta)+sin(alpha)sin(beta)(n dot m).
```

The two radial depths `alpha` and `beta` do not determine the third pair
distance. At `alpha=beta=pi/3`, direction dots `+1,0,-1` give pair dots

```text
1, 1/4, -1/2
```

and therefore three different separations.

This is the exact role of the angular “orchestra”: even on the simplest
complete branch, non-collinear observer composition is not a scalar-depth
problem. The angular relation participates necessarily.

The founding additive law remains exact on its legitimate one-dimensional
ordered-depth chain. It must not be generalized to arbitrary observer
triangles without the angular sector.

## 7. Scalar distance and full transport separate at the cut locus

For every observer `p` on round `S3_b`, the antipode `-p` has the unique
scalar distance

```text
d(p,-p)=pi b.
```

Infinitely many minimizing great semicircles realize that same distance.
Their full tangent transports differ.

In a common tangent basis, the semicircle initially directed along `e1`
acts as

```text
diag(-1,1,1),
```

while the semicircle along `e2` acts as

```text
diag(1,-1,1).
```

Both are exact isometries and they are unequal.

Therefore:

```text
scalar metric distance at the cut locus = single valued;
complete frame transport at the cut locus = path family.
```

A scalar pair depth `F(d)` need not choose a path there. A complete angular
observer-frame comparison does.

## 8. Reciprocal-current integral

For a supplied global reciprocal section,

```text
S(phi)=diag(exp(-phi),exp(phi)),
J=S^-1 dS=diag(-dphi,dphi).
```

Along any path inside a global exact chart,

```text
integral J
 =diag(-(phi(q)-phi(p)),phi(q)-phi(p)).
```

Exponentiation recovers the endpoint operator. This is exact and
path-independent because the current is an exact Abelian differential.

It inherits two gates:

1. the current requires the open identification of a complete physical
   reciprocal section; and
2. a global scalar difference cannot serve as every isotropic pair depth on
   the complete round branch.

If only a closed local form exists on a nontrivial global completion, its
period may be nonzero and path/holonomy data return. The current identity
therefore does not by itself close the global observer-pair map.

## 9. Scale gate

`d_h` has dimensions of length. Reciprocal depth is dimensionless. A linear
relation

```text
rho=kappa d_h
```

requires `kappa` with inverse-length dimensions.

The founding `c` and `1/c` relation exactly converts clock and length units.
But `d/c` is a time, not a dimensionless exponential depth. A clock duration
or length normalization is still required to produce `kappa`.

This does not demote `c`: it remains the clock-length scale anchor. It says
only that `c` alone does not numerically select the dimensionless
depth-per-length profile. A future mass/bootstrap structure may supply a
length such as the global diameter, but none is inserted here.

## 10. Ruling

Derived in the bounded complete controls:

- observer-frame dilation leaves every local self-frame neutral;
- one absolute global scalar difference cannot universally represent
  centerless isotropic pair depth, without invalidating pairwise dilation;
- observer-indexed bilocal metric depth is a valid centerless type given
  `F`;
- ordinary geodesic additivity forces a universal continuous `F` to be
  linear;
- angular data are required for non-collinear composition;
- scalar distance remains unique at a cut locus while full transport is a
  path family;
- the exact reciprocal-current integral realizes endpoint differences
  conditional on a supplied global section.

Still open:

- `F`;
- signed orientation/lift into the ordered reciprocal operator;
- observer-chart transition law;
- complete clock/angular solder and event pairing;
- whether the asymptote belongs to proper distance, a projective display,
  or a location-dependent accumulated depth;
- physical `X_max`, mass response, density, and CMB interpretation.
