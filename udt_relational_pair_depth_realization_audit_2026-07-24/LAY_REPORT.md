# Lay report

Your correction is now explicit: dilation exists only in the comparison
between observer frames. Each observer's own local frame reads itself as
normal, and its local physics is unchanged.

We found why that relational observer-pair effect kept resisting a single
absolute global `phi` field.

Imagine painting one number on every point of a round universe and trying to
use the difference between two painted numbers as their distance. Pick three
places equally far from you in three different directions. Each painted
number could only be your number plus the common distance-value or minus it.
With three places and only two choices, two places must receive the same
number—even though they are separated from each other.

So one absolute number attached to each location cannot encode every
pairwise distance in a centerless three-dimensional universe. That does not
invalidate any of the pairwise dilations—just as adding a third observer
does not invalidate relative time dilation in SR.

The natural alternative is that every observer has their own equivalent
depth map:

```text
depth seen from observer p to observer q
    = some function of the complete metric distance between p and q.
```

That does not make each observer a privileged center. It means every
observer can place themselves at the origin of their own comparison chart,
just as every person on Earth can measure distances outward from where they
stand. The depth to themselves is always zero.

The angular sector then becomes essential. Knowing that two people are each
1,000 miles from you does not tell you how far apart they are. You also need
the angle between their directions. The complete metric behaves the same
way: general observer comparisons are a depth-plus-angle problem, not a
single-number problem.

Those angular relations govern how different observer-frame comparisons fit
together. They do not change any observer's local physics.

We also clarified the asymptote. If clock depth is simply proportional to
ordinary proper distance, a finite maximum distance produces only finite
depth. Infinite clock dilation at a finite limit therefore requires
something different:

- the bounded distance may be a nonlinear display of unbounded depth;
- depth may accumulate at a varying rate and diverge near the limit;
- or another metric-derived relation may connect them.

The metric still has to choose. We have not inserted `tanh`, mass, or the
CMB as the answer.

At the most distant point in the round control, the distance itself remains
unique even though many equally short routes reach it. What becomes
route-dependent is the complete orientation of a transported frame. That is
where angular modulation and intermediate structure can eventually enter.
