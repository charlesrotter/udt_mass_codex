# Preregistration: angular bulk Jacobi/selector audit

Date: 2026-07-23

## Whole question

Does the complete non-block UDT metric, together with the exact registered
Reciprocity, Common-Scale Neutrality (CSN), finite-cell, seal, topology,
Cartan/Bianchi, bootstrap, and observed-`c` structure, derive a native bulk
law that determines the two invariant angular diagnostics

```text
A_rel   = relative angular-area rate
S_shape = angular shape-speed squared
```

and forces

```text
A_rel=0, S_shape=1
```

throughout a complete regular finite-cell branch?

The audit is metric-led. It is not a search for an action, particle,
carrier, source, familiar field equation, or preferred desired branch.

## Candidate missing object

The neutral candidate is the exact covariant transport object for the
angular deformation endomorphism along a supplied `phi`-normalized depth
flow. The audit will derive:

1. the full screen deformation, including trace, trace-free symmetric, and
   skew/transport parts;
2. its curvature/Jacobi/Riccati identity with acceleration, non-affinity,
   normal-bundle leakage, and non-block effects retained or separately
   exposed;
3. the trace and trace-free scalar equations controlling `A_rel` and
   `S_shape`;
4. the transformation under full-metric common rescaling and depth
   reparameterization;
5. the exact curvature data equivalent to the target pair in every
   declared subcase; and
6. whether any registered UDT premise supplies that curvature data as an
   independent equation rather than a definition or identity.

Calling this object a "law" is forbidden until item 6 passes.

## Observing, not targeting

The sampled object is the complete family of metric-derived deformation and
curvature relations on a supplied regular reciprocal/angular split.
Matched, unmatched, twisting, accelerated, non-affine, and endpoint-flat
profiles are retained and classified. No solution is discarded for failing
the reciprocal target.

  metric;

  response;

- GPU numerics or solution fitting; or
- canonization or navigation/reorganization changes.

## Success contract

`DERIVES_NATIVE_BULK_LAW` is allowed only if:

1. the equation is independent of the definitions of connection and
   curvature;
2. it follows from exact registered UDT premises without a chosen action,
   representative, connection class, curvature ansatz, boundary amplitude,
   carrier, or source;
3. every metric degree of freedom appearing in the equation is determined
   or is shown to cancel;
4. it is valid for the complete non-block metric in the declared regular
   scope;
5. it forces both target diagnostics throughout every admitted complete
   regular branch, not only at a seal or on a selected profile; and
6. no smooth source-matched countermetric survives.

A conditional theorem must name every added premise and may not be promoted
to native closure.

## Falsification contract

The native derivation fails if any one of the following holds:

- the candidate equation is a Cartan, Jacobi, Riccati, Gauss, Codazzi, or
  Bianchi identity whose curvature/source side is determined by the same
  freely chosen metric jets;
- an arbitrary curvature, acceleration, twist, leakage, lapse, shift, or
  connection component remains;
- endpoint-flat non-block counterfamilies satisfy the registered premises
  while changing the bulk diagnostics;
- the target follows only after setting a curvature eigenvalue, repeated
  direction, affine parameter, unit amplitude, area ratio, action, or
  representative by hand;
- seal data determine only a local jet and no uniqueness/propagation
  theorem is derived;
- the construction fails full CSN covariance or erases explicit `c`; or
- a pointwise result is promoted to global transport.
