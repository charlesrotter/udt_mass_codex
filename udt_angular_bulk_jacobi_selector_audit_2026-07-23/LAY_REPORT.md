# Lay report

We found the mathematical object that tells the angular part of the metric
how to change as we move through the finite cell.

It works like a steering equation:

```text
change in angular stretching
+ the stretching already present
+ the geometric push from the rest of the metric
= zero.
```

That “geometric push” includes curvature, acceleration, twist, movement of
the angular/reciprocal split, and leakage between the sectors. All of it
comes from the complete metric.

There is an important catch. The steering equation is an identity—rather
like saying that a road bends according to its curvature. If we freely draw
a different road, its curvature changes to match. The identity does not
choose which road the universe uses.

But testing it exposed a much sharper possibility.

The established reciprocal clock/ruler pair has a definite geometric push.
We can compare the angular push with it. Call their difference
`Delta_K`.

If a native UDT rule eventually shows

```text
Delta_K=0,
```

then a finite cell with two regular mirror boundaries becomes remarkably
rigid. At the first mirror the angular area must stop changing, but the
stretching strength is initially free. Requiring the area also to stop at
the second mirror leaves only strength one. The angular sector then keeps
the exact equal-and-opposite reciprocal stretch throughout the cell.

In ordinary language:

> If the reciprocal and angular sectors feel the same geometric push, two
> mirrors tune the angular stretching to the reciprocal unit automatically.

That is the most elegant closure found so far. It is not yet UDT physics,
because two things remain unproved:

1. UDT has not derived that the two sectors feel the same geometric push.
2. UDT has not selected a two-mirror universe rather than the other
   registered finite-cell completions.

So we have not completed the theory, but we have replaced a vague missing
law with one exact tensor equality and one exact conditional theorem. The
next question is no longer “what equation might help?” It is whether
`Delta_K=0` is already hidden in the founding metric principles.
