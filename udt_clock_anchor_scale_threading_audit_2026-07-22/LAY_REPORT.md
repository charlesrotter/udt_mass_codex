# Lay report — putting `c` back where it belongs

The audit confirms the owner's correction: Einsteinian `c` is not something added after the UDT
metric is built. It is already the metric's dimensional clock–distance anchor.

At an ordinary local metric-null relation, the stationary frame's clock component and ruler
component cancel their dilation factors exactly. What remains is

```text
local ruler component = c_E × stationary-frame clock component.
```

Equivalently, one unit of local ruler interval corresponds to `1/c_E` units of the reference
clock's interval. This is true for arbitrary positive reciprocal modulation `X` and arbitrary
positive threading factor `F` in the tested spherical time-live chart. The clock component here is
that of the stationary local reference frame; it is not the proper time of a null curve, which is
zero.

Under the co-present interpretation, this equation need not describe a little object fundamentally
travelling through an external time at `c_E`. It can be read as the metric's local conversion
between its clock and ruler directions. The audit does not derive a material signal law, and it does
not make distant information instantly available. Those are separate source/response questions.

## What actually dilates

Three quantities had been getting conflated:

```text
c_E                         local dimensional clock–distance anchor
F sqrt(X)                   clock rate relative to the selected time coordinate
c_E F X                     radial metric-null slope in the selected coordinates
```

The last two may change from place to place. The first does not. Calling either of the last two
“effective c” is safe only if the coordinate or comparison protocol is named explicitly.

This explains why scaling `T=c_E t` was mathematically harmless but conceptually costly: it hid the
physical anchor and made the dimensionless dilation factors look as though they created `c`.

## Did existing UDT principles fix the missing threading?

No current registered premise fixes the radial content of `F`.

- Pair Reciprocity fixes the inverse `sqrt(X)` clock/ruler modulation. It leaves `F` outside that
  reciprocal pair.
- Setting the time–radial coordinate determinant to a fixed value would force `F=1`, but a passive
  relabeling of time changes that determinant. It would be a synchronization choice, not an
  observer-covariant derivation.
- A time-only multiplier in `F` can be removed locally by resetting the clock coordinate. A radial
  change cannot: its radial derivative changes the stationary clock congruence and curvature.
- CSN does not choose a physical representative.
- Finite-cell endpoint data do not choose the interior. An exact positive family preserves `F` and
  its first two derivatives at both ends while changing interior curvature.
- Registered bootstrap says which completed universes may be admissible, but supplies no operation
  or equation that calculates `F`.

The static WR-L metric has `F=1`, but that is a scoped static realization rather than a general
time-live theorem.

## The proposed reflective extremes

The outer picture is coherent but conditional. If `X` tends to zero and `F` does not diverge too
quickly, both the remote clock factor and radial coordinate slope tend to zero. The recorded WR-L
wall is an exact example. Other behaviors of `F` can change the result, so `X→0` by itself is not
the complete statement.

At an ordinary smooth spherical center, full curvature requires `X→1` and a finite nonzero lapse
whose first radial derivative vanishes. Making either `X` or the radial part of `F` diverge there
makes an exact positive curvature invariant diverge. Thus an infinite inner clock rate is not a
regular spherical-center branch.

That does **not** rule out the owner's micro reflection generally. The repository has no derivation
identifying “subnuclear separation approaching zero” with a spherical areal radius shrinking to a
center. A toric, Hopf-like, throat, quotient, or other non-central geometry can have small intrinsic
lengths without possessing that center. Those possibilities remain open.

## Why scale keeps disappearing

Keeping `c_E` explicit restores dimensional meaning but does not produce an absolute ruler. `c_E`
has units of length per time. Together with measured `G_obs`, it fixes mass per length. Exact
dimensional rank still leaves only the compactness combination

```text
G_obs M / (c_E^2 L).
```

Without native mass, an independently derived length, or another homothety-breaking datum, the
metric does not yet say which dimensionless depth is terrestrial, cosmological, or subnuclear.
That missing regime map is probably why correct structural results have repeatedly been given the
wrong physical interpretation.

## Honest result

The audit restores `c_E` to its proper foundational role and prevents `F` or `X` from being mistaken
for its origin. It does not close the time-live metric or absolute scale. It divides the remaining
work into two distinct problems:

1. what native realization law, if any, selects the global threading and scale; and
2. what intrinsic geometric limit represents physical small separation in non-central UDT matter.

Neither should be answered by imposing the spherical center or the desired reflection.
