## What did the boundary calculation actually find?

It found the exact kinds of geometric information a finite-cell wall can hold or exchange:

1. the wall's own metric (`h`);
2. how that metric changes in the outward direction (`K`);
3. the conformal-curvature momentum conjugate to that change (`E`);
4. the complementary momentum conjugate to the wall metric (`Pi_h`);
5. an extra flux where wall pieces meet.

## Did the conditional C2 action select one wall rule?

No. Several mathematically consistent choices survive:

- hold both wall geometry and slope fixed;
- hold geometry fixed and let the slope obey a natural equation;
- hold slope fixed and let geometry obey a natural equation;
- let both obey natural equations;
- add a separately derived mixed completion.

Nothing currently in the UDT premises uniquely chooses among these.

## Did the boundary select scale or mass?

No. A pure common change of scale remains invisible to the bare `C^2` boundary potential. On the
conformally flat branch, the bare potential and bare Noether charge both vanish. That is a statement
about this conditional mathematical object, not a theorem that physical mass vanishes.
