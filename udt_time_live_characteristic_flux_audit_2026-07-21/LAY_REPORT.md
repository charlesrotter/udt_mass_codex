# Lay report — what time dependence added

We asked whether letting the UDT metric genuinely change with time would tell us how the edge of the
universe must behave.

It gave us a sharper map, but not the missing final rule.

The easiest way to picture it is to separate a **road** from a **gate**. The metric determines the
roads along which disturbances can travel. In both conditional action candidates, those roads are
the metric's light cone. The EH candidate uses that cone once; the higher-derivative `C^2` candidate
uses it twice.

But knowing where the road is does not tell us how the gate at the edge is operated. It does not say
whether the gate holds the field value fixed, holds its conjugate response fixed, uses a mixture, or
lets different angular parts behave differently.

Time dependence also did not prove that the finite-cell seal is itself a lightlike surface. If we
tentatively describe the moving seal by `phi=0`, it can move slower than `c`, exactly at `c`, or
faster than `c` as a geometric level surface. Those correspond to timelike, null, and spacelike
boundary types. Reciprocity supplies the cone used to classify them; it does not choose the speed.

Most importantly, the angular part of the metric remains active. We constructed exact local
time-dependent angular distortions that satisfy the appropriate principal wave equation and carry
boundary flux even though the reciprocal `phi` variation at the seal is zero. So the `phi` seal is
one genuine wire, not a master switch for the complete metric.

There are still multiple internally consistent ways to make the boundary flux vanish. The two
action lanes also retain boundary-term and corner ambiguities. Therefore a time-live solve started
under one convenient wall rule would merely explore that chosen rule; it could not prove that UDT
selected it.

The useful accomplishment is that we have ruled out a tempting shortcut:

> “The metric has a light cone, therefore the finite-cell seal is characteristic, therefore its
> boundary rule is automatic.”

Each “therefore” is a separate missing step.
