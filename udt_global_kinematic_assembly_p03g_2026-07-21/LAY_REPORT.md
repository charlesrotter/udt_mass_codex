# P03G in lay language

The earlier map described everything the metric could look like in a tiny neighborhood around one
point. That is like cataloguing every kind of room that could appear in a building.

This stage asked the next neutral question: which rooms can actually be joined into one complete
building?

We found one firm construction rule. UDT's reciprocal “same-way” and “flipped-way” joins form a
precise algebra. If you follow a closed sequence of joins that is supposed to return exactly to the
same frame, the number of reciprocal flips must be even. Three flips cannot close by themselves;
two flips plus the right non-flipping adjustment can.

But a hinge rule is not a blueprint. The metric rules we currently have do not tell us:

- how many patches the universe needs or which ones overlap;
- where the reciprocal flips occur;
- how the two reciprocal directions join the complete four-dimensional frame;
- what the angular directions do at the mirror;
- which changes are allowed at the boundary;
- which caps or topology finish the finite cell;
- how degeneracies are handled; or
- which common-scale representative becomes physical.

Several concrete alternatives still work with everything currently established. Four different
ways of lifting the mirror to the whole frame survive. Multiple boundary choices survive. Several
different cap/topology examples survive. Even the surviving mixed clock/ruler geometry carries a
dimensionless freedom with at least two exact values.

So we did not fail to find a preferred universe. We learned that the present local metric map does
not yet contain enough global information for “preferred” to be a well-formed question.

Most importantly, we did **not** pick an action or bend the search toward GR, matter, a soliton, or a
known equation. All seven ways of realizing the local fields remain on the table, and none has yet
been proved to assemble into a complete universe.

The honest stopping point is: UDT already constrains the grammar of the whole geometry, but it has
not yet supplied the complete global sentence.
