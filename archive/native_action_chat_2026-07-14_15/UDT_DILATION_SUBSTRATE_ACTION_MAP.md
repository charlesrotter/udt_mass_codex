## 2. The distinction the derivation must preserve

Three objects must not be silently identified:

1. **Kinematic dilation factor** — how two frames compare clocks/lengths/observed energies.
2. **Geometric action** — what assigns dynamics to the metric or dilation geometry.
3. **Conserved ledger** — the charge/energy whose redistribution could be called matter recycling.

The owner guidance may constrain all three, but it does not make them identical by wording alone.

## 3. Configuration-space branches to keep live

### M — metric-only

Independent field: unrestricted physical metric \(g_{\mu\nu}\). Positional reciprocity is imposed
only after full variation unless equivalence with constrained variation is proved.

- M1: local covariant metric action, unrestricted variation.
- M2: reciprocal metric built in before variation.
- M3: matter as localized geometry; no independent carrier.

### S — metric plus substrate

Independent fields: \(g_{\mu\nu}\) plus an as-yet unspecified \(\Psi\). The \(S^2\) map is one
survivor candidate, not the definition of \(\Psi\).

- S1: conservation of total energy only, from time symmetry plus a sealed boundary.
- S2: an additional internal substrate current \(\nabla_\mu \mathcal J^\mu=0\).
- S3: localized matter is a topological or nontopological state of \(\Psi\).

### B — boundary/global ledger

Total mass is a finite-cell boundary/Hamiltonian charge. A global fixed-mass constraint inserted by
hand is inadmissible; the charge and its conservation must follow from a local action plus boundary
symmetry/conditions.
