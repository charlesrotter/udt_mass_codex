## 3. Candidate frozen before variation

### 3.1 Reciprocity supplies an invariant, not yet an energy

For a pair (A,B), define

\[
\Delta\phi_{AB}=\phi_B-\phi_A,
\qquad D_{AB}=e^{\Delta\phi_{AB}},
\qquad D_{BA}=D_{AB}^{-1}.
\]

The lowest algebraic reciprocal-even combination normalized to unity at zero difference is

\[
\Gamma_{AB}=\frac12\left(D_{AB}+D_{BA}\right)=\cosh(\Delta\phi_{AB}).
\]

On conditional branch H, (u_{AB}=d_{AB}/X=\tanh|\Delta\phi_{AB}|), so

\[
\Gamma(u)=\frac1{\sqrt{1-u^2}}.
\]

The **MR-1 candidate resistance readout** is frozen as

\[
\boxed{\mathcal R(u)=\Gamma(u)-1
=\frac1{\sqrt{1-u^2}}-1
=\cosh(\Delta\phi)-1.}
\]

The equality is DERIVED on branch H. Calling (mathcal R) an energy, mass multiplier, inertia,
or gravitational source is not derived.

### 3.2 Non-uniqueness gate

R1/R3 symmetry alone permits every

\[
\mathcal R_F(\Delta\phi)=F(\cosh\Delta\phi),\qquad F(1)=0,

including infinitely many positive diverging choices. Therefore MR-1 is the simplest candidate,
not a claimed unique consequence. One admissible counterfunction is enough to defeat uniqueness.

## 4. Preregistered analytic gates

### G1 — reciprocity and divergence

MR-1 must be even under (A\leftrightarrow B), vanish at coincidence, remain nonnegative for
(0\le u<1), and diverge as (u\to1^-).

### G2 — uniqueness

If another even, regular, nonnegative, divergent (F(\cosh\Delta\phi)) satisfies the same frozen
kinematic premises, “MR-1 is forced” fails.

### G3 — finite nonzero whole-scale equilibrium

For a uniform dilation/contraction (d_{ab}\mapsto\lambda d_{ab}), a static finite universe is
supported by P only if (dE_P/d\lambda=0) at some interior (0<\lambda<1/u_{\max}), with positive
second variation in the scale mode. Local vector cancellation is not accepted as a substitute.

### G4 — configuration stability

For the pair functional written in (phi_a), compute its full gradient and Hessian. A nonconstant
stationary configuration must survive the Hessian audit after the exact common-shift zero mode is
removed.

### G5 — additivity/locality/covariance

A proposed continuum action must state its off-shell fields, measure, kernel or neighborhood rule,
and four-dimensional contractions. If retaining the (u\to1) barrier requires finite-separation
all-pair data, it is nonlocal. If a controlled local limit removes the barrier or requires an
unforced kernel/cutoff, the native local-action claim fails.

### G6 — carrier emergence

A carrier counts as derived only if the construction supplies:

- its target/configuration space;
- a smooth global field on the admissible whole-cell domain;
- its transformation law from positional-dilation variables;
- a nontrivial topological sector if particle stability relies on topology;
- its continuum action without importing the existing (S^2), (L_2+L_4) model.

Merely observing that directions in three-space form (S^2) is insufficient.

### G7 — global (c,X,M_{\rm total}) closure

The candidate must define native total mass and provide two independent equations if both (c)
and (X) are solution outputs. Dimensional rearrangements of
(GM_{\rm total}/(c^2X)) count as one relation only.
