# Cover note — fresh-eyes review of the UDT brief

You're receiving a short, self-contained brief (the "UDT" document) describing
an attempt at an alternative physics built from a single geometric field. We
are **not** looking for validation or encouragement — we want your most
**skeptical, independent** read, and above all **where to look and what to
compute next.**

Please **engage it on its own terms.** It assumes no ΛCDM (no separate dark
matter / dark energy / inflation) and no Standard Model particle content; SM
names (quark, gauge, color) are labels only, to be used if and when the
geometry demands them. Read §0–5, then help us with the open problem in §6.

## The two questions we most need fresh thinking on (§6)

1. **What discretizes the family of cells?** The single-cell route to the
   particle spectrum is now **falsified** — a clean, pre-registered,
   data-blind test (see §4): one cell holds exactly one soliton, its internal
   modes are O(1), and it cannot make the lepton hierarchy or Koide 2/3. So the
   discrete family must live **across distinct cells**. But a single cell's
   depth is a *continuum* — so what selects a discrete set of stable cells?
   Candidates we can't yet decide between: the **topological/winding sectors**
   (where N=3, q=1/3 live), an **ensemble selection** (cells interacting in a
   shared back-reacted medium), or a **dynamic, time-dependent** phi–angular
   interaction.

2. **Where does the exponential hierarchy come from?** Lepton mass ratios are
   exponential (hundreds to thousands), not algebraic. Is **m ~ M_Planck·e^(−Γ)**
   the right shape, and what sets Γ from the angular/topological data and the
   cell's dilation depth?

## How to be maximally useful to us

- **Compute, don't just narrate.** If you can do a calculation — even a small,
  honestly-labeled toy, a worked limit, a scaling/Derrick argument, a derived
  inequality — **do it and show the math.** One worked result is worth ten
  paragraphs of direction.
- **Label every suggestion** as a *derived result*, an *argument/heuristic*,
  or a *guess*. We treat these very differently, and conflating them has burned
  us before.
- **Precedent vs import.** A *precedent* (a known system showing a mechanism
  *can* exist) is welcome. An *import* (adopting a borrowed model's equations
  in place of deriving ours) we must resist — our binding rule is that any
  needed structure must **come out of the metric/action**, not be added because
  it helps. If you reach for a standard gadget (a Skyrme term, a potential, a
  quantization rule, a sigma-model target space), **flag it as such** so we can
  test whether the geometry actually produces it. (Last round one such
  "import" — the Skyrme term — turned out to be **native** to this geometry;
  another was a dead end. We need to know which is which, by derivation.)
- **Attack our assumptions.** Name the single premise in the brief you would
  challenge first, and why. If you think the program is wrong somewhere, say so
  plainly.
- **Name the first computation.** If you had to run one calculation next, what
  would it be — and what specific result would **confirm** or **kill** the
  direction?

## Ground rules (same as the brief)
