# Lay report

Imagine the angular part of the metric as two instruments whose relative
volume changes with `phi`.

- On one side, instrument A is the quietest or shortest one.
- On the other side, instrument B is.
- Exactly at the reciprocal center, they are equally short.

Reciprocity swaps A and B. That symmetry respects the **pair**, but it cannot
say “choose A” or “choose B” at the center: either choice would favor one
member of an exactly exchanged pair.

There is a clean way to carry A into B: glue the two sides with a swap. But
the present finite-cell rule tells us only that scalar `phi` changes sign and
vanishes at the mirror. It does not yet say that the complete angular frame
must be glued by that swap.

So this work did not produce a particle or mass. It revealed that our
single-instrument question may have been wrong. The metric naturally carries
the duet. If matter emerges from this structure, it may use the relationship
between both instruments rather than selecting one in isolation.

That is directly relevant to the Hopfion, which already uses coordinated
angular information, but the connection must be checked rather than assumed.
