# Lay report — what phi is, and what is still missing

We were not completely confused about `phi`. We were combining two different questions.

The first question is: **what does `phi` measure?** That part is fairly clear. Imagine a paired clock
and ruler balance. When one side shrinks by a factor, the other expands by the reciprocal factor.
`phi` is the logarithmic reading on that balance. It has no units. It is not a distance, and it
cannot itself become a Planck length.

The second question is: **where is that balance located inside the complete four-dimensional
geometry?** That part is still open.

If we first mark one direction as the clock direction and one as its reciprocal spatial partner,
then the metric lets us calculate `phi` exactly from their ratio. Common rescaling does not disturb
that answer. But the full metric does not yet mark that pair for us. The angular and shift sectors
can mix all four directions, and the accepted coframe audits retain several different ways of
turning the reciprocal pair into a physical metric. Some make `phi` visible in the metric; one exact
control hides it completely.

That explains the recent atlas result. We deliberately gave the ten metric controls and `phi`
separate knobs so we would not impose a coupling. When only the `phi` knob was turned, the metric
stayed flat because the test contained no rule feeding one knob into the other. That is honest
bookkeeping, not a discovery that physical `phi` cannot shape geometry.

The finite-cell seal tells us that a static `phi` profile mirrors sign and is zero at the mirror. It
does not tell us how `phi` evolves, how its normal derivative is chosen, or how the rest of the
metric crosses the seal. Bootstrap currently says a finished universe must be self-consistent; it
does not yet supply the operation that builds or selects that universe.

So the clean result is:

- what `phi` measures: substantially settled;
- `phi` versus physical distance: cleanly separated;
- `phi` under Common-Scale Neutrality: settled—it is neutral, not the scale gauge;
- `phi` inside a chosen reciprocal metric block: exactly reconstructible;
- which reciprocal block the complete metric owns: open;
- whether `phi` is independently varied or metric-derived: open;
- native time evolution: open.

In plain language, we know what the dial reads, but the theory has not yet told us how the dial is
wired into the whole machine.

That missing wiring rule must be identified before a meaningful GPU time-live exploration. A
Planck length could later be drawn as a comparison mark on a physical distance axis; it cannot be
used as the endpoint of the dimensionless `phi` dial.
