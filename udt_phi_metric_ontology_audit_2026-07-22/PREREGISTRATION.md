## Whole question

Does the registered post-2026-07-01 UDT foundation determine what `phi` is in relation to the
complete metric or coframe, strongly enough to define the degrees of freedom of a future static or
time-live solution-space calculation?

The audit will not ask which interpretation produces a preferred universe, particle, action, or
topology. It will ask only what the registered sources entail and what remains compatible with them.

## Why this precedes a solve

The current atlases deliberately varied metric jets and `phi` jets independently. A future solver
cannot inherit that bookkeeping choice as physics without first deciding whether `phi` is:

1. the logarithmic reciprocal ratio of a clock/ruler comparison pair;
2. a parameter recoverable from a selected metric/coframe block;
3. an independent spacetime scalar field;
4. an independent off-shell variational field;
5. a coordinate or relational-depth label;
6. a CSN gauge/representative variable;
7. an on-shell solution or boundary datum; or
8. some typed combination of these roles.

Those roles are not interchangeable. The audit must preserve simultaneous roles when the sources
support them and must not force a single label merely for convenience.

## Required distinctions

Every load-bearing proposition will be typed along these independent axes:

- `kinematic_role`: reciprocal ratio/depth, comparison data, or none;
- `geometric_role`: coframe parameter, metric-block readout, scalar field, coordinate, or none;
- `off_shell_role`: independently varied, metric-derived, constrained, unspecified, or inapplicable;
- `CSN_role`: invariant/neutral, representative-selecting, gauge, conditional, or unspecified;
- `boundary_role`: seal value/parity/normal derivative, other boundary datum, or none;
- `dynamical_role`: equation-supplied, action-supplied, on-shell only, static-only, or open;
- `authority`: FOUNDING, DERIVED, CANONIZED, POSIT, CHOSE, WORKING, CONDITIONAL, OBSERVED,
  OPEN, comparison/readout, or historical-negative only.

The words `scalar`, `field`, `metric`, and `dilation` do not by themselves settle off-shell
ownership.

## Entailment questions

The audit will test, separately:

1. whether the positive reciprocal pair defines a dimensionless `phi` by a logarithmic ratio;
2. whether a chosen reciprocal coframe/metric block makes `phi` locally reconstructible;
3. whether the complete metric, without a chosen representative/slot/coframe, uniquely determines
   `phi`;
4. whether CSN acts on `phi` or only on the common scale;
5. whether the static finite-cell seal turns `phi` into an independently dynamical field;
6. whether any registered action, equation, fixed-point map, boundary relation, or bootstrap rule
   supplies the missing off-shell or on-shell realization relation;
7. whether at least two inequivalent ownership models satisfy the complete registered foundation;
8. whether any physical-length interpretation of `phi`, including a Planck-length endpoint, is
   registered or is a dimensional category error.

## Countermodel contract

Any non-entailment ruling must exhibit at least two explicit typed realizations that preserve the
same registered foundation while differing on the disputed ownership statement. A countermodel
fails if it violates reciprocal composition, dual Reciprocity, CSN, the chosen conditional
Lorentzian readout when invoked, or the static seal statement in its registered scope.

Candidate realization classes are preregistered, not accepted in advance:

- `DEPENDENT_RECIPROCAL_BLOCK`: `phi` is reconstructed from a selected determinant-one reciprocal
  coframe block and is not independently varied;
- `PARAMETRIZING_SCALAR`: `phi` is supplied as a scalar that parametrizes the reciprocal block, but
  its native equation/variation status is unspecified;
- `RELATIONAL_DEPTH_DATA`: `phi` represents comparison depth and acquires a scalar-field
  presentation only after a reference/section is chosen;
- `INDEPENDENT_OFF_SHELL_FIELD`: `phi` and the metric are varied independently and require a native
  relation or action.

The audit may reject or refine any candidate. It may not add an equation to rescue one.

## Scope and exclusions

This audit covers source semantics, exact algebraic reconstruction where already stated, and logical
countermodels. It does not solve ODEs/PDEs, run a GPU, choose an action, vary a field, select a CSN
representative, adopt a carrier, impose a Planck cutoff, or edit `LIVE.md`, `HANDOFF.md`, `CANON.md`,
research artifacts, fixed historical records, scripts, data, or current registries.

## Certification and falsification contract

The package must fail closed if:

- a cited line or file hash differs from the frozen base source;
- a pre-firewall source supplies affirmative ontology;
- dimensionless `phi` is equated to a physical length;
- CSN common-scale freedom is mislabeled as `phi` gauge freedom without source support;
- conditional coframe/slot/readout choices are upgraded to universal derivations;
- a static seal statement is upgraded to an action or time-live equation;
- an independent-field claim is made without an independent variation/equation source;
- a non-entailment claim lacks surviving complete-foundation countermodels;
- an inherited-unreviewed occurrence is presented as adjudicated;
- any audit output claims a physical realization, dynamics, action, topology, mass, or scale.

Mechanical verification will independently replay source hashes, row uniqueness, enum values,
logical cross-table constraints, countermodel gates, and mutation catches. Repository gates will
replay the six hard-frozen manifests, current-path map, links/frontier targets, tests, and original
dirty-checkout metadata without opening dirty-file contents.

## Maximum allowed conclusion

At most the audit may establish:

- exact roles of `phi` that are already FOUNDING, DERIVED, CANONIZED, or CONDITIONAL;
- an exact conditional reconstruction formula in an explicitly selected coframe/representative;
- whether a complete-metric-to-`phi` or `phi`-to-complete-metric realization relation is entailed;
- whether independent off-shell variation is entailed;
- the smallest precisely typed missing datum required before a static or time-live solver is
  well-posed.

If the foundation permits inequivalent ownership models, the result must remain `OPEN` and the
future numerical solve must not begin until Charles supplies or recognizes the missing premise.
