## Question and bounded regime

This is a neutral audit of whether the complete global/coframe branches already retained by the UDT metric-mapping program determine the two-ended accessibility behavior recorded by the preceding two-frame limit audit. It asks what each retained branch actually specifies when assembled with a stationary one-dimensional connector, including its clock leg, shift, spatial/angular line element, path, global completion, and regime field.

The bounded calculation is stationary and kinematic. It does not solve an action, field equation, carrier equation, bootstrap, or time-live evolution. It does not infer physical information transfer from a coordinate or anchor-relative null rate.

## Method and premise ledger

The audit is metric-led. It starts from each retained global/coframe branch and asks what data it supplies; it does not select branches for resembling a particle, cosmology, Hopfion, GR, or the desired two-ended limit.

- `pinned-by-HABIT`: none may be used as a conclusion-bearing premise. A diagonal chart, zero shift, spherical path, round angular geometry, or GR observer mechanics may appear only as a labeled control.
- `free-and-explored`: positive clock-threading factor `F`; admissible shift `B`; connector direction and path; spatial/angular line factor `H`; asymptotic behavior; ordinary-anchor placement; global gluing and seam interpretation where the retained branch leaves them free.
- `OPEN`: the physical scale/regime map, micro inheritance of macro observational-frame reciprocity, the complete time leg and observer congruence, action, matter carrier/source, boundary functional, and global bootstrap selection.
- `CONDITIONAL`: Lorentzian readout and any representative or chart choices required to express a stationary connector.

For every eligible branch, test whether an allowed positive clock threading, allowed shift, or allowed connector/angular path can change the accessibility class while preserving all explicitly recorded branch requirements. Such a family is a branch-preserving kinematic counterfamily only; it is not a claimed complete UDT universe.

Each selector is classified as `SELECTS`, `CONSTRAINS_NOT_SELECTS`, `SILENT`, or `OPEN`. No selector may be strengthened from its name or desired role.

## Certification and falsification

The audit must fail closed if any of these occurs:

- a frozen candidate row is missing or duplicated;
- overlapping presentations are counted as distinct universes without an identity argument;
- the completion classes are silently treated as disjoint physical topologies;
- topology-only, coframe-only, or distribution-only data are promoted to connector-complete;
- `F`, shift, angular/path weight, or connector domain is dropped from a load-bearing claim;
- a desired-result, particle, Hopf, GR, or SNe filter affects candidate coverage;
- local `c_E`, coordinate rate, anchor-relative rate, and physical information transfer are conflated;
- a counterfamily violates the branch conditions it is claimed to preserve;
- an unrecorded action, carrier, boundary, or observer mechanics is imported.

Required evidence includes deterministic source hashes, complete row coverage and overlap counts, runnable exact algebra for the generic connector and every load-bearing counterfamily, raw stdout/stderr with hashes, and an independent implementation or derivation of the load-bearing result.
