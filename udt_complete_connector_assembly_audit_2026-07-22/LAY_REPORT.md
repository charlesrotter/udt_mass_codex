# Lay Report

We checked whether the large map of UDT metric shapes already tells us how the clock behavior must
work. It does not—but the reason is now much sharper.

Most of the map describes how space can close, twist, cap off, mirror, or form different global
shapes. Those are real geometric findings, but they do not yet tell us how a clock is threaded
through each shape. Counting every table row as a universe would have hidden that distinction.

There is one especially useful control metric in the map, shown repeatedly against 12 possible
global completion types. That repetition is one metric viewed through 12 compatibility questions,
not 12 solved universes. Its two angular directions carry the
beautiful reciprocal factors `exp(-phi)` and `exp(+phi)`, but its clock coefficient is simply
constant. In that control, a more strongly warped spatial path is longer by exactly the same factor
that it takes longer to cross. The measured rate stays at ordinary `c_E`; the angular reciprocity
does not make it blow up or freeze.

That does not disprove your two-ended picture. The control metric is conditional and has never been
selected as the universe. What it proves is that reciprocal spatial/angular warping does not, by
itself, force the clock to carry the same factor.

None of the 12 presentations is yet a complete global connector. Some may be compatible after more
boundary or gluing data; one is intrinsically singular; the nonintegrable-orbit class conflicts
with this particular commuting torus control.

The remaining missing piece is therefore quite concrete. The metric must tell us which direction
inside the complete geometry represents the physical clock, how that clock direction tilts or
shifts relative to space, and how it is connected to the reciprocal angular structure. Once that is
known, the effective travel behavior follows mechanically.

In plainer language: we have mapped many possible shapes of the road, but UDT has not yet told us
which clock is mounted in the vehicle or whether the road itself is moving beneath it. The next
metric-led task is to map every clock-direction candidate already present in the complete metric,
without choosing the one that gives the desired answer.

The SNe work is unchanged. It can later tell us which completed large-scale clock/road combination
matches observation, but it cannot manufacture the missing connection between them.
