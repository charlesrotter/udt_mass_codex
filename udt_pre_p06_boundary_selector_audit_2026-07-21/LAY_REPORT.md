# Pre-P06 boundary audit in lay language

UDT already tells us one real thing about the edge of a static mirrored cell: the clock-versus-ruler
imbalance must be zero there. Think of that as one dial on a large control panel being set to zero.
UDT also says the rate at which that dial approaches zero is free.

The rest of the panel is still unwired. The existing principles do not say whether the overall size,
angular shape, twisting components, or their outward slopes are fixed or free. They also do not tell
us whether the relevant edge is the non-null mirror fold, the null WR-L horizon, a moving boundary,
or a surface that is crossed or identified rather than terminal.

The two conditional bulk rulebooks need different boundary machinery:

- The fourth-order `C²` rulebook needs both the metric and its outward slope accounted for, plus
  corner information.
- The second-order EH rulebook can be completed in familiar ways, but choosing one of those ways
  would be importing a convention. UDT has not selected it.

There is a second, independent ambiguity. Even if we chose which boundary quantities may vary, we
could still add boundary terms that leave the interior equation unchanged but alter the boundary
momentum or charge. The freely surviving Euler coefficient proves this directly inside both
conditional action families.

So the answer is not “UDT has no boundary information.” It has one authentic boundary wire. The
answer is that one wire does not yet determine the full socket arrangement or the circuit attached
to it.

That is why P06 cannot honestly begin yet.
