# Lay report

The Hopf sphere needs two independent kinds of motion:

- moving north or south; and
- moving around the sphere.

The part of the metric bridge we already had definitely supplies the first
motion. By itself, it can slide the exact seed north or south, but it cannot
reshape an arbitrary sphere-valued field.

The complete metric contains two promising pieces related to the second
motion:

- angular shear rotates a locally preferred pair of angular axes;
- the four cross-coupling fields form a connection that tells angular phases
  how to compare from one place to another.

These are real geometric structures. But neither is yet the missing physical
phase field. The shear angle depends on the chosen angular chart and becomes
undefined when the two angular directions are identical. The connection can
have curvature and global holonomy; even when it is locally integrable, its
integrated phase can be removable coordinate choice unless a physical
section or boundary framing fixes it.

So the metric has not produced the full Hopf carrier. It has produced:

```text
one definite sphere direction of motion,
one local phase-like axis angle,
and one phase-comparison connection.
```

The open problem is now to determine whether the metric itself has a
frame-independent way to assemble those three ingredients into one global
field.

Bootstrap still belongs just outside this construction. Once complete global
fields and equations exist, bootstrap may accept only a narrow family—perhaps
through total density. It cannot choose the missing local phase or section
for us.
