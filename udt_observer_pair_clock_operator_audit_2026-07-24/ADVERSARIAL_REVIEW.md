# Adversarial review

## Strongest overclaim risk

The exact balanced matrix looks like a Lorentz boost. It would be easy to
declare its `sech(delta)` factor the physical mutual clock law. That does not
follow unless the founding `K` pairing is also the physical observer
interval and the endpoint observer frames are fixed. Both remain separate
typed premises.

## Strongest false-negative risk

The orthonormal spatial transporter is the identity in the static diagonal
control. This does not refute the founding reciprocal operator. The operator
acts on the named coordinate coframe/covector conversion pair, for which the
metric transport agrees exactly. It only refutes identifying that
coordinate-component agreement automatically with a physical spatial
observer boost.

## Endpoint-field risk

`S(phi(p))^-1 S(phi(q))` is exact algebra and composes perfectly. It does not
prove that a single global signed `phi` field represents every centerless
nonnegative pair separation. The current ontology correctly marks that join
open.

## Path risk

The abstract operator requires only additive ordered depth. It is wrong to
make shortest-path selection a prerequisite for its existence. Conversely,
it is also wrong to claim a complete global physical operator without
handling angular transport, holonomy, multiple geodesics, and endpoint
frames.

## Gauge risk

The half trace is invariant under a common conjugation but changes under
independent endpoint frame refactorizations. It is not automatically a
frame-independent pair observable.

## Scope risk

Nothing in this audit derives the distance-to-depth function, `X_max`, a
mass response, an action/source/carrier, a density window, or a CMB
prediction.
