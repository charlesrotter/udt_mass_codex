# Next metric-led step

The next bounded question is no longer how to invent a clock operator.

It is:

```text
What exact geometric object supplies the additive ordered depth delta(p,q)
to the already-derived reciprocal pair operator in a complete centerless
finite-cell geometry?
```

The audit should classify, without selecting in advance:

1. a primitive genuinely bilocal depth kernel;
2. an observer-based family of depth charts `rho_p(q)`;
3. a difference of one global signed local field;
4. a line/path integral of the reciprocal current;
5. a function of complete metric pair distance;
6. a set-valued or branchwise object at a cut locus.

Each candidate must be tested for:

- zero at coincident observers;
- nonnegative physical separation versus signed ordered comparison;
- reversal/inverse behavior;
- composition only on its legitimate domain;
- no privileged center;
- covariance under observer-frame changes;
- behavior on the complete round branch and registered nonround control;
- angular/shift sensitivity;
- global single-valuedness and cut-locus behavior; and
- compatibility with the named exponential clock channel.

No action, source, carrier, mass, density, `X_max` value, CMB fit, time-live
solve, or GPU work is justified by this next step.
