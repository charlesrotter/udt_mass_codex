# Lay decision tree

1. **What makes the GR connection unique?**
   - A chosen metric, no torsion, and preservation of that metric.

2. **What changes in pre-scale UDT?**
   - UDT supplies a conformal family of equally calibrated metrics, leaving one small connection
     freedom.

3. **Does Reciprocity remove that freedom?**
   - Yes, completely—if reciprocal structure has first been attached consistently to spacetime.

4. **Does the simple clock/radial-only attachment work with a general angular sector?**
   - Not generically. The angular directions impose an additional scaling relation.

5. **What attachment works in the tested class?**
   - A full four-direction structure containing two reciprocal pairs.

6. **What does that predict conditionally for the angular pair?**
   - One angular direction shrinks by `exp(-phi)` while the other grows by `exp(+phi)`.

7. **Have we seen that pattern before?**
   - Yes. It is exactly the reciprocal angular block found independently in Hopf geometry.

8. **Does that prove UDT has two reciprocal pairs or derive the Hopfion?**
   - No. The second physical pair, periodicity, topology, carrier, and action remain unselected.

9. **What is the sharpened missing principle?**
   - Whether Reciprocity is only the founding longitudinal pair or a complete two-pair organization
     of the four-dimensional metric.
