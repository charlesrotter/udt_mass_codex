# Lay Decision Tree

1. **Does scale-free UDT allow an ordinary two-derivative angular term with a fixed coefficient?**
   No, not for a neutral dimensionless angular variable. It changes when the common ruler is
   recalibrated.
2. **Does it allow four-derivative structures?**
   Yes. Several survive the recalibration, so allowance is not selection.
3. **Does the reciprocal angular/Hopf geometry choose one balance?**
   Not yet. It fixes a topological winding/flux, but smooth angular shapes with different local
   normalizations remain possible.
4. **Do the finite wall, bootstrap, or `Xmax` choose the number?**
   Not with their current definitions. They constrain the arena and dimensional form but supply no
   coefficient equation.
5. **What is the promising interpretation?**
   Four-derivative geometry may belong to the scale-free phase. A two-derivative material term may
   appear only when the whole universe chooses a physical curved scale.
6. **What must be derived next?**
   Show from one complete metric equation—not from the old particle action—that the angular shape
   and physical scale are selected and that the lower-derivative term appears with one forced
   normalization.
7. **What if that fails?**
   Then this economical one-parent bridge is not supplied by the conditional `C^2` branch, and the
   action/variation-domain choice must be reconsidered before any more particle numerics.
