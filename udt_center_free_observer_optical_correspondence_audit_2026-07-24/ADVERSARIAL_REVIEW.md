# Adversarial review

## Strongest positive challenge

Does “the metric supplies an optical correspondence” silently import a light
field?

No physical propagation is claimed. The Levi-Civita connection, null
geodesics, screen quotient, curvature, and Jacobi equation are geometric
objects of a supplied Lorentzian metric. Identifying one member with actual
light, information, or luminosity requires an additional registered readout
or native field law.

## Null/rest conflation challenge

The round ultrastatic and centered static controls make null and spatial
transverse radii coincide. A time-dependent conformal metric gives

```text
D_A(null)=a_source chi,
D_rest(observer slice)=a_observer chi,
```

so the equality is not universal.

## WR-L globality challenge

Could observer-centered WR-L charts simply be ordinary coordinate charts on
one centerless universe?

The prior curvature-atlas audit already refuted that interpretation. The
new transverse calculation independently gives

```text
K_rad=1/(2XR),
K_tan=1/(XR),
```

and a varying spatial scalar invariant. Recentered seats cannot be related by
isometries of one homogeneous metric. The residual pair-chart
interpretation remains working, not canon.

## Isotropy challenge

No preferred center does not require isotropy. The squashed-`S3` control is
homogeneous and centerless but direction dependent. The constant-curvature
theorem applies only when every observer has the same direction-independent
polar law.

## Xmax challenge

The round branch has maximum pair distance and zero transverse area at the
antipode. The constant-curvature static control has infinite lapse dilation
at half the completed spatial diameter. Therefore no one of these endpoints
can be renamed physical `X_max` without a branch-specific theorem.

## SNe challenge

The existing SNe score uses WR-L clock, centered areal radius, and the
registered luminosity relation. The score is untouched. What is corrected is
its scope: no complete global center-free WR-L metric has yet been supplied.

## Independence caveat

Production uses SymPy. Independent verification uses standard-library
trigonometry and exact rational arithmetic without importing SymPy or the
production script. This is implementation independence within one active
model context, not a fresh external model-family audit.
