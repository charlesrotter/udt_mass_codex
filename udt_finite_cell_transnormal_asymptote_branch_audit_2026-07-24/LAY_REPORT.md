# Lay report

We now know exactly why the calculation cannot yet produce the final
number—and it is not because the distance formula is missing.

The repository contains three nearly complementary pieces:

1. The older WR-L metric says how clock slowing approaches an endpoint. Its
   actual proper-distance endpoint is `2X`. But that model does not contain
   the complete global angular shape needed to find the greatest distance
   between any two observers.

2. The reciprocal angular metric says exactly how to turn its `phi` into
   distance once its remaining profile is known. But that `phi` stretches
   two angular directions; it has not been shown to slow clocks.

3. The round conditional Bach solution has a complete closed spatial shape.
   In it, the distance from the neutral middle to the angular endpoint is
   one quarter of the full diameter. But its clocks run at a constant rate,
   so its angular `phi` is not yet the clock-dilation field.

In short:

```text
clock behavior       lives in one branch;
complete angular shape lives in another;
complete round diameter lives in the strongest conditional branch.
```

We are not allowed to paste them together and call that UDT.

We also learned something global. On a smooth closed space, any ordinary
real-valued field has a highest and lowest point. At those places its
spatial slope is zero. Therefore one smooth `phi` cannot act as an
everywhere-running distance coordinate across the whole closed universe.

That does not prevent a finite maximum distance. It means the depth variable
must work more like:

- map coordinates that need more than one chart;
- an asymptote that no observer occupies;
- a direction/angle-like variable;
- a distance defined between pairs of observers rather than painted as one
  scalar over the universe; or
- a horizontal distance in a twisted clock geometry.

The next calculation is therefore not a blind theory hunt. We should test
whether the clock-dilation part of the metric can be joined to the known
round reciprocal-angular geometry without making the metric singular. That
will tell us whether the two uses of `phi` are one phenomenon or genuinely
different objects.
