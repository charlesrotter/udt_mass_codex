# Lay Decision Tree

Start with UDT's exact reciprocal pair: one clock/ruler factor shrinks by the same amount that the
other grows.

1. **Does swapping the pair reverse depth?** Yes. That algebra is exact.
2. **Is the swap automatically a physical reflection of the previously written clock/radial
   metric?** No. In that diagonal reading it changes the metric's overall sign, which a positive
   change of units cannot repair.
3. **Can the reciprocal pairing be read in another mathematically valid way?** Yes. If the
   off-diagonal dual pairing is chosen as a null-coordinate Lorentz metric, the same reciprocal
   factors are an ordinary boost and the swap is an ordinary reflection.
4. **Did UDT already tell us to make that physical identification?** No. The source calls that
   matrix a faithful *pairing formalization*, not the physical metric.
5. **Would choosing it finish the whole seal?** Still no. The two angular directions can remain
   unchanged, both flip, or reflect about any axis. The time-dependent/off-diagonal sector also has
   an unresolved rule. These alternatives obey the algebra but give different boundary tangents.

So the audit found a real structural bridge, but not a unique law. The smallest missing ingredient
is not another coefficient. It is a metric-native rule saying which quadratic reading of the
reciprocal pair is physical and how the same seal acts on the normal, angular, and time-on coframe.
Until that rule is derived, the finite-cell seal supplies only the already known scalar fact:
`phi=0` there while its normal slope is free.
