# Lay report

The metric gives us six local ways to tilt a frame: three behave like
boosts and three like rotations. We asked whether those two teams remain
meaningful across a whole finite universe-cell.

Where the gradient of `phi` points in a time-like direction, the answer is
yes. The teams remain three-and-three. But as the geometry bends, members
of one team can feed into the other. That mixing is not arbitrary: the
ordinary geometric connection contains exactly three pieces that keep the
teams separate and three pieces that mix them.

Where `dphi` becomes space-like, there are still two groups of three, but
they no longer mean “an observer's boosts and rotations.” The geometry has
changed which kind of direction is being singled out.

Where `dphi` becomes light-like, the clean two-team division collapses
into a one-way layered structure. Where `dphi` becomes zero, even the
distinguished direction disappears; approaching that point from different
directions gives different answers.

We applied those rules to all twelve finite-cell shapes already catalogued.
The important caution is that these are twelve possible kinds of global
completion, not twelve finished solutions. We still have no complete
equation-selected metric-and-`phi` universe to tell us which causal path
is actually taken.

The useful new clue is this: common-scale neutrality preserves the
three-plus-three split, but it does not preserve the amount of
Levi-Civita mixing. Selecting the physical scale representative may
therefore be inseparable from deciding which transport/mixing is physical.
