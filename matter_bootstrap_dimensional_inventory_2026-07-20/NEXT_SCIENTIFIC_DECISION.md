# Next Scientific Decision

## Recommended bounded derivation

Audit whether the UDT metric's complete angular/finite-cell structure, CSN, bootstrap, and conditional
`Xmax` reciprocity produce a native **relative derivative normalization** before a carrier target or
matter action is chosen.

The derivation should remain carrier-neutral at entry. Enumerate metric-native angular scalars at the
first two nontrivial derivative orders, track their scale weights and finite-cell boundary terms, and
ask whether their relative coefficient is:

- uniquely zero;
- uniquely dimensionless times `Xmax^2`;
- tied to another independently derived dimensional eigenvalue;
- or underdetermined.

Do not insert a round `S2`, the Faddeev-Skyrme action, EH equations, a physical box wall, or a target
particle size. `Xmax` reciprocity must be tested as a full compatibility condition but not assumed to
select absolute `Xmax`.

## Maximum allowed conclusion

At most, derive or rule out a metric-native relative coefficient/scale relation. Even a successful
`kappa/xi ~ Xmax^2` result would select a particle-to-cell ratio only. It would not by itself derive
the carrier, complete matter action, total mass, absolute `Xmax`, physical time evolution, or
unconditional mass.
