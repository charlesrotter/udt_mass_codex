## What was found

The computer soliton has a stable-looking size because two parts of its chosen energy law pull in
opposite directions. One part gets more expensive as the object grows; the other gets more expensive
as it shrinks. Their balance picks a size.

But the relative strength of those two parts was supplied to the code. It was normally written as
`1` and `1`, which makes the hidden measuring stick look invisible. In physical units, their ratio is
a length squared. The soliton did not create that ruler; it used it.

- The finite-box stability evidence still says that branch has a local stable configuration under
  its recorded premises.
- The mass/virial calculations still provide their stated conditional consistency tests.

None of those results determines how long one code unit is.

## What Xmax could do

If UDT itself later proves that the coefficient ruler is a fixed fraction of `Xmax`, the particle
size could become a fixed fraction of the universe's maximum reach. That would connect small and
large scales elegantly. It still would not tell us the absolute size of the whole universe; the
global bootstrap would need one more independent condition.
