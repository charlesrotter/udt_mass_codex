## Whole question

The parent atlas found that an integral torus fiber with normalized metric
`H` has a canonical set of shortest primitive dual characters

```text
W_min(H)=argmin {w^T H^-1 w : w in Z^2 primitive}/(w identified with -w).
```

This audit asks:

> What is the complete continuous chamber, wall, and vertex structure of
> `W_min` for the UDT angular metric, and when does that set define a global
> rank-one character local system and projected connection through each of
> the twelve registered finite-cell completion classes?

This is not a search for a Hopf result. No character, sign, completion, cap,
phase section, or physical interpretation is preferred.

## Exact bounded frame

### Continuous local frame

The positive determinant-one angular metrics generated by

```text
D=[[exp(-phi),shear],[0,exp(phi)]],
H=D^T D
```

with `(phi,shear)` in `R^2` are treated exactly. The audit must prove whether
this parameterization covers the full positive determinant-one two-metric
space and must stop if it does not.

The primitive dual lattice is the supplied integral character lattice of a
genuine torus fiber. It is not assumed on nontoric completion classes.

### Global frame

All twelve inherited completion classes are classified exactly once.
The audit distinguishes:

1. the set-valued invariant `W_min`;
2. a unique unoriented character line;
3. an oriented/signed character;
4. the projected `U1` connection;
5. a phase section;
6. physical selection of any of the above.

No level may be promoted to the next.

1. Derive the upper-half-plane coordinates of `H` and the exact character
   norm.
2. Define every unique-character chamber by all primitive inequalities.
3. Derive every possible equality wall without truncating the lattice.
4. Prove or disprove that independent co-shortest primitive characters must
   form a unimodular pair.

6. Derive `GL(2,Z)` covariance of `W_min` and of the projected connection.
7. Determine local constancy away from walls and the exact information lost
   at a transverse wall crossing.
8. Derive the complete shear-free `phi` path as an analytic control.
9. Classify caps, periodic monodromy, mirrors, nonorientation, rank changes,
   anholonomy, and boundaries for every registered completion.
10. Audit whether any existing UDT premise makes the shortest-character
    invariant physically operative.

## Falsification contract

The proposed continuous atlas fails if any of the following occurs:

- the `(phi,shear)` chart misses a positive determinant-one metric;
- a pair of independent shortest primitive characters with determinant
  magnitude greater than one is possible;
- the claimed wall segment admits another strictly shorter primitive
  character;
- the claimed maximum tie multiplicity is false;
- `W_min` or the projected connection fails `GL(2,Z)` covariance;
- a unique character changes along a continuous path that avoids every tie
  wall;
- a global character is asserted where the torus lattice disappears;
- a cap extension is asserted without the character annihilating the
  collapsed cycle or a separately supplied vanishing amplitude;
- an unoriented line is silently promoted to a signed phase;
- any physical selection, carrier, action, density law, or matter solution is
  inferred from the geometric invariant alone.

Exact symbolic identities must have zero residual. Finite independent
controls must agree below `1e-11`. Catch-proofs must exercise every failure
class.

the exact conditions for a branchwise character local system and projected
connection, and completion-scoped obstructions. It may not derive or adopt a
physical phase, carrier, action, source, boundary functional, density map,
mass, stable solution, or bootstrap closure.
