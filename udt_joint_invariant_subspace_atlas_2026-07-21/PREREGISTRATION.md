## Classifications

For every configuration/family, record:

- dimension of the unital associative algebra generated by the family;
- dimension of its commutant, the center of that commutant, and the metric-self-adjoint portion;
- dimension of the smallest family-invariant Krylov subspace generated by `grad phi`;
- when that orbit has dimension one, two, or three: its metric rank and signature, with null and
  degenerate cases retained;
- all numerically certified minimal central reducing-block ranks obtained from real,
  metric-self-adjoint central projectors;
- whether those blocks give no rank-two block, one distinguished rank-two block, one unique
  complementary `2+2` split, multiple competing rank-two blocks/splits, a continuous/noncentral
  ambiguity, a complex/Jordan obstruction, or a numerical uncertainty.

Separately diagonalize the Riemann and Weyl operators on bivectors. Inventory every isolated real
one-dimensional eigenspace whose bivector is simple, retain its plane signature, count complementary
unordered plane pairs, and mark all repeated/complex/near-threshold eigenspaces unresolved rather
than selecting a vector inside them.

The atlas characterizes existence, multiplicity, degeneracy, and ambiguity. It must never rank a
plane by resemblance to the historical angular sector.
