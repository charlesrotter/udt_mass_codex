promoting one of the 11,983 smaller-family unique splits: every such case becomes full-algebra
irreducible when the complete Riemann or Weyl curvature family joins it. Nor should the 2,225
multiple-split rows be ranked; each has three competing pairings.

The next objective mapping question, if separately authorized, is no longer “which local plane looks
angular?” It is whether the smaller-family planes define a coherent **transported or global pattern**
even though they are not invariant under the full pointwise curvature algebra.

A proper continuation should inventory, without physical ranking:

1. parallel-transport and holonomy orbits of the complete ensemble histories;
2. higher-jet differential concomitants and their degeneracies;
3. Frobenius/integrability status of every constructively generated candidate distribution;
4. compatibility across finite-cell seals and time-live histories;
5. cases where no globally consistent distribution exists.

That map must distinguish a transported/global structure from a pointwise algebraic selector and
must not import an action, preferred section, carrier, or desired universe. It should be preregistered
only after a fresh `MAP -> PONDER` decision with Charles.

No continuation is authorized by this package.
