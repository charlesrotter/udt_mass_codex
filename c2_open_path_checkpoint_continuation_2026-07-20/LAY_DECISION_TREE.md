# Lay decision tree

## What did the 22 paths do?

- One path reached the already familiar round geometry and passed the stronger checks.
- Six paths seemed to reach new nonround geometries in the small mathematical model. When the full
  curvature equation was checked, all six failed badly. They were mirages caused by squeezing the
  geometry into too small a set of adjustable shapes.
- Fifteen paths honestly remain open at the registered time or safety limits.

So the computer work strengthened the round branch without proving that it is the only possible
geometry.

## Was the stable Hopfion round?

No. Its shape in ordinary three-dimensional space was a twisted torus: more like a knotted smoke
ring than a ball. But the mathematical *color wheel* used to describe its internal directions was a
round sphere, and that color wheel was assumed rather than derived from UDT. The surrounding metric
was also held fixed.

## Why does that help?

Think of a round room containing a nonround knot. Making the room less round is not necessarily how
the knot is made. The knot comes from how a thread winds and links inside the room.

Our recent metric search mainly varied the shape of the room. The Hopfion result says the missing
physics may instead be the rule for threading directions through that room. In geometric language,
that means a section, connection, framing, or holonomy in the metric's angular/null-direction
structure.

## What follows?

- If the metric itself supplies a nontrivial angular section whose linking number matches the Hopf
  structure, that is a genuine bridge candidate from geometry toward matter.
- If it supplies only a round fiber with no selected section or transport, the Hopf carrier remains
  an independent posit and this route stops.
- Either result is useful. Do not add an action, carrier, source, or boundary rule to force success.
