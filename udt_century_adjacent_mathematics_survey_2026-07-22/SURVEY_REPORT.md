## Result first

The survey found no historical theorem or established physical mechanism that closes UDT's open
action, carrier, source, boundary-charge, bootstrap, or scale joins. It did find a materially better
way to pose the search.

The central new `LEAD` is a local/global fork:

1. If the missing selector is regular, local, covariant, and built only from the complete UDT
   metric/coframe/`phi` data, Peetre-Slovak and natural-operator theory reduce it to a finite-jet
   differential object. After its output type, parity, and CSN weight are fixed, that class can be
   enumerated and exhausted rather than hunted impressionistically.
2. If that exhaustive local census is empty, the selector cannot be repaired by trying more local
   curvature combinations. It must instead be global, boundary-dependent, nonlocal, genuinely
   variational, or a typed whole-solution bootstrap relation—or else require a new premise.

The second new `LEAD` corrects the scope of the existing bivector result. The absence of isolated
real simple curvature eigenbivector planes in the registered two-jets is strong local evidence, but
it is not the complete persistence test. Ambrose-Singer and infinitesimal-holonomy theory point to
the algebra generated by curvature and its covariant derivatives. A proper subspace must survive
that common algebra to define a parallel geometric reduction. This is the metric-wide replacement
for chasing individual promising eigenspaces.

These are methodological and mathematical leads, not new UDT physics. No external action, field
equation, flow, carrier, connection, boundary term, fixed-point map, or scale condition was adopted.

## The most consequential lessons

### 1. The local-selector problem is potentially closable

Locality is load-bearing. Peetre's linear theorem and Slovak's nonlinear extension do not tell UDT
that its selector is local. They say that **if** UDT derives a regular local operation between
specified geometric bundles, it has locally finite differential order. Natural-operator invariant
theory can then classify candidates by their finite jets, covariance, tensor type, parity, and
homothety/CSN weight.

This converts a vague search for an elegant curvature expression into a fail-closed program:

```text
type the desired output
-> verify locality and regularity
-> enumerate natural finite-jet candidates
-> impose Reciprocity and CSN weights
-> test branch rank and degeneracy
-> either find a metric-native distinguisher or close the local class
```

The output type cannot be guessed. A scalar representative selector, a real two-plane projector,
a closed two-form, and a boundary density are different natural-operator problems. The downstream
join must determine which types are relevant.

### 2. Holonomy is the correct persistence test

The global atlas already distinguishes open-path Kato projector transport from Levi-Civita
holonomy. The literature makes the missing test precise:

```text
pointwise curvature spectrum
    < curvature transported around reachable paths
    < algebra generated by curvature and covariant derivatives
    < globally glued bundle or quotient
```

An eigenplane at one point can rotate away. A proper parallel distribution must be invariant under
the generated holonomy algebra. Conversely, absence of an isolated simple curvature eigenplane
does not alone exclude every invariant subspace of the full algebra. The next metric-native audit
should therefore construct the common real invariant-subspace lattice of the curvature-jet algebra
on complete full-orchestra branches.

The de Rham product lesson is conditional: an appropriate nondegenerate invariant splitting can
imply product structure under additional hypotheses. It does not select that split, and the original
positive-definite theorem cannot be transferred silently to the Lorentzian UDT cell.

### 3. Local geometry, global topology, and physical realization are separate gates

Connections transport data on a supplied bundle. Characteristic classes classify data on a
supplied global bundle. Universal-bundle and gluing theory classify possible globalizations after a
group and cocycle are supplied. None chooses UDT's structure group, cap cycles, seam, periods,
orientation, quotient, section, or carrier.

This agrees with the atlas's 12 retained completion families. The historical mathematics supports
the present stopping rule: compute global cocycles only for locally persistent structures, then keep
all compatible completions until a UDT relation ranks them.

### 4. Existence is not selection, and spectrum is not shape

The Yamabe sequence is a particularly sharp control. Even the existence of a distinguished-looking
representative in every compact Riemannian conformal class required a chain of hypotheses and hard
global analysis. That does not make constant scalar curvature UDT's physical clock/scale rule, and
the signature and functional differ.

Milnor, Sunada, and Gordon-Webb-Wolpert show that spectral data can be shared by distinct geometries.
Therefore an eigenvalue route to `X_max` needs, at minimum, a derived native operator, derived
boundary conditions, a degeneracy audit, and an additional reason that a particular eigenvalue is
physical. A spectrum alone cannot supply unique geometry or seal shape.

### 5. Bootstrap is not yet a mathematical operation

Schauder, Leray-Schauder, and local bifurcation theory all begin with objects UDT does not yet have:
a map or nonlinear operator, a domain and codomain, a topology, compactness or a priori bounds, and
often a derivative and transversality condition. They can test a bootstrap once it is written; they
cannot generate it.

The current simultaneous-density idea remains the least circular bootstrap form, but it is still a
schema:

```text
(trial whole solution) -> (native mass and proper volume from that solution)
                       -> (updated whole solution)
```

The arrows, function space, boundary data, and isolation criterion remain `OPEN`.

### 6. Action, boundary, charge, and time are one dependency chain

Inverse-variational results block a common shortcut: a geometrically interesting differential
equation need not come from an action, a local Lagrangian need not patch globally, and varying only a
symmetric ansatz need not reproduce the full equations. Any future equation must pass covariant
Helmholtz, global-patching, and symmetric-criticality tests.

Boundary mathematics then requires the variation domain and boundary polarization before a
differentiable generator or integrable charge exists. The GR examples are only controls. Their
actions, boundary terms, asymptotic phase spaces, and charges are not UDT inputs.

Likewise, Ricci flow and harmonic-map heat flow demonstrate that a useful metric evolution is an
additional chosen equation. Availability of a curvature tensor does not make its flow physical time.

### 7. Hopf topology remains valuable and conditional

Hopf and Whitehead certify topological information only after a global map, domain, target,
orientation, and normalization are supplied. Derrick, Vakulenko-Kapitanskii, Faddeev-Niemi, and
Lin-Yang constrain supplied functionals. Together they reinforce the current ledger:

- the existing implementation is full 3D Hopf-capable;
- the conditional topology calculation is meaningful inside its supplied quotient/carrier premises;
- topology does not derive the `S2` carrier, its round target metric, `L2+L4`, coefficients,
  boundary completion, physical time law, or unconditional mass.

## What may have been hiding in plain sight

The survey does not reveal a forgotten external formula. It reveals a structural dichotomy already
latent in UDT:

> Either the complete metric contains a local covariant selector, in which case its finite-jet
> natural-operator class can be exhaustively enumerated; or the selector is intrinsically a relation
> of the whole finite cell—its gluing, boundary variation, global spectrum, or simultaneous
> bootstrap—and local curvature drilling cannot find it.

That is a smaller and cleaner fork than “find the action.” It also explains why many previous leads
ended `CONDITIONAL` or `UNDERDETERMINED`: compatibility was being asked to do the job of selection.

The smallest genuinely missing selector remains an **operational relation with a typed domain and
codomain**. The survey supplies no honest basis for naming a new physical postulate.
