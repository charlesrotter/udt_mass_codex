# Source Notes by Frozen Family

These notes are navigation summaries. Exact hypotheses, match grades, firewall prohibitions, and
returned UDT questions are in `SOURCE_CENSUS.tsv`.

## F01 — natural operators and concomitants

- [Peetre 1959](https://doi.org/10.7146/math.scand.a-10574): locality makes a linear operator
  differential; UDT must still establish locality.
- [Slovak 1988](https://www.math.muni.cz/~slovak/Papers/nonlinear_Peetre.pdf): a nonlinear extension
  under regularity hypotheses; this is the main route to finite-jet closure.
- [Slovak 1992](https://doi.org/10.4310/jdg/1214453183): natural operations reduce to invariant
  theory once the input/output bundles are typed.
- [Lovelock 1971](https://doi.org/10.1063/1.1665613): a sharp uniqueness theorem inside a narrow
  tensor class; a control against silently assuming that class.

## F02 — holonomy, decomposition, and persistent structure

- [de Rham 1952](https://eudml.org/doc/139052): parallel nondegenerate splittings can force product
  structure under additional Riemannian/global hypotheses.
- [Ambrose and Singer 1953](https://doi.org/10.1090/S0002-9947-1953-0063739-1): curvature transported
  through the connection generates holonomy.
- [Berger 1955](https://doi.org/10.24033/bsmf.1464): irreducible nonsymmetric Riemannian holonomy is
  strongly constrained.
- [Ozeki 1956](https://doi.org/10.1017/S002776300000012X): curvature derivatives enter the
  infinitesimal-holonomy closure.
- [Merkulov and Schwachhofer 1999](https://annals.math.princeton.edu/1999/150-1/p03): irreducible
  torsion-free affine holonomies, including exotic cases, are classified.

## F03 — conformal/projective representative and scale

- [Yamabe 1960](https://doi.org/10.18910/8577), [Trudinger 1968](https://archive.numdam.org/item/ASNSP_1968_3_22_2_265_0/),
  [Aubin 1976](https://zbmath.org/?q=an%3A0336.53033), and
  [Schoen 1984](https://doi.org/10.4310/jdg/1214439291): a chain showing how much structure and global
  analysis a representative-existence theorem can require. None supplies UDT's selector.
- [Fefferman and Graham 1985](https://www.numdam.org/item/AST_1985__S131__95_0/): local conformal
  invariants are organized by weights, jets, dimension, and obstruction order.
- [Ehlers, Pirani, and Schild 1972](https://inspirehep.net/literature/2930504): light and free-fall
  path classes can reconstruct compatible conformal/projective geometry under imported axioms;
  retained only as a question generator.

## F04 — exterior forms, curvature, and topological data

- [Chern 1946](https://doi.org/10.2307/1969037): curvature forms encode characteristic classes on a
  supplied bundle.
- [Atiyah, Hitchin, and Singer 1978](https://doi.org/10.1098/rspa.1978.0143): four-dimensional
  Riemannian Hodge/self-dual structure, with signature and reality caveats for UDT.
- [Whitehead 1947](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.33.5.117): an integral Hopf invariant after the
  global map and normalization exist.
- [Atiyah, Patodi, and Singer 1975](https://doi.org/10.1017/S0305004100049410): a boundary index can
  require nonlocal spectral boundary data; a question generator, not a UDT boundary condition.

## F05 — bundles, cocycles, and gluing

- [Ehresmann 1952](https://www.numdam.org/item/SB_1948-1951__1__153_0/): connection and horizontal
  transport vocabulary on a supplied fiber bundle.
- [Milnor 1956](https://doi.org/10.2307/1969609): universal-bundle classification after a structure
  group and classifying map are supplied.
- [Uhlenbeck 1982](https://doi.org/10.1007/BF01947073): removable singularities under finite-action
  Yang-Mills hypotheses; a strict nonapplicable control for the seal.
- [Corvino 2000](https://doi.org/10.1007/PL00005533): gluing is an operator-and-cokernel problem, not
  merely matching pictures at an edge.

## F06 — boundary variation, generators, and charge

- [Regge and Teitelboim 1974](https://doi.org/10.1016/0003-4916(74)90404-7): differentiable
  Hamiltonian generators depend on surface terms and the phase-space boundary class.
- [Gibbons and Hawking 1977](https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevD.15.2752): an action's normal derivatives
  and held-fixed boundary data determine its boundary completion.
- [Friedrich and Nagy 1999](https://pure.mpg.de/pubman/item/item_152679): a time-live boundary problem
  couples evolution, constraints, gauge, characteristics, and incoming boundary data.
- [Wald and Zoupas 2000](https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevD.61.084027): boundary flux can obstruct a
  Hamiltonian charge and require an integrability prescription.

All four are universal controls; their GR structures are prohibited imports.

## F07 — spectral and inverse-spectral geometry

- [Milnor 1964](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.51.4.542),
  [Sunada 1985](https://annals.math.princeton.edu/1985/121-1/p04), and
  [Gordon, Webb, and Wolpert 1992](https://doi.org/10.1090/S0273-0979-1992-00289-6): distinct
  geometries or domains can share spectral data. A UDT eigenvalue cannot select scale or shape
  without a derived operator, boundary class, and degeneracy-breaking datum.

## F08 — inverse variational problem and faithful reduction

- [Douglas 1941](https://doi.org/10.1090/S0002-9947-1941-0004740-5): equations need not be
  Euler-Lagrange equations.
- [Anderson and Duchamp 1980](https://doi.org/10.2307/2374195): local variational forms can face
  global obstructions.
- [Palais 1979](https://doi.org/10.1007/BF01941322): symmetric criticality is conditional, not
  automatic.
- [Fels and Torre 2002](https://doi.org/10.1088/0264-9381/19/4/303): local metric field reductions
  have two independent group-action gates for faithful symmetric variation.

## F09 — fixed points, continuation, and bifurcation

- [Schauder 1930](https://doi.org/10.4064/sm-2-1-171-180): fixed-point existence after a continuous
  self-map and compact convex domain exist.
- [Leray and Schauder 1934](https://doi.org/10.24033/asens.836): nonlinear continuation after an
  equation, compactness, admissible homotopy, and a priori bounds exist.
- [Crandall and Rabinowitz 1971](https://doi.org/10.1016/0022-1236(71)90015-2): local branch structure
  after a nonlinear operator, Fredholm derivative, simple kernel, and transversality exist.

These results test bootstrap machinery; they do not create it.

## F10 — evolution, constraints, and analysis flows

- [Foures-Bruhat 1952](https://doi.org/10.1007/BF02392131): local hyperbolic development requires a
  specified PDE reduction, constraints, gauge, and data.
- [Eells and Sampson 1964](https://doi.org/10.2307/2373037): a supplied energy and curvature regime
  make harmonic-map heat flow an analysis tool.
- [Hamilton 1982](https://doi.org/10.4310/jdg/1214436922): Ricci flow is a chosen curvature evolution,
  not a consequence of having a metric.
- [DeTurck 1983](https://doi.org/10.4310/jdg/1214509286): solver/gauge modification must be separated
  from geometric content.

## F11 — Hopf topology and soliton controls

- [Hopf 1931](https://doi.org/10.1007/BF01457962): the global map `S3 -> S2` carries integer homotopy
  data.
- [Derrick 1964](https://doi.org/10.1063/1.1704233): scaling obstruction for a supplied energy class.
- [Vakulenko and Kapitanskii 1979](https://www.mathnet.ru/eng/dan42751): topological energy bound for
  a supplied `S2` sigma-model functional.
- [Faddeev and Niemi 1997](https://doi.org/10.1038/387058a0): numerical knot-like solutions in a
  supplied model.
- [Lin and Yang 2004](https://doi.org/10.1007/s00220-004-1110-y): minimizer existence in stated Hopf
  sectors for the exact Faddeev functional.

These control conditional UDT Hopfion claims. None derives UDT's carrier or matter action.
