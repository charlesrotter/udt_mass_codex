# Lay Decision Tree

We gave the conditional metric four ways to change at once: its clock rate, radial shape, angular
squashing, and time-to-angle twist. We then nudged those functions in 198 predetermined ways,
including asymmetric and two different seal-parity sectors.

The outcomes divide cleanly:

1. **147 attempts found a genuine numerical stationary point.** Every one returned to the same
   round, untwisted metric after coordinate and common-scale gauge were removed. Rechecking with
   twice the quadrature and two additional shape modes did not dislodge them.
2. **51 attempts did not find a stationary point.** Almost all began with the larger nudge. They did
   not converge, but that does not mean no solution is there. It means the present Newton route
   cannot see through that region.
3. **The three parity sectors produced no different certified metric.** This also cannot decide
   between coframe sign choices that leave the metric unchanged; a metric-only law has no observable
   with which to tell those copies apart.

So we learned that the round conditional geometry has a wide local basin when all four retained
metric functions are allowed to react together. We did **not** learn that it is the only geometry.
The honest next move is to trace the unresolved directions gradually, rather than throw more random
seeds or declare the failures empty.
