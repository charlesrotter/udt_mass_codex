This is a complete atlas of the declared registry. It is not a global classification theorem for

## Stage 1 — continuous projector transport

For a smooth complete family of mutually annihilating, metric-self-adjoint projectors `P_a` and a
curve tangent `T`, derive and test the metric-compatible Kato/Grassmann transport

```text
D_T P_a = nabla_T P_a,
K = sum_a (D_T P_a) P_a,
nabla_T U = K U.
```

The proof must establish `K` is metric-skew and `[K,P_a]=D_T P_a`, so that
`P_a(s) U(s)=U(s) P_a(0)` and `U(s)` is an isometry between endpoint tangent metrics. The curve is a
numerical probe through configuration space, not physical time or a worldline.

Fine transport stops at rank/signature change or projector degeneracy. Coarse transport may survive
only when an explicitly verified coarsening exists. Scalar and full-irreducible motifs transport the
whole tangent bundle trivially but supply no distinguished proper subbundle.

All 95,232 frozen path summaries are retained. Dense numerical transport at 33, 65, and 129 nodes
uses the lowest SHA-256 key `sha256(identity_id + "\t" + family_id)` in every populated
`(motif,family_id)` stable stratum. No anchor may be changed after seeing a residual. Required
diagnostics are projector intertwining, endpoint metric isometry, ODE refinement, and exact
reciprocal-toric control.

Numerical controls are `FREE / NUMERICAL ONLY`: centered derivative step `1e-5`, RK4 grids
`33/65/129`, transport pass threshold `2e-7`, refinement threshold `5e-6`. Failures and near margins
are retained rather than removed.

## Stage 4 — bundle and holonomy data

Keep four different notions separate:

1. projector/Kato transport along the metric path;
2. Levi-Civita tangent holonomy;
3. integral torus-lattice monodromy/cap-cycle data; and

an explicit endpoint gluing. Local projector matching may never manufacture that gluing. Finite
endpoints retain boundary dependence. `Q=1` may appear only as one conditional row carrying every
period, action, cap, orientation, and normalization premise.
