# Lay report — what the global assembly map found

We now know how the local geometric “instruments” can be carried continuously from place to place.
The transport preserves both the geometric directions and the metric used to measure them. This is
real mathematical progress: the motifs are no longer merely unrelated snapshots.

One orderly family also survived a stronger test. Every candidate that looked like two clean
complementary surfaces at the midpoint still looked that way at all nine sampled positions.

But its provenance matters. All those candidates came from the test in which only `phi` was playing.
The metric’s base, angular, and shift sections were silent. The 1,536 rows are eight mathematical
descriptions of 192 underlying `phi`-only configurations. So we found a coherent `phi` foliation,
not yet the full orchestra organizing itself into angular circles.

We then catalogued the ways a finite geometry could close globally. It can retain physical
boundaries, close one circle, close the same circle at both ends, close opposite circles, form lens
spaces, glue into periodic torus bundles, use different mirror actions, become nonorientable, retain
an anholonomic twisting plane, or pass through points where the fine projector pattern changes.
The familiar `S3`/Hopf completion is one exact conditional member of that much larger territory.

The current UDT rules constrain this atlas but do not choose one member. Reciprocity controls the
opposing dilation pattern. CSN removes common scale. The finite-cell principle removes spatial
infinity. The seal constrains `phi` in its static setting. Bootstrap asks a complete universe to be
self-consistent. None currently says which circles exist, how they close, which quotient to take, or
what action governs a deformable quotient.

That is why we did not proceed to a time-dependent matter solve. Doing so would require us to choose
the missing global structure and dynamics ourselves.

The density/bootstrap idea has a sensible future form. Density should not be guessed first. A
complete UDT solution would produce its own mass and proper volume; their ratio would then feed back
into the same global consistency condition. That could resolve the chicken-and-egg problem as a
simultaneous fixed point. At present we can describe the possible volumes and boundaries, but UDT's
native mass numerator is still missing.

The honest picture is therefore:

- continuous metric-motif transport: derived and numerically mapped, with seven margins retained;
- coherent cell-wide sampled foliation: observed, but it is `phi`-only;
- global completion and holonomy territory: catalogued for the registered families;
- one globally selected quotient: not found;
- carrier/action emergence and physical time evolution: not yet activated;
- density as a future simultaneous bootstrap output: promising architecture, still open.

The key clue is now sharper. The obstacle is not simply carrying local structure across the cell.
The obstacle is getting the full metric orchestra—not the `phi` solo—to organize into a globally
closed quotient under a law UDT itself supplies.
