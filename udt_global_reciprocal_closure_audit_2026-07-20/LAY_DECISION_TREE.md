# Lay decision tree

## What did the armchair audit find?

UDT already has several strong pieces of one picture:

- clocks and rulers change reciprocally;
- an overall local enlargement is initially only a choice of calibration;
- the universe is treated as finite rather than extending to spatial infinity;
- one static mirror surface has a known `phi` condition;
- light cones belong to the conformal metric;
- the realized whole universe must pass a global self-consistency test.

The missing piece is not obviously another force or another term in an equation. It is the exact rule
that says how those pieces make **one complete geometric object**.

## Decision tree

```text
Do the local reciprocal clock/ruler formulas already tell us the whole universe's shape?
|
+-- No. They do not fix the angular closure, topology, or complete boundary.
|
Does the known phi=0 mirror condition fix the rest of the boundary?
|
+-- No. It fixes one number-like direction, not every direction or every legal variation.
|
Does co-presence fill the gap?
|
+-- No. It tells us how to interpret all events as belonging to one solution;
|   it does not add an equation.
|
Does bootstrap fill the gap?
|
+-- Not yet. Bootstrap cannot choose among complete universes until UDT defines
|   exactly what counts as a complete universe and a legal deformation of one.
|
What is the best next target?
|
+-- Try to derive a global completion rule directly from the metric:
    how the reciprocal frame glues through the finite-cell mirror in every direction,
    including the angular directions and the boundary variations.
```

## The plainest analogy

We have the local weave of the fabric and one instruction for folding an edge. We do not yet have the
sewing pattern that says how every edge and corner joins into one garment. Trying more local equations
before knowing the sewing pattern can give false answers, because it quietly holds part of the fabric
still when that part should have been allowed to move.

The attractive possibility is that the sewing pattern is already implicit in the metric's reciprocal
structure. That is worth pursuing. This audit did not find the pattern already written in the current
rules, and it did not prove that a new postulate is needed.

## If the global completion rule is derived

Then we would know the honest space of possible whole UDT geometries and their legal variations. Only
then could bootstrap be tested as the selector of one physical representative, and only after that
could the `C2`, EH, or two-stage action question be asked without smuggling in a variation domain.

Matter would still remain a separate final layer: the carrier, source, boundary coupling, and
unconditional mass would still have to emerge.
