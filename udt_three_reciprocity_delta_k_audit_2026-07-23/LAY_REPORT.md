# Lay readout

There really are three different reciprocity ideas here, and they are not
duplicates.

- Reciprocal-c gives us the basic two-sided clock/ruler pattern.
- Equal observational frames says that a real mismatch cannot be erased by
  merely changing observers.
- Proposed Xmax reciprocity gives the pattern a possible global direction
  and reversal.

Together they make the open problem much cleaner, but they do not finish
it.

Think of reciprocal-c as drawing a tuning mark on one instrument.
Observer reciprocity guarantees that every observer agrees whether the
angular orchestra is in tune with that mark. Xmax reciprocity may tell us
how the performance is mirrored from one end of the hall to the other.
None of them yet forces the orchestra to tune to exactly that mark.

With the strongest favorable assumptions, all the complicated possible
mistuning shrinks to one number called `lambda`. Two matching ends force
the angular behavior to stay perfectly consistent with that same number,
but they still allow `lambda=2`, `lambda=3`, and so on. They do not select
`lambda=1`, which is the exact reciprocal match.

That is progress: the best-case missing principle is no longer an
unstructured tensor equation. It is one dimensionless relationship
between the reciprocal and angular sectors. But current UDT premises do
not yet derive that relationship, and Xmax's two limits are not currently
derived as two physical seals.
