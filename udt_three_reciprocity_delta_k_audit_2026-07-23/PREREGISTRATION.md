# Preregistration: three-reciprocity `Delta_K` audit

Date: 2026-07-23

## Whole question

Do the three distinct structures currently called reciprocity in UDT,
separately or in exact composition, supply either missing premise in the
conditional angular closure theorem?

The structures are:

1. **Reciprocal-c:** the coequal clock/ruler conversion directions
   `c_E` and `1/c_E`, with their reciprocal positional character;
2. **observer-frame reciprocity:** no preferred macroscopic observational
   frame, implemented as equality of physical law under tensorial changes
   of description rather than equality of numerical components; and
3. **Xmax reciprocity:** the proposed bounded positional composition and
   reversal associated with one global `Xmax`.

The missing premises are:

```text
Delta_K = K_eff_angular - K_rec = 0
```

and a global completion supplying two regular reciprocal mirror seals.

The audit asks whether either statement is actually derived, merely
covariant/admissible, conditional on an unregistered join, or false in the
current premise space.

## Observing, not targeting

The audit will keep the three structures mathematically and
epistemically separate before composing them. It will retain every
covariant angular source compatible with the stated symmetries. It will
not insert `Delta_K=0`, a unit angular normalization, a second seal, a
projective position-field identification, or a chosen universe to obtain a
positive result.

## Success contract

`DERIVES_MISSING_PREMISE` is allowed only if:

1. the result follows from registered premises without inserting an action,
   source, angular normalization, representative, boundary amplitude, or
   projective position-field join;
2. observer covariance contributes an actual necessity rather than only
   preserving the form of an optional equation;
3. Xmax reciprocity supplies actual global metric completion data rather
   than a coordinate/group label or inaccessible limiting endpoints;
4. no nonzero covariant `Delta_K` survives all three structures;
5. the result applies to the complete supplied non-block metric, not only
   an isotropic control; and
6. the regularity and independence of any two seals are derived.

A conditional reduction must expose every added homogeneity, isotropy,
centrality, threading, join, or boundary premise.

## Falsification contract

The claimed derivation fails if any one of the following occurs:

- `Delta_K=0` is invariant under frame changes but is not forced by them;
- a central deformation `Delta_K=mu I` survives reciprocal-c, frame
  covariance, and Xmax reversal;
- an anisotropic covariant deformation survives when no angular isotropy
  premise is registered;
- the strongest charitable composition reduces the source only to
  `K_eff=-lambda^2 I` while leaving `lambda` free;
- two seals, even when granted, select only a relation between initial
  shear and `lambda` rather than `lambda=1`;
- Xmax endpoints are limiting, noncomposable group boundaries rather than
  regular mirror seals;
- a projective position-field or absolute/relative-phi join is required;
- scale anchors `c` and `G` determine dimensions but not the missing
  dimensionless angular normalization; or
- the result depends on a chosen action, source, carrier, or bootstrap
  response map.
