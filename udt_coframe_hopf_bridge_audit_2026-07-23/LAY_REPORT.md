# Lay readout

We found an exact chart-level crosswalk between the metric’s new “gearbox”
and the Hopfion blueprint. It is not yet a physical bridge to the finished
particle.

Inside the complete metric frame are two independent balance dials. One
belongs to the base chart block and one belongs to the angular chart block.
Their physical ownership has not been selected. Each dial
has the same clean property: combining two frame changes adds their dial
settings.

On the previously identified Hopf-friendly angular branch, the angular dial
controls where a direction lies between the north and south poles of a
sphere. The new result is that the coframe gearbox automatically supplies an
exact rule for combining those north–south positions. It also combines the
relative weights of the two angular circles used in the Hopf construction.

That is not a coincidence or a visual resemblance. It is exact algebra.

But a point on a sphere needs two pieces of information:

- how far north or south it is; and
- its direction around the equator.

Chosen chart multiplication supplies the first piece on the aligned branch.
It does not
supply the second—the angular phase. Nor does it tell us why the
Hopf-friendly aligned branch should be selected from the complete angular
possibilities. In fact, an allowed angular shear moves the frame out of that
diagonal branch.

Global Hopf topology also needs the angular circles, their periods, the way
they close at the ends, and an orientation. The pointwise gearbox does not
create those global facts, and its composition rule is not a composition law
for Hopf charge.

So the honest picture is:

```text
complete metric gearbox
    -> exact angular reciprocal dial
    -> exact Hopf north/south coordinate and connection weight
    -> [missing phase, physical frame selection, and global closure]
    -> exact Hopf seed
    -> [missing native deformation space and action]
    -> existing conditional stable Hopfion
```

This is worthwhile progress. It places part of the Hopf geometry directly
inside the metric’s own composition structure. It does not yet explain why
matter must use that geometry or why the stable relaxed Hopfion follows.
