> `Xmax` is one universal positional limit shared by all ordinary
> observational frames, analogous in kinematic role to the shared
> Einsteinian-`c` limit. It may be unmeasured and bootstrap-derived. No
> ordinary observer reaches or exceeds it.

This does not identify `Xmax` with `c`, make it a velocity, determine its
value, or extend ordinary-regime observer mechanics silently to every
extreme or microscopic regime.

angular direction remains separate.

`DERIVES_UNIQUE_POSITION_LAW` is allowed only if:

1. `tanh` follows from registered metric/observer premises without assuming
   first-degree projectivity or importing SR transformations;
2. every slope-matched smooth bounded observer action is excluded for an
   independently derived geometric reason;
3. the selected `X` is identified operationally as coordinate reach,
   proper distance, optical distance, or another exact metric observable;
4. the law acts consistently on the complete supplied coframe and angular
   sector;
5. one asymptotic boundary is sufficient—no second regular seal is silently
   restored; and
6. no recorded metric control supplies a distinct admissible map.

`lambda=1` is derived only if a registered complete-metric invariant or
boundary equation excludes every `lambda != 1` branch without inserting a
unit convention.

Uniqueness or closure fails if any one of the following occurs:

  arbitrary monotone chart;
- an odd slope-matched `H_epsilon != tanh` survives all invariant-bound,
  group, reversal, and local-calibration conditions;
- the complete coframe depends on `dphi` and is unchanged by exact
  reparametrization of the bounded display;

  same clock asymptote;
- coordinate reach, proper reach, and optical depth disagree;

  markings;

  control; or
