# What the reciprocal-c and observer-frame audit found

## The short version

Charles's correction was significant. The two founding ideas were already doing more work than the
recent investigations were giving them credit for:

1. `c` and `1/c` are the two directions of one reversible clock–distance conversion; and
2. positional dilation changes those two measures reciprocally.

Together with the already registered rule that relative comparisons compose smoothly, those ideas
give one clock factor that shrinks exactly as its paired distance factor grows. The familiar
exponentials follow. With the declared local interval readout, this is precisely the reciprocal
clock–distance part of the UDT metric.

So the core reciprocal block was not waiting for the metric to rediscover a preferred plane. We had
partly mistaken a relational measurement rule for a demand that the laws paint one permanent
two-dimensional plane onto every spacetime.

## What “all observational frames are reciprocal” means

It does **not** mean every observer must write the same diagonal list of metric numbers. Moving or
accelerating observers can use different component lists while describing the same geometry. That
is ordinary geometric covariance: the measuring grid changes, and the components change with it,
but the underlying interval does not.

The exact test showed that forcing every moving observer to retain the identical nonzero diagonal
dilation factors would eliminate the dilation entirely. Only the trivial zero-dilation case can
stay numerically diagonal and unchanged under every boost.

The consistent interpretation is therefore:

> no observer is privileged by the macro laws, but a particular universe or local solution may
> contain a clock direction, radial direction, gradient, or matter rest frame that transforms as
> geometry.

That is compatible with Charles's GR-like reciprocity of observational frames. It imports no GR
field equation, action, equivalence principle, or claim about the extreme-`phi` observer group.

## What is actually closed

The reciprocal clock–distance block is closed, with its premise stamps. The recorded static,
spherical UDT metric is also recovered exactly once its declared temporal/radial slots, local
Lorentzian interval, transverse completion, and spherical areal coordinates are included.

This does not make those extra ingredients consequences of the number `c`. It means the original
short derivation was sound in its declared static/spherical scope.

## What is still open

The open question is smaller than “derive the metric again.” It is:

> Away from the declared static, spherical setting, how does the reciprocal clock–distance relation
> assemble with all three spatial directions, the angular geometry, and possible shift terms into
> one nonspherical or time-dependent four-metric?

Choosing an arbitrary `x` direction or `y` direction produces different four-metrics when dilation
is nonzero. Spreading the reciprocal weight equally over all three spatial directions is not forced
either; there are multiple volume-preserving ways to distribute it. A full dynamic or micro theory
therefore still needs a metric-native assembly rule. This is not a missing reason for reciprocal
`c`, and it is not evidence for a preferred observer.

## What this corrects

The previous universal-subbundle audit remains exact mathematics, but its leading question was too
strong. It correctly showed that the registered complete geometry does not select one permanent,
law-fixed rank-two plane. The current audit shows that UDT's relational measurement meaning does
not require such a plane in the first place.

The broad ten-amplitude and ensemble atlases also remain valid maps of geometric configurations.
They should not be mistaken for the physically derived UDT metric family until the general assembly
law is known.

Nothing here closes the action, source, carrier, mass, boundary charge, `X_max`, or scale. It does
restore the right deductive order: begin with the founded reciprocal measurement relation and extend
it covariantly, rather than asking unconstrained metrics to rediscover it.
