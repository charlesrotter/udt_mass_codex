## Lay summary

The metric knows **several different radial lengths**. The finite-reach story knows a **compositional** distance \(x\). Gluing them is not automatic.

| Length | Everyday meaning |
|--------|------------------|
| **Areal \(r\)** | How big the sphere looks (area \(4\pi r^2\)); what full light uses as \(D_A\) |
| **Proper \(\ell\)** | What stacked static rulers measure |
| **Compositional \(x\)** | The “how far in the position-composition group” coordinate of the reach law |

**J1** says: treat compositional distance as the same number as areal radius (\(r=x\)).

light still uses **areal** \(r\).  
You only get **\(M=X/2\)** and the simple \(d_L(z)\) cascade when you identify charts.

So the skeleton “reach = critical fill with \(M=X/2\)” is really:

> **finite reach + reciprocal Einstein continuum + chart join J1.**

Honest. Still elegant. Not forced in one step from the metric line element alone.
