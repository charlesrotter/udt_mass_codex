# Lay report — what the orchestra map found

We divided the metric's controls into four groups:

1. the part describing the basic time/depth sheet;
2. the part describing the angular screen;
3. the shifts that tie the basic sheet to the angular screen; and
4. phi, which is still represented as a separate field.

Then we played every possible combination—silence, each group alone, every pair, every trio, and
all four—using 48 different chords in eight local settings. We did not judge whether anything
looked like matter, gravity, or our universe.

The result gives the orchestra metaphor a precise, limited bookkeeping meaning.

At the simplest bookkeeping level, the three geometric groups add independently. But when those
parts are assembled into the metric, the angular screen and the shifts leave a nonzero pair
remainder in this Boolean accounting. When curvature is calculated, every pair of geometric groups
has such a remainder. All three groups also have a smaller three-way remainder in seven of the eight
sampled settings.

In the orchestra metaphor, the arithmetic therefore needs duet and three-section entries as well as
the solos. But this can happen automatically because curvature is nonlinear. It does not yet show
that the sections exchange energy, dynamically influence one another, or form a physical mechanism.

There are two important restraints:

- This tells us how one chosen metric chart and partition account for their parts; it does not yet tell us the law or
  score governing their motion.
- Phi remained completely separate because the current test deliberately inherited phi as an
  independent control. That is confirmation of the test construction, not proof that physical phi
  is unrelated to the metric.

The next useful question is whether these pair and trio bookkeeping relationships survive reasonable changes
in how we describe or factor the coframe. If they survive, they become much stronger clues about
which features belong to the geometry rather than the bookkeeping. If they move or disappear, they
were properties of this particular arrangement.
