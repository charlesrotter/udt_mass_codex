# P05 in lay language

We took the two possible rulebooks Charles had authorized for examination and asked a very strict
question: **if we temporarily accept each rulebook's assumptions, what complete metric equation does
it actually produce before we simplify the shape of the universe?**

Both answers are clean:

- The scale-free route produces the Bach equation, a fourth-derivative geometry equation.
- The already-scaled route produces the Einstein equation with an unfixed cosmological term, a
  second-derivative geometry equation.

That does not mean UDT has chosen either one. It means we now know exactly what each conditional door
contains.

The valuable finding is where both doors stop. A universe is a finite cell, so its edge matters. The
bulk equations naturally throw terms onto that edge. UDT has not yet told us which edge data may
vary, which must be fixed, what edge action cancels or governs those terms, or how corners are joined.
Nor do these metric-only equations govern a separately existing `phi`, frame, projector, connection,
or matter carrier.

We also proved why solving a highly simplified metric too early can fool us: the simplified equation
can say “stationary” while some discarded direction of the full metric still says “move.” One
spherical EH reduction even collapses to an edge term although the full Einstein equations remain
nontrivial.

So P05 did not finish the UDT action. It replaced an ambiguous fork with two exact conditional bulk
operators and exposed the same sharp missing object in both: the native finite-cell variation rule,
including its boundary data and its treatment of any extra fields.
